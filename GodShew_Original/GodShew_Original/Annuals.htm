But now, after that ye have known God, or rather are known
of God, how turn ye again to the weak and beggarly elements, whereunto ye desire again to be in bondage? Ye observe days, and months, and times, and years. ... non eternal things ...Eg:

Christmas: is not the nicest time of year, nor the birth time of Jesus.
Easter is Problem-Attic: "It is finished" brings forth death, not life.

Thanksgiving Day USA: it's become Macy's Day in the good old USA.
Observing non eternal things like annual holydays around the world (rooted in laws and traditions) is as if being bewitched. After we're saved (delivered from law) our focus should be on eternal things. So Apostle Paul admonishes churches for observing plural days, and months, and times, and years he calls weak and beggarly elements. For if we compromise on little things, like observing natural birthdays, counting our age in years, observing annual holydays, it's as if we've compromised on returning to law bondage, like a dog turned to his vomit, a washed sow to her mire, a fool to his folly.
