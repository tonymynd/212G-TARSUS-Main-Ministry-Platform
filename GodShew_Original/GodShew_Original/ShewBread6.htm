Our Father: Hallowed Be THY NAME: GRACE
GRACE unto you, and peace,
from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ

I asked my religious mom what the hallowed name of our Father which art in heaven was. She replied God. So I asked her what the name of Jesus Christ was. She replied Jesus Christ. So I said, with the patience of Job, what is the "name" of Jesus Christ? She said Jesus! I said, with more emphasis: what is the name " of " Jesus Christ, the sur name of Jesus? She said Christ. I said, with even more emphasis: what is the NAME "OF" Jesus Christ, one that has to be the same heavenly name of Our Father and of Our Lord Jesus Christ if he is the Son of God? She stared blankly at me, as if I was crazy to ask: What is the heavenly family name of God & Son? What is y(our) heavenly name, the name of brethren all? She didn't get it. For three score and ten years she's been taught the name "OF" Jesus is Jesus. I could not get her to see the difference between the "name" Jesus Christ and the "name of" Jesus Christ. I didn't dare ask her what the "name of" someone having the wrong father or mother was: a bastard.

How often have people mindlessly prayed: Our Father...hallowed be thy name, not realizing such a name of Our Father is also "the name of Jesus Christ" and even "the name by which ye are called"? And called: Come, let us reason together... both caller and callee being an us, reasoning together suggesting thinking is allowed, even encouraged, to resolve what heavenly  name applies to us, all, and why such name is to be hallowed, not feared, since one characteristic of such heavenly name is perfect love void of fear. Another characteristic is light void of darkness. Another is blessing void of cursing. Another is liberty void of limitation. Another is freedom void of bondage. Another is truth void of lie. Another is mercy void of sacrifice. Another is life void of death. What one name, above every other name named, would fit all such descriptive characteristics of the manifold grace of that God & Son, who do not impute sin (2Cor 5:19), nor accuse (John 5:45), nor condemn (John 3:17...John 8: 3-11), nor steal, nor kill, nor destroy? Grace. Grace is the "name of" Our Father which art in heaven (higher than the heavens). Hallowed Be THY NAME also refers to who and what you are.

Mike Williams tells a cute story about his childhood, whereby he found a hallowed identity in his family name. While going through the check out at the store with his mom he stole some gum. At home, all seeing mom noticed him chewing the gum, and asked where he'd gotten such delicious gum. Back to the store they went, to pay for the gum since he'd already eaten it. He would have rather gotten a beating than to fess up to the store owner. Gum paid for, on the way home he expected a lecture about how bad he was, and fearful threats from good old Do-teronomy & Don't-eronomy. But rather his mom then explained that he is a Williams, and one thing hallowed about Williams folk is they do not steal; And not because of fear of being caught, nor the penalty if caught, but rather Williams folk get it: understanding, and thereby understand nobody benefits from stealing. By such edification and exhortation concerning his family name, he better understood who/what he is: someone who does not steal. He never stole again, nor had the desire to. He could not even be tempted to steal, because he now knew what sort of man a Williams is: a grace us man, not an awful lawful man.   

Grace people do not steal, nor kill, nor destroy; And not because of any "God forbid" law prohibiting such awful things, nor because of fear of any penalty, but rather because we understand it is not beneficial in the long run to add any law to grace is sufficient. By virtue of our hallowed name: "grace", we are love void of fear, light void of darkness, blessing void of cursing, truth void of lie, mercy void of sacrifice, and life void of death. We do not frustrate (set aside) our hallowed name by adding law to what grace is: "sufficient"(no law req'd).

The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ with you all. Amen.

We are of them that believe: to the saving of the soul

Grace unto you, and peace,
from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ

The first man Adam was made a living soul; the last Adam [was made] a quickening spirit. The latter of twain Adams doesn't require salvation. He is salvation, savior of the first man. For the "name of" the last Adam, the Lord from heaven, Jesus Christ: "grace", means salvation. So the birth of Jesus Christ is also called the birth of salvation . Grace came by Jesus Christ, as the second coming of twain: (i) law via Moses (ii) grace via Jesus Christ (Jn 1:17).

The latter is simply the truth about the first: the law was a lie, and such was not sufficient for eternal life. When the fulness of grace and truth ("that", which is perfect: 1Cor 13:10) is come (appears to be fully understood by you all), then the (counter) part (law/lie) of the twain shew shall be "done away" (1Cor 13; 2Cor 3); For then it's no longer required (Gal 3), since it was only a schoolmaster (witch failed every student) unto to Christ: the end of the law, which is salvation. For where "no law", there no transgression (Rom 4). When "no law", sin not imputed (Rom 5). Love: against such, there is "no law" (Gal 5). Love one another, as I have loved you .

To wit, that God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them, and hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation: grace. 

The very God of peace sanctify you wholly; and [I pray God] your whole spirit and soul and body be preserved blameless unto the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ. Whole sanctification seems to involve spirit, soul, and body being preserved "blameless". Blameless speaks of "no law", for wheresoever law, there is blame: "accusation" (Jn 5:45). Hence by law: God blames man. Man blames God. Man blames woman. Woman blames man. Pakistan blames India. India blames Pakistan. Catholics blame Protestants. Protestants blame Catholics. Children blame parents. Parents blame children. America blames Iraq. Iraq blames America. Take away the law (blame), the fear thereof, the torment thereof. Ah, peace at last, at the coming of our Lord: Jesus Christ: the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ: salvation (from law, the strength of sin, the sting being death). The coming of our Lord Jesus Christ is an allegory for becoming aware what law was, understanding it's done away because of what it was: the root of blame, accusation, war, strife, mis-understanding, dis-ease, and death.

The word of God [is] quick, and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing even to the dividing asunder of soul and spirit . The word, of God: grace (Grace unto you ... from God our Father), divides asunder soul and spirit. Such is allegorically the dividing of twain Adams (1Cor 15), into two one man scenarios (Rom 5), and comparing such, in the fashion of a before/after shew, to see which is witch, and which one you are, the script u are (Gal 4), by virtue of the grace of the God of all grace being salvation (1Pet 5).

Now the just shall live by faith: but if [any man] draw back, my soul shall have no pleasure in him. But we are not of them who draw back unto perdition; but of them that believe to the saving of the soul . The law is not of faith (Gal). And whatsoever is not of faith is sin (Rom). And in him there is no sin. Hence in him there is no law, of sin and death. And here, in Hebrews 10, we see Paul comparing twain thems: (i) them who draw back unto perdition (law) and (ii) them who believe to the saving of the soul (grace). And such is as comparing two Gods, or two God conclusions about them: (i) God concluded them all in unbelief: law, (ii) that he might have mercy upon all: grace. To wit, that God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them: grace.

In a shew, whereby all the worlds a stage, there are twain thems, also them and us, mirrorly for comparison, but in reality there is just us, and the Lord is l-o-n-g-suffering to us-ward.
 
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ with you all. Amen.
