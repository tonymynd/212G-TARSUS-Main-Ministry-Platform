Two Parts: Their Part vs Your Part

Two parts occur throughout the Bible in many allegorical forms of before/after, such as them/us, they/we, ye/you, etc; But the most noticeable mention of two parts is in 1Peter 4:14, which speaks of their part and your part:

"If ye be reproached for the name of Christ, happy [are ye]; for the spirit of glory and of God resteth upon you:
on their part he is evil spoken of, but on your part he is glorified".
(note: but always notes a comparison and contrast of law vs grace is being made)

At first one might assume such two parts are the same as corruptible/incorruptible seeds and crowns, or the same as changeable/unchangeable priesthoods; But what's incorruptible and unchangeable is not one of twain, but above twain; Neither of two parts, but rather whole. Paul says it this way in 1Corinthians 13, along with mention of child/man: when that which is perfect is come, then that which is in part is done away. Such does not leave one part of two parts, but one whole that is neither of two parts, not part-iality but perfect-ion. So neither of their part or your part are whole, but rather only part. So to become whole, after playing your part of two parts, put away part-iality. James 2 says it this way: have not the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ [the Lord] of glory with respect of persons. James 3:17 also notes first pure wisdom from above (grace unto you from God) has no partiality as one of seven characteristics of grace.

Two Sons Jesus plays two parts in the new of old/new testaments: Son of Man and Son of God on high; And neither are Christ: true Son of God in heaven: higher than the heavens. For Christ is the end of the law: Romans 10:4, and not the mend of the law: Luke 5:36-38. So it's clarified peace with God and eternal salvation are "through Jesus --> Christ", in Christ thereof. And "to wit": reconciling the world by and unto that God is also done "in Christ", not in Jesus who brought division and not peace: Mt 10:34; Lk 12:51, and thereby Jesus did not give them rest(peace): Hebrews 4:8. So rather than wrongly divide the word of truth to end approved unto the wrong God and thereby be ashamed, let us rightly divide the word of truth to end up a workman both approved unto God and unashamed, which speaks of leaving the principles (two parts) and going on unto perfection, which is whole, like the God of all grace (no law at all), who is merciful (no sacrifice at all) and peaceful (no division at all). For God is not [the author] of confusion, but of peace.

The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ with you all. Amen.
