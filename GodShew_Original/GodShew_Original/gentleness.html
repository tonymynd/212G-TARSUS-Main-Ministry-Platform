Gentleness

 love joy peace longsuffering GENTLENESS goodness faith meekness temperance

 

I became curious about this Spirit  prompting, began pondering things about MIDST…

 

My pondering re Midst first reminded me of what’s in midst of Genesis 1:1, which is a Menorah Seven in the Hebrew language, read left←right, reverse of English. Midst of Seven is 1 thing called: eht being 2 things: alaph/tav re a PluralGod called ‘elohyim:

haeretz – vaeht – hashamayim – eht – Elohim – barah – Bereshit 

[earth] - [and] - [heavens] - [aleph/tav] - [God] - [created] - [In the beginning]

Midst can be LukeWarm of cold←LukeWarm→hot.

 

So, perhaps this Gentleness being the midst of 9 has some significance we may get enlightened about.  Let’s study it; then also discuss it:

 

There’s only two mentions of “gentleness” in NT. Such are different in Greek:

-        Gentleness1: 2Cor 10:1 “Now I Paul myself beseech you by the meekness and gentleness (‘epieikeia’) of Christ, who in presence [am] base among you, but being absent am bold toward you: …”

-        Gentleness2: Gal 5:22 “But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness (‘chrestotes’),goodness, faith, …” 

 

So, in English both are gentleness, but in Greek as different as ‘epieikeia’ & ‘chrestotes’. The ‘epieikeia’ gentleness, of Christ, is first rendered as requested “clemency”, by Jewish orator Tertullus, from Roman governor Felix, in Acts 24:4, where Paul is on trial, and the governor Felix is referred to as a Judge who’s hearing both sides of this trial matter. So it’s kinda like Paul is first beseeching Corinthians using this “gentleness” aspect.

 

Now, also looking at ‘chrestotes’ kind of “gentleness”, it’s also rendered as “goodness”, “kindness”, “good”. Yet it’s notable this “goodness” aspect of this “gentleness” = 5th fruit aspect notably differs from the 6th fruit aspect = “goodness” = ‘agathosyne’ = uprightness of heart and life; Which is yet to be discussed as next chapter. So perhaps Greek renderings, as if inside of and more than English renderings, hereby becomes as if first comparing 2 kinds of “gentleness”, then also as if comparing 2 kinds of “goodness”:

- gentleness1: 2Cor 10: of Christ, was 1st rendered as clemency requested of governor.

- gentleness2: Gal 5: also rendered as goodness, is still only 1st of 2 kinds of “goodness”.

 

Gentleness2 (midst) = Goodness, but ‘chrestotes’ goodness = 5th is not goodness = 6th.

-        goodness1: ‘chrestotes’ = 5th aspect, now being looked at, as if the midst

-        goodness2: ‘agathosune’ = 6th aspect, yet to be looked at, as beyond midst

 

love joy peace longsuffering gentleness2=goodness1 goodness2 faith meekness temperance

 

Oh boy, now seems as if it’s getting more complicated, rather than simpler, and more like a mystery having lots of twists, rather than a comfy notion of gentleness just being “gentle”; Yet not like gentle1 (‘epios’ = affable) in 1Thess 2:7 & 2Tim2:24, but another gentle2 (‘epieikes’ = patiently equitable) in Titus  3:2 (to all men) & in James 3:17 (as 2nd of 7 apsects of wisdom from above). Oh shit, it’s like we’ve got twain of everything to sort out: twain gentleness x2, twain goodness x2, twain gentle x2, to learn something.

 

Perhaps it’s why we need to be led of the Spirit, unto spiritual aspects of fruit, rather than just use our natural (carnal, flesh) thinking about such things. For even when spiritual, of natural/spiritual=after, there’s still “comparing spiritual things with spiritual”, after the after. When spirit mode of soul/spirit, there’s still “spirit of error” & “spirit of truth” to contrast. Order of twain important, for 1John 4:6’s contrast order of truth←&→error ends error; Like Jesus’ and/or Mt 12:30-37 twains all end as bad as saved←or→damned.

 

So what can we learn about this gentleness2, of twain, being as if goodness1, of twain?

 

Goodness1: as the ‘chrestotes’ of Gentleness2, in midst of 9 fruit:

-        Romans 2:4’s goodness (‘chrestotes’: goodness1 of gentleness2), of goodness, forbearance, longsuffering of God, notes goodness of God leadeth to repentance. It’s said to ‘anthropos’ man (“liar” in Rom 3:4). It’s about how such a man who judges others (eg homosexuals & lesbians) is also as “inexcusable” as them who are “without excuse”. Judging them is not how to escape the judgment of a law God who has no accuse-others and excuse-me partiality. So repentance is needed.

-         Romans 11:22’s goodness  (‘chrestotes’: goodness1 of gentleness2) is goodness of a God who spared not; So says behold (both) goodness and severity of (this God), who’s goodness toward thee is conditional upon thee continuing in such goodness, lest also be not spared, described as cut off=hewn down.

 

Such goodness of a God who “spared not” is not [yet] your Father is “merciful”. OMG.

 

So, perhaps it’s why we should ponder about this gentleness (goodness) being the midst of 9. For it seems to be about a Plural God having both goodness←&→severity, even potential severity after goodness; Kinda like Mt 18:23-35’s forgiven←&→unforgiven. It’s like the order is reversed. If both, should be severity first, goodness after. It’s beginning to seem like Law, being both good←&→evil, ends with evil: reverse of live.

 

Seems we’re looking at gentleness that’s also something other than gentle, thereby goodness that’s something other than good. Perhaps if we now also look at this gentleness2 (chrestotes) = goodness1 (chrestotes) as kindness (chrestotes), we`ll learn more.

 

Kindness (chrestotes) x4: as gentleness2`s goodness1 (how weird is this?)

1- 2Cor 6:6’s kindness is listed among things like ‘gnosis’ knowledge (not epignosis) on one side and Holy Ghost (not Spirit) on other side, midst sandwiched between pureness→kindness←love unfeigned; So it may not be either, yet as if in midst of both; Kinda like CJ a Mr In-Between as a mediator between God←&→men in 1Timothy 2:5.

2Cor 6:6 “By pureness, by knowledge, by longsuffering, by kindness, by the Holy Ghost, by love unfeigned,”

2- Eph 2:7’s kindness is equally as strange, speaking of such kindness toward us (reverse of them), to be shewn in ages to come through Christ Jesus (reverse of Jesus Christ)… 2:10 then saying we (not them) got created (not made) in Christ Jesus (them), which this God BEFORE ordained that we should walk in them (not yet us). How weird is this? …

 

For even Plural God in Gen 1 never said let us create(bara) man, but let us make(asah) man; Yet then created man in his image (reverse of likeness)… as if first play out a  reverse scenario: “in our image” as if reverse before of before/after, after being “after our likeness”. Perhaps our likeness should be Jesus Christ, not Christ Jesus. Seems why Paul turns CJ around to JC in Hebrews. For JC is NT foundation to build upon: grace & truth. CJ as if reverse: law & lie. So in 1Pet 5:10’s before/after, CJ is before, as if created of created/made, thereby imperfect. After made perfect is by the God of all grace.

 

3- Col 3:12’s kindness is low on a list of upgrades, only 2nd of 7 topped off with charity. Previously 3:10 noted putting on the new man, of old/new, is as being renewed in the ‘epignosis’ knowledge, to be changed from the former corrupt state of the old man. Col 3:12 also mentions the elect, and false Christs arise to seduce even the elect if possible. So the upgrades listed are to take the elect above the potential of being seduced on high. So it’s kinda like getting above(epi: higher) the above(ano: high), as if it’s after the after: After spirit of soul/spirit, also try the spirits: compare spirit of error & spirit of truth, and after spiritual of natural/spiritual, also compare spiritual things with spiritual. For law is both natural & spiritual, but when spiritual it can be spiritual wickedness in high places.

 

So Paul’s writings say:

Be not highminded←mind not high things→be not highminded

 

Mind not high things would include a Majesty on high, old/new high priests thereof.

CJ is portrayed as new high priest in Heb 3, mediating BetterLaw, is NotYet Grace.

So in Hebrews, Paul turns CJ around to JC to end the epistle with Grace. Interesting.

 

4- Titus 3:4’s kindness and love(philanthropia) toward man(philanthropia) is mentioned as an after scenario, yet seems to refer to them, of them/us, and via CJ, of CJ/JC.

 

This is quite an interesting and somewhat complicated study of gentleness in midst of 9.

 

There’s yet a 4th rendering of this gentleness=goodness=kindness=good in Rom 3:12, where it says of “they”: “They are all gone out of the way, they are together become unprofitable, there is none that doeth good(chrestotes), no, not one”. However the context in prior verses seems to suggest a “they”: are “together” unprofitable includes both Jews & Gentiles, as if both are “they”; neither we of they/we, nor us of them/us.
