## Content

Merciful? of 4 different NT kinds, your Father = oiktirmōn. Why?
- eleēmōn: Matthew 5:7; Hebrews 2:17(be merciful)
- oiktirmōn: Luke 6:36; James 5:11(of tender mercy)
- hilaskomai: Luke 18:13; Hebrews 2:17(make reconciliation)
- hileōs: Matthew 16:22(be it far); Hebrews 8:12 = will I = (?)
Discussion: 
-- seems there's law mercy for them←&→grace mercy for us.
-- law mercy can also be unmerciful; grace is never unmerciful.
-- eg: like law forgiven can also be unforgiven + tormented after.
-- Allegory for the perfect will of God is:
I will have mercy, and not sacrifice = I will have grace, not law.
Seems then, only the oiktirmōn kind of merciful fits that criteria.
