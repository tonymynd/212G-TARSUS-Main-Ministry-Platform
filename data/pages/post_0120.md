## Content

Bible Mystery: Galatians 5 clarifies being ``led of the Spirit`` as
``But if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law``.
(Romans 6 contrasted not under the law as being under grace).
Gal 5 seems to allegory contrast Law = driven vs Grace = led,
seem as different as ``Walk before me`` vs ``Follow me``. 
Law 
driven seems to fulfill the works of the flesh, But
if Grace led ``ye shall not fulfill the lust of the flesh``;
``And these are CONTRARY the one to the other``.
So then it`s reasoning law is contrary to grace,
and if grace led then ye shall not fulfill the law.
Note: ``Love thy neighbour as thyself`` = law, not grace.
In Mt 22:36-40 it`s called ``the second`` of law1 law2,
where it also clarifies ``the second is like the first``.
In Rom 7 it`s called ``another law``, where it`s revealed
law2 can bring you into ``captivity`` again to the law of sin,
which then did all manner of evil ``concupiscence`` on Paul.
The result of such law concupiscence, followed by a guilt trip,
made Paul feel as ``wretched`` & suicidal as if having PTSD.
Gal 5 mentions ``love thy neighbour`` as fulfilling the law,
which is called ``fulfill the lusts of the flesh``, if under law.
So if led of the Spirit we should stop trying to fulfill any law;
For it clarifies ``if led of the Spirit ye are not under the law``.
So after listing many ``works of the flesh``,
one such notably being (law worketh) ``wrath``,
(which is what to ``put away`` in Eph 4:31),
Paul leads us thru the nine fruit of the Spirit:
- love
- joy
- peace
- longsuffering
- gentleness: meekness with fairness: midst
- goodness
- faith
- meekness: opposite of rod of iron rule of law
- temperance = self-control
Then it mentions ``against such  there is no law``,
``And they that are Christ's have crucified the flesh``. 
Notes: 
gentleness & meekness speak of ``Christ``: 2Cor 10:1,
and ``Christ is the end of the law``: Romans 10:4; So
``if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law``. 
Interesting stuff.
