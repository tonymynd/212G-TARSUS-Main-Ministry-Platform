Discipline or Temperance? 
Three best wishes to ALL: grace, mercy and peace, from God our father and Jesus Christ our Lord. 
Now, is interesting how "discipline" is OT word or a law word, as they use it for "disciplinary actions" for not complying with law, as Jesus Christ was sacrificed under Roman law and Sanhedrin law, this why, the will of God is "I will have mercy and not sacrifice" allegorically means 'I will have grace and not law', since law only offers sacrifice Heb 10:8 AKJV,
So, according to Stoicism, discipline is associated with temperance, being the last one the 9th aspect of the fruit of the Spirit, "against such, there is no law" Gal 5:22:23, so the most adequate usage from a grace perception is "temperance" (self-control) which includes to refrain oneself from the urge to law/judge or criticize others, or trying to be right by making others wrong, to refrain from guilt tripping others or accusing others, as JC said "Do not think that I will accuse you to the Father: there is one that accuseth you, even Moses(law), in whom ye trust." , this is why Christ abolish the enmity "even the law" Eph 2:15, because it was "contrary" to us Col 2:14.
Is temperance (not discipline) which helps us to reach the end:
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ  [is] with you all. Amen.
