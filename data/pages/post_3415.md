## Content

DUAL meanings in Greek for many biblical words. 
WHY?
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
In 
biblical "mystery" there's not only "allegory" meanings such as two sons of Abraham by two mothers are two covenants, and Agar is Mount Sinai (Galatians 4); But many dual meanings in Greek. 
Examples:
For "word" of God, 2 kinds:
- the "word" (rhema) of God: Ephesians 6:17
- the "word" (logos) of God: Hebrews 4:12
For "converted", 2 kinds:
- "converted" (strepho): Matthew 18:3
- "converted" (epistrepho): Luke 22:32
For "knowledge", 2 kinds:
- "knowledge" (gnosis): 1Corinthians 8:1; 13:8
- "knowledge (epignosis): Eph 4:13; 1Tim 2:4
For "learning", 2 kinds:
- "learning" (didaskalia): Romans 15:4
- "learning" (manthano): 2Timothy 3:7
For "liberty", 2 kinds:
- "liberty" (eleutheria): Galatians 2:4; 5:1
- "liberty" (apolyo): Hebrews 13:23
For "man", 2 kinds
- "man" (anthropos): Romans 2:1; 3:4
- "man" (aner): Romans 4:8; 1Cor 13:11; Eph 4:13
For "goodness", 2 kinds:
- "goodness" (chrestotes): Romans 2:4; 11:22
- "goodness" (agathosyne): Rom 15:14; Gal 5:22; Eph 5:9
For "immortality", 2 kinds:
- "immortality" (aptharsia): Rom 2:7; 2Tim 1:10
- "immortality" (athanasia): 1Cor 15:53,54; 1Tim 6:16
What are your insights about such biblical things?
