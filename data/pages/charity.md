# Pauline Clarity About Why Charity Never Faileth

I - Pauline Clarity of Charity  
  
Pauline Clarity of Charity #1: 1Corinthians 8:1  
  Charity Edifieth up (not puffeth up)  
[](ShewBread5.md#evilc)

[Paul The Apostle](Paul.md), as "his witness unto all men", and writer of more NT writings than all six other NT writers combined, is not only the first biblical writer to mention the word "charity", but is also the major clarifier of charity.  
  
Paul begins his mention and clarity of charity in 1Corinthians 8:1 by noting "charity edifieth". Furthermore he uses the comparative teaching method to compare "charity edifieth" with law knowledge witch "puffeth up". He later clarifies in 1Cor 13:8 [law](Law.md) knowledge "shall vanish". And for assurance it is law knowledge that shall vanish, he clarifies in Heb 8:13 law is so old and faulty that it is "ready to vanish". For in Galatians he clarifies [law had an expiry date](ShewBread9.md#Expired), or "fulness of the time" for the  [imperfect](Hebrews11.md) part of imperfect/perfect (law/grace) parts he allegorically calls child/man; Clarifying in 1Cor 13 that when that which is perfect [is come](GodShew3.md#Second), then the imperfect part is to be "[done away](RevelatorySermons1.md)", just as Paul "put away" [childish things](GodShew.md#nomorechildren) (laws) to become a [man](TwainShew.md#Blesseds) in 1Cor 13:11.  
  
Paul, as a constant clarifier, brings forth much clarity of charity, and further clarifies in 1Cor 14 there are three things that we should speak unto [men](RevelatorySermons6.md):  
(i)  edification - building up from below, up-right from the proper foundation: "[Jesus Christ](ChristJesus.md)"  
(ii)  exhortation - up lifting from above, ever higher, and sometimes with "sharpness"  
(iii) comfort - 'paramuthia' comfort, via purposeful persuasion of clarity, and  
      for clarity it's neither sort of 'parakeleo' comfort in 1Thes 4:18 & 1Thes 5:11  
  
Charity edifieth. Edification is defined as the condition of being spiritually enlightened; but also clarified as building up, growing up, up from the correct foundation; lest spiritual things built end up as nothing more than sounding brass without charity, profit nothing in comparison to what profits everything. Paul clarifies [the only foundation he laid is "Jesus Christ"](GodShew5.md), even though he also speaks about "Christ Jesus" (which is mirrorly the reverse of Jesus Christ). So those building things like love never fails and [rapture theories](RevelatorySermons8.md) upon the wrong foundation may be spiritual builders, even gifted apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors(priests), and teachers; But if what they're building is nothing more than childish law law, unaware such law <= law can lead to a latter end worse, then in the end such profiteth nothing if done without charity.  
  
Paul further clarifies in 2Cor that Lord given authority, to him and to [us](ShewBread2.md#justus), is for edification (building up: grace), and not for [destruction](lawphoto.md) (tearing down: law). For [law](Law.md), even the better sort of [two laws](TwainShew4.md#Laws) in Romans 7 & 8, albeit the right side of a law law sides war, is still partaker of an accusation-all [blame game](RevelatorySermons15.md); Law having been already clarified by JC as the accuser of the brethren in John 5:45. Because law is limited, also a ministration of death, being up right in a sides war only to make others wrong is as being higher on a Mosaic ladder to heaven witch dead ends short of entering.  
  
Edification, the condition of being spiritually enlightened, is also clarified as promoting growth in grace: [babe](Hebrews6.md) > [child](TwainShew2.md#Childrens)> [man](TwainShew.md#Blesseds) > [perfect man](RevelatorySermons17.md). Paul further clarifies in Eph 4, where he also says "be nor more children" (neither sort in Rom 9), we should grow up unto the perfect man, which is the "[fulness of Christ](GodShew2.md#Allness)". Therefore edification is also defined as growth in [Christian wisdom](TwainShew7.md#Wisdoms), affection, grace, virtue, holiness, and blessedness (all bless and no curse at all).
