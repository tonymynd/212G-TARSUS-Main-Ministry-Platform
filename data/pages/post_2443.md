## Content

A MORE EXCELLENT WAY:
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord. 
KJV uses both "love" & "charity" for Greek word 'agape'. Yet for the "more excellent way" (1Cor 12:31) described in 1Cor 13, KJV uses "charity" (not love); Allegory as if charity [agape 2] is somehow more than love [agape 1].
Seems why is:
Jesus noted in Mt 24:12, re love[agape]:
And because iniquity shall abound, the love[agape] of many shall wax cold.
(so 1Cor 13:8 KJV says charity [agape 2] never fails)
Love [agape: noun; agapao: verb] was/is always a "commandment", even when Jesus made it a "new commandment".
1Timothy 1:5 KJV notes:
Now the end of the commandment (to love one another) is:
"charity" [agape 2] out of a pure heart,, and
of a good [agathos: grace] conscience, and
of faith unfeigned.
Interesting allegoric "mystery" details to notice, ponder, discuss.
What are your insights on how/why:
KJV uses both love & charity for agape?
KJV uses both Ghost & Spirit for pneuma?
The grace (only) of our Lord Jesus → Christ be with you all. Amen.
