## Content

Son of man COMING IN THE CLOUDS?
Son of man doesn't occur beyond Acts.
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God, our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
It's 
interesting to notice and consider why there is no more mention of "Son of man" beyond Acts, then only "Son of God", 
And 
Then it's called the gospel (good news) of "Christ", is then noted as being "through" our Lord Jesus → Christ is the end of the law. 
It's allegory like then having gone with Jesus  
through 2 Sons Jesus mode (Mt - Acts), was still both old & new,
to 1 Son Christ mode (Romans → forward), is then all things new.
Seems 
mentions of Son of man in Mt - Acts are both nice and nasty, but lastly nasty, allegory like OT LORD [Yehova] both saved and destroyed afterward (as reminded in Jude 1:5). 
For after healing in cities of disciples, he (as Son of man) did then also upbraid and threaten Woe at a coming day of judgment [krisis]: Mt 11:20-24.
THREATS of 
"coming of the Son of man" (Mt 24:27, 30, 37, 39).
"Son of man cometh" (Mt 24:44, 25:13; Lk 12:40, 18:8).
"Son of man shall come" (Mt 16:27; Mt 25:31).
"Son of man coming in clouds" (Mt 24:30) = "mourn", not rejoice.
day of Son of man revealed = "destroyed them all" (Lk 17:24-30).
note: 
"destroyed them all" by law is contrary to save them all by grace.
(law) voice of Son of man says judgment [krisis] also (Jn 5:27).
(law voice of Son of man (John 5:25-29) wants two resurrections:
resurrection of life AND resurrection of damnation = bad end.
(grace) voice of Son of God (John 5:25) says only "live" (by only grace), and says (John 5:45) do not think I (grace & truth) will accuse you to the Father; Noting Moses (aka law) does that.
So,
in Paul's Gospel: Romans → forward:
Thoughts of threatening, division, judgment[krisis], destruction 
begin to "vanish away" (1Cor 13:8) as we have NO more mention of Son of man, ONLY Son of God in Paul's Gospel of God (Grace & peace to you), and of Christ (is end of the law for righteousness)
as if then 
no more twain-Jesus mentions of being both Son of man and Son of God (aka both old and new), and of both salvation and destruction afterward, as the last state of law's both good and evil being worse: "evil" is reverse of "live" in English.
It's tricky in Mt - Acts,
For Lk 9:56 says Son of man did not come to destroy, but to save.
YET,
Lk 17:26-30 twice notes the day of Son of man is revealed would be as days of Noah & Lot, resulted in destroyed (not saved) them all.
So, Paul's Gospel, by having no mention of Son of man, is allegory as if it cancels the law threat, division, judgement, destruction; By grace and truth mention until thought of destruction is put away, by put away wrath, by put away "law worketh wrath".
Such info is not posted to speak against Jesus when he played both Son of man and Son of God modes of old and new for contrast sake; But to aware what is said about such.
What are your insights about such a delicate topic?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
