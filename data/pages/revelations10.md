Son of man  

The term Son of man occurs aprox 109 times in the Bible, and carries with it no implication of divinity. Rather the phrase is translated in the Hebrew as Ben Adam or in the Aramaic as Bar Enash, which is also loosely translated in at least 12 instances as man in general, or humanity, the first Adamic sort of [two Adams](TwainShew.md#Adams), the created of created/[made](RevelatorySermons17.md) things, [them](ShewBread2.md#inthem) of them/us ([two alls](TwainShew.md#Alls)), the ye do errr of [ye/you](YeYou.md), the [imperfect](Hebrews11.md) of imperfect/perfect parts, [child](GodShew.md#nomorechildren) of child/man, [servant](TwainShew6.md#Servants) of servant/son, old of old/new, first of first/second, and of [two sons](TwainShew6.md#Sons) of Abraham the one to cast out (the script-u-are saith "cast out the bondwoman and her son": Gal 4); allegorically speaking of law of law/grace and lie of lie/truth in such things are an [allegory](Allegory.md).  
  
So "like unto Son of man" in [Revelation 1,2,3](GMP/Notes/GodShew/Revelation.md) speaks of being like unto first Adam, or them (which is Adam in Gen 5:2), which is like unto a vessel fit for destruction; for them of them/us is the counter part in such a two part shew about a third part. And once the point of such is understood, the counter part is to be done away; Not to mention God is one, of us, with us, for us, and hath given [us](ShewBread2.md#justus) the victory [thru](through.md) [JC](GodShew5.md).
