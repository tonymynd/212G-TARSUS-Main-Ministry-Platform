## Content

Some interesting biblical things I noticed, about when in spirit mode of soul/spirit, also "try the spirits", since there's both false/true spirits until we dare compare spiritual things with spiritual, aka plural with one only in the end, to have truth only of false/true things, even of false/true spirits:
- in 1John 4, after saying "try the spirits", his ORDER of "spirit of truth and spirit of error" is whacky: ends with "error", not "truth". So when contrasting, even the 
"order" is important, so it ends well, not only true of false/true, but ONLY true = ONEderful.
- in Greek mode, it's interesting to compare two kinds of "Lamb" in NT scriptures, for 'arnion' Lamb in Revelation is not same as 'amos' Lamb in John 1:29. Who knew this? I didn't until the Spirit prompted me to dare compare:
-- 'amnos' Lamb, of God, taketh away the sin of the world.
-- 'arnion' Lamb, not of God, taketh away peace of the world.
- although false/true Y2Ks did have proper order, ending with true, it still had both, not yet one ONLY, to have truth ONLY. So it seems we've all gone on, beyond Y2Ks, to a third "day" of AD, which should be the end of plural last "days", to be ONEderful: graceful, merciful, peaceful, as if the fulness of Christ, who is the end of the law, not just the mend of the law.
