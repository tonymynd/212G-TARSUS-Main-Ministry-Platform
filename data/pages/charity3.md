Patience does not get easily provoked, but patience does run (not walk) the race to [the end](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md), which is notably [the end of law](Perfection.md); And especially "when seeing we also are compassed about": surrounded, by so great a [cloud](RevelatorySermons8.md) of witnesses, false witnesses who would scape-goat you in a lawed heart beat for love of money, and say your martyr-dumb was for the greater good (the greater law, that is also greater damnation). What then, when seeing this? Lay aside the weight that easily besets, to "run" the race to the end, and run along with patience, which does not get easily provoked, but does run the race to the end.

Pauline Clarity of Charity #12: 1Cor 13: 5  
Charity thinketh no evil.  

  
Charity thinketh no evil. JC said either make the tree good or corrupt (evi) in Matthew 12:33, which is [allegoric](Allegory.md) for either make it grace [or](OneLiners.md#Shakespeare) law, not both; Since both good(grace) + evil(law) is an oxymoron with a [bad ending](LatterEnd.md). He also said "this is an evil generation: they seek after a sign" in Luke 11:29; Which speaks of the law generation of the generations of Adam, not of the generation of Jesus Christ which is a grace generation. And "[law](Law.md) worketh [wrath](lawphoto.md)" is no doubt an [[evil work]], especially when considering "God hath no appointed us unto wrath" is allegoric for God hath not appointed us unto law (worketh wrath) in such comparison and contrast of [them](ShewBread2.md#inthem) and [us](ShewBread2.md#justus) in 1Thes 5:3 and 5:9.  
  
So the biblical [allegory](Allegory.md) for "evil" gets clarified as being "law", especially when considering "evil concupiscence" as being law concupiscence in Romans 7. For Paul clarifies in Rom 7 that "another law" of such Rom 7 & 8 [law law](TwainShew4.md#Laws) only produces "all manner of [concupiscence](Concupiscence.md)", and later clarifies such law <= law is "evil concupiscence", confirming law is evil when comparing grace and law as good and evil; Also lie when comparing grace and law as lie and truth: Jn 1:17. But when the law is called good, then the comparison  is as good/better.  
  
In [Hebrews 5: 13-14](Hebrews6.md) Paul notes many Hebrews are as "babes" not yet weaned of the law, thereby unable to reason both good + evil ends badly. In contrast those of full age, the age of grace, do discern (avoid) both good + evil. Hence it reasons "charity thinketh no evil" is allegorically charity thinketh no law. The reason that charity thinketh no law is no law = no sin imputed (Rom 5:13), and no law = [no sin imputed](Reconciliation.md) = [no dead end](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md) (1Cor 15:56; 2Cor 3:7; Jam 1:15). For law is both the source and the strength of sin, which is notably a deadly "sting", not only a ministration of condemnation but a ministration of death. For law imputes sin; And sin, when it is finished, brings forth death: James 1.  
  
Charity thinketh no evil (law), neither of Rom 7 & 8 law law. For one "such like" compasses land and sea to make one proselyte: [more the child of hell](Allegory3.md). And "another law" makes "all manner of (evi) concupiscence". Not to mention the second of such [law <= law](LawLaw.md) gives place to the first as two beasts of Rev 13. So either way of such a law <=> law merry go round it's a [latter end worse](LatterEnd.md); similar to [mercy seatward](RevelatorySermons27.md) being overshadowed by Cheribum from both ends. So unless we go beyond the narrow way of broad and narrow ways, which notably only "leadeth unto life", but can also be a dead end if such life is in [Christ Jesus (mirrorly the reverse of Jesus Christ)](GodShew5.md#Peace), the life of life + death... Unless we [go beyond](Hebrews7.md) such to a "more excellent way" of "charity never faileth" because charity thinketh no evil(law), and all put away such "childish things" as law law, we'll have a latter end [sevenfold worse](Revelations9.md) instead of better; as is noted in the johnny law revelation of [two revelations](Revelations2.md#Rev4) contrasted by Paul.  
  
  

Pauline Clarity of Charity #13: 1Cor 13: 6  
Charity "rejoiceth not in iniquity, but rejoiceth in the truth"  

  
Charity rejoiceth not in iniquity, but rejoiceth in the truth. Iniquity is of the law; for where no law, there no transgression: Rom 4:15, and when no law, no sin imputed: Rom 5:13. So charity rejoiceth not in law is the allegoric moral.

He also said he knows nothing about the book of Revelation other than this; being only an expert in faith. But the faith he's an expert in is [blind faith](TwainShew2.md#Faiths), and the game he's playing is the childish [blame game](RevelatorySermons15.md) of imputing sin for the [hell](lawphoto.md) of it. At his healing conferences many who go away unhealed are [condemned](Law.md) for not having enough faith. In contrast, Jesus went about healing all who were oppressed of the devil... afflicted by devilish law. Paul clarifies "whatsoever is not of faith is sin": Rom 14:23, and later "the law is not of faith": Gal 3:11.  
  
  

Pauline Clarity of Charity #14: 1Cor 13: 7  
Charity "beareth... believeth... hopeth... endureth: all things"  

  
Charity beareth all things. Charity believeth all things. Charity hopeth all things. Charity endureth all things. So the common denominator of "charity" is "all things", which allegorically speaks of grace void of law; For law is limited, forbidding, thereby limited to not all things: Which "things" are an "[allegory](Allegory.md)". In contrast grace does not forbid; especially concerning the little children. For such Don't-eronomy of such Do-teronomy and Don't-eronomy only produces all manner of (evi) concupiscence; Whereas grace produces understanding. And as Solomon the wise guy exhorts: "with all thy getting, get understanding".
