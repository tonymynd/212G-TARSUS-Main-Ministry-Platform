## Content

Reasoning: God isn't both Law & Grace, but Law or Grace. Why? Both would be a bi-polar oxy-moron: 2 contrary things that can't co-exist in peace; as if confusion, not peace. So Paul reasons "God is not [author] of confusion, but of peace".
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
Although apostle Paul mentions "law of God" (Rom 7:22; 7:25; 8:7) & "wrath of God" (Rom 1:18; Eph 5:6; Col 3:6); He also helps 
reason that because "law" worketh "wrath" (Rom 4:15) it's what to "put off" & "put away" (Eph 4:31 & Col 3:8); As if to put away childish things like beLIEve law & wrath are of God.
For if God were law (works wrath), or even both law & grace, who'd dare draw nigh, lest God may be in law (wrath) mode?
Spirit helped me reason grace by connect these verses:
- Our Father...deliver us from "evil": Luke 11:4 (prayer)
- We are delivered from the "law": Romans 7:6 (answered).
For law, by being both good & "evil" is lastly reverse of "live".
So to think law (wrath) is of God is to think death is of God.
But where no law, there no transgression: Rom 4:15, and 
sin is not imputed when there is no law: Rom 4:8; 5:13. For we are not under the law, but under grace: Rom 6:14.
Yet it seems because grace will not force itself on anyone, we need to reckon ourselves dead to sin to be alive unto God; As if dead to the law is then alive unto Grace, mercy, and peace.
To help with mind reckoning process the Spirit of truth is sent to guide us, as if through a minefield of mystery twists, yet will not force feed, but prompts us to study or ponder things, then provides revelation to help replace ignorance with awareness.
The "grace" of our Lord "Jesus Christ" [be/is] with you all. Amen.
