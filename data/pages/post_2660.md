## Content

Grace [charis] is MUCH MORE than Favour [charis]
How?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Both 
"favour" and "grace" are used for Greek word 'charis', yet only in Luke to Acts; Then beyond Acts only "grace" occurs for 'charis'.
Favour only occurs 6x, first for Mary (Lk 1:30), then for Jesus (Lk 2:52), but is lastly requested "against" to "kill" Paul, in Acts 25:3.
In contrast
Grace occurs over 100x, yet never against any nor to kill any.
Interesting thing is, when favour ends being mentioned beyond Acts, then the holy kiss greeting in Paul's epistles is: "Grace unto you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ". 
So it allegory and mystery reasons grace is much more than favour, even though both mean 'charis' in Greek.
It also reasons grace is not unmerited favour, but much more than favour.
Interesting "mystery' details in the biblical narrative to ponder.
What are your insights about grace being much more than favour?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
