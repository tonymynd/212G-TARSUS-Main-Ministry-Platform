## Content

Why do people seldom include John 3:17's further clarity, when quoting John 3:16? Seems...
although John 3:16's contrast: should not perish, but have everlasting life ends well; Yet 3:17's contrast adds yet more CLARITY of how: God's Son not sent to condemn the world, but that the world "THROUGH HIM" might be saved...
Eg:
- 
"through him" (aka through Jesus Christ), is further clarified in John 20:31 as "through his name" (aka through Jesus to Christ), and 7 times in Romans clarified as "through our Lord Jesus Christ": Perhaps different than Lord "Christ Jesus", 
for 
- John 1:17 clarified grace and truth came by "Jesus Christ"
- John 17:3 clarified only true God "sent" only "Jesus Christ".
Seems John 3:16 = God "gave", yet John 3:17 = God "sent".
John 3:16 noted "only begotten Son" a given, yet also for "Son" 3:17 noted "through him" is required for "world" to be saved, as if allegory saying through 2Sons Jesus (Mt-Acts) to 1SonChrist (Romans-fwd) is how "the world" gets "saved". 
For
- Jesus was only called "Saviour of Israel" = not the world.
- Christ is called "Saviour of the world" = the world
- only Christ is called the "seed", not seeds: Gal 3:16
- Jesus was only "change of the law", from OT law to NT law = better; But NT better law is not yet grace.
- Christ is "the end of the law" for righteousness = grace.
Interesting stuff to notice, ponder, and discuss
