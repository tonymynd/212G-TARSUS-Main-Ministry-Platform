## Content

FORGIVENESS?? Are there perhaps 2 Law/Grace kinds to Dare Compare? Is praying "forgive us as we forgive" perhaps not yet aware of such Law/Grace compare? Let's Dare Compare:
- Matthew 6:14,15's PS to prayer model (containing forgive us as we forgive) notes (as if by law) you must first forgive to get forgiven.
BUT
- Ephesians 4:32 clarifies God, for Christ's sake, hath (first freely) forgiven you (as if by grace); And before you forgave 
others.
SEEMS
Luke 11:1 reveals Jesus got requested to teach them to pray AS JOHN (the Baptist) taught his disciples. ODD thing about such is John didn't forgive, but accused & condemned a king. John also didn't say grace, mercy, peace to come, but "wrath to come". Such is CONTRARY to "God hath not appointed us to wrath".
PERHAPS THEN, it may be a good thing this prayer model has been removed from schools, if it contains "forgive us as we forgive" instead of God hath (freely) forgiven you (by grace).
SO
when we dare compare, the correct order should be 
freely forgiven to freely forgive; Not forgive to be forgiven.
What are your thoughts on such?
