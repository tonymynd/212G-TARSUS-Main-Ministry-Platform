# Colossians 2:12 The Operation of God removes law

**Colossians 2:12 Allegory** The faith of **The Operation of God** removes all the cancer us law.**  
For a little law can and will kill the whole body of Christ if not removed in time.  
**  
**Colossians 2:12 allegorically speaks of The Operation of God:**  
"_Buried with him in baptism, wherein also ye are risen with_ [him] _through_ _**[the faith](Faiths.md) of**_ _**the operation of God**__, who hath raised him from the dead_".

  

**The faith of the operation of God: The faith?**

**The faith** speaks of [the faith of the Son of God](Faiths.md), the better of two sorts of faith (trust: John 5:45)in grace rather than law; For conversion (repentance, change) is "from faith to faith" of [two faiths](TwainShew2.md#Faiths) compared in the Bible: blind faith in law vs seeing faith of grace. For when [connecting biblical dots](BiblicalDots.md) of Romans 14:23 and [Galatians](Revelations7.md#23) 3:11, we learn "whatsoever is not of faith is sin" and "the law is not of faith". So blind faith in the law is not real faith at all, but sin, which is imputed by [the law](Law.md), which we are [delivered from](RevelatorySermons6.md) as if delivered from [evil](BiblicalDots.md); And as if answer to the common prayer: Our Father, deliver us from evil.                        **Prayer:** Our Father, deliver us from **evil**: Matthew 6:13                        **Answer:** We are delivered from the **law**: Romans 7:6

**The faith of the operation of God: The operation of God?**

**The operation of God** speaks of an operation, which removes something from the body.

**Which things are an allegory: Bible is allegoric, allegorical**

To clarify more churchy definitions of **allegory**: **biblical allegory** is simply a story in which people, places, and things often have [another](AnotherLaw.md) meaning, kinda like **allegoric** parables and **allegoric** proverbs do. **Scriptures are allegorical**, stories with a spiritual moral of [life or death](OneLiners.md#Shakespeare) (**allegory**: grace or law) consequence. **Biblical allegory** is more than symbolic, figurative, or an extended metaphor.   
  
The [Holy Bible](biblicalemblems.md) is **allegoric**. Being **allegorical** it is a virtue-all God [shew](shew.md), whereby [all the worlds a stage](OneLiners.md#Shakespeare), and a mystery to be solved "in time" (Heb 4:12) since there's neither any mystery nor time for it in eternity; The [reconciliation](Reconciliation.md) unto God(Grace) objective being  "[with all thy getting, get understanding](Topics.md)". Then he notes "**which things are an allegory**", for these things(plura) "are the two covenants" ([two testaments](TwainShew6.md#Testaments): law/grace). What it says, in plain **allegorical** english, is [law](Law.md), which was "added" to "my grace is sufficient" (no law required) was **plan b**: "**b**ondage"; Whereas firstly pure wisdom (of [two wisdoms](TwainShew7.md#Wisdoms)) from above ([Grace](GraceUntoYou.md) unto [you](YeYou.md) from [God our Father](GodShew2.md#Allness)) is plan a: approved.  So in the exhortation: "study to [shew](shew.md) thyself [approved](GodShew2.md#Allness) unto God", "shew" suggests a study of twain things: disapproved (law) vs approved (grace), with [only](GodShew3.md#Only) [one](ShewBread2.md#OneSlice) of [twain](TwainShew.md) being what's right for you: all the KofG within "[you](YeYou.md)".
