**Bible Mystery: 2 different "Comforter"(s) in John 14-16 KJV\*:**

- Comforter1: Holy Ghost: John 14:26 KJV

- Comforter2: Spirit of truth: John 15:26; John 16:13 KJV

\*modern versions change of Holy Ghost to Holy Spirit is err.

KJV uses both "Holy Ghost" and "Holy Spirit" to distinguish,

as if allegorically distinguishing Ghost (law) & Spirit (grace).

- Comforter1 is Law, witch is both comfort + dis-comfort.

- Comforter2 is Grace, edifies + exhorts to comfort only.

Notes:

- John 1:17 KJV says "grace and truth" came by Jesus Christ.

John 3:17 KJV says God did NOT send his Son to condemn.

John 4:24 KJV clarifies "God is a Spirit" (aka not a Ghost).

So worshippers must worship that God in "spirit and truth".

John 16:13 KJV clarifies when the "Spirit of truth" is come

he will guide you (of ye/you) into "all truth".

John 17:3 KJV says "the only true God" sent "Jesus Christ".

But 3:17 also clarified salvation of the world is "through him".

through him = through his name = through Jesus→Christ

(John 3:17 ... John 20:31 ... Romans x7)

- John is the only "Verily, verily" account of the 4 gospels.

"Verily, verily" is like 'Amen, amen' to two different things.

In Numbers 5:22 "Amen, amen" is to Blessed + cursed.

In Luke 8:24 two Sons Jesus is called "Master, master".

But no man, nor servant, can serve "two masters".

So in Matthew 23:10 "one" is your "Master": "Christ".

Romans 10:4 clarifies "Christ is the end of the law".

So we only get the "all truth" from the "Spirit of truth".

Another name for "Spirit of truth" is "Spirit of grace".

**The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ** [be] **with you all. Amen.**
