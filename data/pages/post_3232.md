## Content

WHY is the gospel according to John 
the only (double) "Verily, verily" account?
Grace, Mercy, Peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
The 
gospel according to John is the only (double) "Verily, verily" account of four. Matthew, Mark, Luke all say (singular) "Verily"; but in John it says double "Verily, verily" 25x. WHY?
In Greek "Verily, verily" means 'Amen, amen', which is not only double, but Big, little. Double (Big, little) "Amen, amen" in Numbers 5:22 was by woman to being both Blessed, cursed by law priest.
Double (Big, little) "Verily, verily" [Amen, amen] in John is allegory like double (Big, little) "Master, master" in Mk 14:45 & Lk 8:24. "Master, master" is allegory like "two" many masters in Mt 6:24 & Lk 16:13. 
Seems why Mt 23:7 -10 clarified be not called Rabbi, Rabbi nor plural masters, for only "one" is your (Big) "Master": "Christ".
So when it's the gospel of "Christ" in Romans forward, it's then "Grace to you" (not law, nor both law/grace, but grace only). 
For "God is not [author] of confusion, but of peace": 1Cor 14:33.
What are your insights about double "Verily, verily" in John?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
