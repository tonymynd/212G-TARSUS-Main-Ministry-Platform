## Content

Wrath? Wrath of God? What sort of God has wrath? 
On this topic, FB friend noted: if Law & Grace so contrary both cannot co-exist in peace, it would also apply to Wrath & Mercy. Law worketh Wrath & Grace worketh Mercy; So then, we need to address "wrath of God" is not graceful, nor merciful, nor peaceful, perhaps put away a God having wrath
Discussion:
- "Law worketh wrath": Romans 4:15; so then "wrath of God" may be allegory for wrath 
of Law, evil aspect of Law both good & evil.
- Wrath is what to Put Away: Ephesians 4:31. How? If Law worketh wrath, perhaps by Put Away Law, even a Law God having wrath.
- "God hath not appointed us to wrath": 1Thessalonians 5:9. Ok, that's "us", but 1Thess 5:3 was about "them": "when they shall say, Peace and safety; then sudden destruction cometh upon them". 
Hmmm, study of wrath seems to reveal it's for them, of them/us.
