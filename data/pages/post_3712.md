KNOWLEDGE (part1)
To All: Grace, Mercy and Peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
“…and to virtue KNOWLEDGE; …” ~ 2Pe 1:5 AKJV
Walk → a more excellent way → RUN:
Love → Joy → Peace → Longsuffering → Gentleness → Goodness → Meekness → Faith → Temperance → (against such there is no law, Galatians 5:22;23; no weakness, but grace force energy to continue forward 1Peter 1:5-10) → Diligence → Virtue → KNOWLEDGE → Temperance…
 (Attribution and Spiritual Map on the comments)
---
It took me a while to study knowledge, is quite extensive, and had to do a lot of “ask God for wisdom” Jam 1:5. So, here we go…
POINT: knowledge 'gnosis' (science) differs from knowledge 'epignosis':
“For this is good and acceptable in the sight of God our Saviour; Who will have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge(epignosis) of the truth.” ~ 1Ti 2:3;4 AKJV
As we have previously learned, there is two of everything, even two knowledges in greek: ‘gnosis’ vs ‘epignosis’. But before we dive into this mystery, let’s ask this question; Why does 2Pe 1:5 tell us to add knowledge to virtue? 
“And beside this, giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue; and to virtue knowledge;” ~ 2Pe 1:5
In our previous study re “virtue” we learned there are also, two virtue(s) in the geek mode: 
- virtue ‘dynamis’: came out of Jesus when touched in Mk 5:30; Lk 6:19; Lk 8:46 but limited to presence for touch.
- virtue ‘arete’: add to your faith virtue 2Pe 1:5. Moral excellence or righteousness, not limited to touch or presence but transcends space and time. Virtue ‘arete’ is what God himself has “called us to glory and virtue(arete)” 2Pe 2:3 and think of these things: Philippians 4:8 … if there be any virtue…
So, why is virtue succeeded by knowledge?
Seems to me, that God would begin to trust us with ‘gnosis’ knowledge once we have reached “fruit of the Spirit” maturity, first baby steps, a small “walk” then a big boy Walk, Gal 5:16; Which is necessary before the Son of God “run” 1Co 9:24, at God speed.
The nine aspects of the fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:22;23) reach maturity when “against such there is no law”; meaning there is no fulfilling of the lawst of the flesh (pun intended) then “led” to the full and mature  “Walk in the Spirit” Gal 5:16;18
So, add 'gnosis' knowledge to virtue 2Pe 1:5 as a part of the things to be added to your faith.
Notice how this knowledge is still gnosis(not yet epignosis); the reason thereby is that knowledge without Self-control (temperance, our next aspect in the list, 2Pe 1:6) ensures that knowledge is used humbly and constructively, aligning with God's will rather than fueling arrogance or selfish desires.
“O Timothy, keep that which is committed to thy trust, avoiding profane and vain babblings, and oppositions of science(gnosis) falsely so called:” ~ 1Ti 6:20 AKJV.
Paul, in his epistles to puffed-up Corinthians, elaborates extensively on this heart of the matter using his rhetorical mastery applying PEP talk mode (Positive Evaluate Positive) by first edifying and validating them:
“I thank my God always on your behalf, for the grace of God which is given you by Jesus Christ; That in every thing ye are enriched by him, in all utterance, and in all knowledge(gnosis); Even as the testimony of Christ was confirmed in you: So that ye come behind in no gift; waiting for the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ:”
 ~ 1Co 1:4-7 AKJV
Then, he gradually and mercifully begins to evaluate the way they were conducting themselves, by revealing:
“Now as touching things offered unto idols, we know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge(gnosis) puffeth up, but charity edifieth.” ~ 1Co 8:1 AKJV
He essentially told them they were adoring plural idols—both a 'good idol' and an 'evil idol'—by consuming toxic food from the lousy law tree of the knowledge of good and evil (Gen 2:17), adding a little leaven that leaveneth the whole lump (Gal 5:9).
 And it did—puffing up the Corinthians, driving them into contentions and divisions among themselves (1 Cor 1:10-13), needing to be instructed like those “that oppose themselves.” 2 Timothy 2:25;26. It is interesting in verse 26 how Timothy was told to instruct them: 
“if God peradventure will give them repentance to the acknowledging (epignosis) of the truth; And that they may recover themselves out of the snare of the devil, who are taken captive by him at his will.” (such snare is the snake Gen 3:4 that lied to Eve(The Church) into transgression.
And this is how we begin to uncover this new epignosis knowledge. Stay ‘sound mind’ tuned for Part 2. Meanwhile, let us remain in:
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen
