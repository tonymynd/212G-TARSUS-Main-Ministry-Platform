GODLINESS part2: God is perfecting us.
So, godliness…what is God like: God is graceful, merciful, peaceful. God is Holy: Hallowed be thy name.
Three Best Wishes unto ALL: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.   
“and to patience godliness;” ~ 2Pe 1:6 AKJV
Love → Joy → Peace → Longsuffering → Gentleness → Goodness → Meekness → Faith → Temperance (Galatians 5:22-23) → All Diligence → Virtue → Knowledge → Temperance(2nd) → Patience → Godliness (What is God Like) → brotherly kindness 
(Attribution and Spiritual Map on the comments) 
Godliness is included in the exhortation of 1Ti 2:1-4
“I exhort therefore, that, first of all,
- supplications,
- prayers,
- intercessions, [and]
- giving of thanks,
be made for all men 'anthropos'; For kings, and [for] all that are in authority; that we may lead a quiet and peaceable life in
<<all godliness and honesty>> 
For this [is] good and acceptable in the sight of God our Saviour; Who WILL have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth.”
It reasons how godliness helps to complete and prove what is the perfect will of God which includes honesty, “impossible for God to lie” and only that which is perfect has no partiality “when that which is perfect is come, then that which is in part shall be done away.” 1Co 13:10 since his “wisdom from above” is also “without partiality” Jam 3:17
Speaking of the perfect WILL above mentioned, Romans 12:2 KJV says "And be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and PERFECT, WILL of God."
It reasons to be transformed by the renewing of mind to then be able to PROVE all THREE aspects of the will of God, which are "good (1/3), and acceptable (2/3), and PERFECT (3/3)".
Interestingly, all other aspects studied have helped us to innerstand godliness, right after patience, that already went through two temperance(s), one at Gal 5:23 and second one in 2Pe 1:6 which sandwiched knowledge ’gnosis’ that puffs up if not self-control while being led by the Spirit, Gal 5:18. So, patience builds up into godliness, since ye of ye/you need patience ‘hupomone’ to be reminded to do the WILL of God, Heb 10:36 in order to receive the promise, since “all godliness and honesty” counted in the exhortation previously mentioned in 1Ti 2:1 for it to be in sight of God our Saviour.  This begins to solve the mystery by seeking and finding.
Mystery of Godliness
“And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.” ~ 1 Timothy 3:16
“without controversy”
without law, without being contrary or having any oppositions and the like, e.g. oppositions of science 1Ti 6:20 or “any other thing that is contrary to sound doctrine” 1Ti 1:10 (revealing “…that the law is not made for the righteous…” 1 Tim 1:9) since our Christ blotted out things “contrary to us” Col 2:14.
“great is the mystery of godliness”
great from great/greater/greatest, perhaps great mystery of godliness three steps down before charity the greatest 1Co 13:13. So, it goes upward in 2Pe 1:7 like this:
godliness(great) → brotherly kindness (greater) → charity (greatest)
Mystery of godliness no doubt is great but great still has potential to turn into “great terror” given by Moses Dt 34:12, which kinda made Moses a terror-ist, perhaps why God called him "Moses, Moses" in Ex 3, to denote he was being a dblemindead man, "unstable" (James 1), by adding "law worketh wrath" to "grace is sufficient".
But what about “greater”, the law was "great". But when grace came by JC (Jn 1:17), it is said: a "greater" is here, and evidently to shew the way from great to greatest. JC, by whom His grace came, is evidently the "greater" of great/greater temples, kings, prophets in Mt 12: 6, 41, 42. He said: the house and leader of such a great house built on sand (as the house of Moses is allegorically the law) would also have a "great" fall (Mt 7:27), like a Humpty Dumpty fall of Babylon the great.
* Reference to famous nursery rhyme with rich allegorical tone in our context:
 Humpty Dumpty sat on a wall,
Humpty Dumpty had a great fall;
All the king's horses and all the king's men
Couldn't put Humpty together again.
Now in what many call the love chapter, 1Cor 13, the conclusion thereof speaks of three things: faith→hop→charity, and as if great→greater→greatest; but greater still has the potential to “greater damnation” not yet “God for Christ's sake hath forgiven you.”
Perhaps great mystery of godliness is lower than greatest charity:
Godliness is a mystery but chairity is the expression of God himself
- Godliness is a mystery, 1Ti 3:16; hidden, revealed only by the Spirit and always pointing to Jesus Christ.
"God was manifest in the flesh..." all six aspects of great mystery spoke of Christ. Notice all six are in past tense, but charity is kNOW, 1Cor 13:13
- But charity is greatest, God himself
"God is love (GEATEST charity ‘agape’); and he that dwelleth in love dwelleth in God, and God in him." 1 John 4:16
- Godliness was through sound doctrine 1Ti 6:3 →Ti 2:1, takes us to God but charity is God himself.
“…and the greatest of these is charity.” (1 Corinthians 13:13)
“God was manifest in the flesh”
(and the Word/Spirit was made flesh, “…full of grace and truth” Jhn 1:14) Such contrary Spirit vs Flesh Gal 5 ended by Christ Col 2:14 moving from “was” to “is” as “now [is] our salvation” since (by grace ye are saved;)
“justified in the Spirit” 
(by the faith of JC Gal 2:16)
“seen of angels”
 (now an in-visible God, kingdom of God with you & in you Lk 17:21)
“preached unto the Gentiles” 
(they without us should not be made perfect. Heb 11:40)
“believed on in the world”
(then known to be set free John 8:32)
“received up into glory” (“from glory to glory” this is, from earthy glory on the law to higher glory above: grace) ~ 1Ti 3:16 AKJV
This is the end of part2, which give us an initial idea of the mystery of godliness, there is more study to be done to get the full picture, and continue towards the end:
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ is with you all. Amen.
