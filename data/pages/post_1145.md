## Content

1Timothy? Here's some NIFTY things I learned about 1Timothy that I didn't [yet] even notice until I asked the God who does not even upbraid (in James 1:5) for His Wisdom:
- 1Tim is "the epistle from Laodicea" (noted in 1Tim 6:21 KJV end note) the Colossians are told to also read in Col 4:16...
- 1Tim begins six fwd mentions of phrase "God our Saviour", from 1Timothy-to-Jude, which lastly calls "God our Saviour" (allegory as if 3rd of 3 Saviours) "the only wise God"...
- 
1Tim 1 changes (as if upgrade) Paul's "holy kiss" greeting, from Grace and peace = 2 things, to Grace, mercy, and peace = 3 things; And it's always from Lord JC (not CJ); But also changes from "the" Lord JC to "our" Lord JC -- Seems why Jude 1:21 says LOOK for the "mercy" of "our" Lord JC unto eternal life, prior to enter Revelation prophecy, which (similar to 1John) notably has no mention of "mercy"...
- 1Tim 1:5 KJV seems to clarify there's also an NOW an "end" to the "commandment" (rule, ordinance, law) to "love one another" (which began in John 13:34); And now the end of the commandment (as if end of needing to be commanded) is "charity" (not love) out of a "pure heart" (established in grace void of law, in Christ of thru J→C is the end of the law, not just mend of the law)...
- 1Tim, like all Paul's epistles, seems to turn CJ around to JC, as if CJ was only the "before" mode (as noted in Eph 2:10), and JC is the "after" mode (as noted in 1Pet 5:10)... 
WHICH is quite SHOCKING, to NOTICE, there's both "created/made" modes to contrast; And even we (of they/we) first got created (of created/made) in CJ (of CJ/JC) to walk (of walk/run) in them (of them/us); And by a plural (them/us) God who NEVER said let us create(bara) man, but rather said "let us MAKE(asah) man", in Genesis 1:26a. But then in Gen 1:26b also gave two before/after scenarios to play out:
-- Before: "in our image" (aka CJ, reverse of JC)
-- After: "after our likeness" (aka turned around)
And then in Gen 1:27 "created" man in "image" mode, and called such male & female Adam "them". OMG...
