**The Path of Grace Diligence to Charity**  

Grace, mercy, and peace be unto all from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.  

Having completed the study of the **Fruit of the Spirit** in Galatians 5:22-23, we are now ready to accelerate from "walking in the Spirit" to a higher pace—running with **diligence**, as described in **2 Peter 1:5**:  

> "And beside this, giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue; and to virtue knowledge."

The word **"diligence"** ('spoude' in Greek) conveys the meaning of "earnest zeal" or "haste accompanied by effort." In some contexts, it can also imply "occupation or work" ('ergasia') and appears in various places in Scripture, revealing its significance in both law and grace.  

### The Two Types of Diligence: Law vs. Grace  
1. **Law Diligence**:  
   Under the law, diligence is often tied to works and striving in human strength, with the potential for failure.  
   - In **Luke 12:58**, the diligence ('ergasia') of someone trying to settle with an adversary before reaching court demonstrates the futility of such effort—it may lead to imprisonment.  
   - Similarly, **Matthew 7:21-23** shows that even "wonderful works" done in law-based diligence can be rebuked by Christ if they lack the foundation of grace and mercy.  
   - Law diligence reflects the weakness of the priesthood under the old covenant (**Hebrews 7:8**) and leads to frustration, as "the law worketh wrath" (**Romans 4:15**).

2. **Grace Diligence**:  
   In contrast, diligence under grace flows from the Spirit and is empowered by God's provision, not human effort.  
   - **Romans 12:8** exhorts us to render mercy with cheerfulness, reflecting diligence ('spoude') that aligns with grace.  
   - However, early manifestations of grace diligence, such as in **Jude 1:3** ("earnestly contend for the faith"), often remain in a "contending" or defensive mode. This represents an incomplete state of spiritual maturity.

### The Role of the Fruit of the Spirit in Preparing for Diligence  
Before we can fully embody grace diligence, we must be strengthened by the nine aspects of the **Fruit of the Spirit** (Galatians 5:22-23): love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, and temperance. These aspects equip us with endurance, patience, and strength to transition from walking in the Spirit to running with diligence.  
- **Love** ('agape') is the foundation, but it must grow from the imperfect love that "waxes cold" (**Matthew 24:12**) into perfected love, or **charity**, which "never faileth" (**1 Corinthians 13:8**; **1 John 4:12**).  
- **Longsuffering** (patience) builds resilience, enabling us to endure as we labor in diligence.  

### The Progressive Nature of Grace Diligence  
Grace diligence is not static; it grows and matures as we move through stages of edification:  
1. **Beginning in Faith**: Diligence first requires faith as its foundation.  
2. **Adding Virtue and Knowledge**: These qualities build strength and understanding to guide our efforts.  
3. **Reaching for Charity**: The ultimate goal of grace diligence is to lead us to charity, which is the greatest of all virtues (**2 Peter 1:7**).  

The best example of "all diligence" is found in **2 Peter 1:5**, where diligence prepares us to ascend a spiritual ladder leading to charity. This diligence transcends the weaknesses of the law because it operates in the strength of grace, enabled by the Spirit.

### Conclusion  
"Giving all diligence" is the call to action that begins with the fruit of the Spirit and culminates in charity. It requires endurance, patience, and strength, but these are supplied by God’s grace and not by human effort under the law. As we transition from law-based diligence to grace diligence, we run the race with purpose, reaching the ultimate goal of perfected love—charity—that never fails.

### The Two Paths of Diligence: Law vs. Grace

#### **1. Diligence Under the Law**

Under the law, diligence often involves striving through human effort, which can lead to frustration or even judgment:

- **Luke 12:58** portrays diligence (_ergasia_) as a futile effort to resolve a dispute before court, only to end up imprisoned.
- **Matthew 7:21-23** reveals that even remarkable "works" performed with law-based diligence are rebuked by Christ, as they lack the essence of grace and mercy.
- This form of diligence reflects the limitations of the Old Testament priesthood (**Hebrews 7:8**) and the wrathful effects of the law (**Romans 4:15**), which demands perfection yet cannot enable it.

#### **2. Diligence Under Grace**

In contrast, grace diligence is Spirit-empowered and flows from faith, not human striving:

- **Romans 12:8** encourages us to "render mercy with cheerfulness," exemplifying diligence (_spoude_) that aligns with grace.
- **2 Corinthians 8:7** refers to diligence in the context of grace, highlighting how it builds upon love and knowledge.
- **2 Peter 1:5** calls for "all diligence" to advance through spiritual growth, culminating in charity, the greatest virtue.

While early stages of grace diligence (e.g., **Jude 1:3**) may involve contending for the faith, its ultimate purpose is to lead us beyond contention into peace and perfected love.

---

### Preparation Through the Fruit of the Spirit

Before running with diligence, we must be fortified by the **Fruit of the Spirit**: love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, and temperance (Galatians 5:22-23). These attributes provide the patience, endurance, and strength needed to labor effectively under grace:

- **Love** (_agape_) must grow from the love that "waxes cold" (**Matthew 24:12**) to **charity**, or perfected love, which "never fails" (**1 Corinthians 13:8**).
- **Longsuffering** (patience) fosters resilience, enabling us to endure trials as we work with diligence.

This preparation ensures that we "lay aside every weight" (**Hebrews 12:1**) and transition from walking to running in the Spirit.

---

### The Progressive Nature of Grace Diligence

Grace diligence is not static; it evolves as we ascend a spiritual ladder:

1. **Faith** forms the foundation of our diligence.
2. **Virtue and Knowledge** are added as guiding principles.
3. **Charity** becomes the pinnacle, fulfilling the commandment to love as Christ loves us (**2 Peter 1:7**).

The repeated exhortation to "give all diligence" (**2 Peter 1:5, 10**) underscores the transformative nature of this journey. Unlike the striving of the law, grace diligence empowers us to run the race with patience and purpose (**Hebrews 12:1-2**; **Philippians 3:14**).
