## Content

Prayer? Should we still Ask Our Father, or Give Thanks for answered prayer? Let's look at Luke 11, see all answered:
Grace, mercy, peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus→Christ.
Seems, when review things in Luke 11 prayer, all answered:
 - thy kingdom come: Lk 11
- 
kingdom of God is (now) within you: Lk 17:21
- thy will (to have mercy, and not sacrifice) be done
- eternal life (already JC) given before the cross: Jn 17:2-4
- give us day by day our daily bread (our daily spiritual food)
- Spirit of truth will comfort & guide you: Jn 14:16,17; 16:13
- forgive us our sins
- God has forgiven you all tresspases: Eph 4:32; Col 2:13
- lead us not into temptation
- true God doesn't ever tempt anyone: James 1:13
- deliver us from evil (from our reverse of "live" thinking)
- we are delivered from the law (flaw thinking): Rom 7:6
So no need to still prayer ask for what's already been given;
But now give thanks, daily, for Father's Grace, mercy, peace. 
Yet James 1:5 notes we should ask for wisdom (awareness); And Jam 3:17 says the wisdom from above is "first pure", so then it's these 7 things when coming down to & within us:
- peace-able (Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father)
- gentle (lovingly, but not gentle & forceful like law)
- easy to be entreated (not hard to swallow)
- full of mercy (empty of sacrifice mentality)
- and of good fruits (not just law, but grace goodness)
- without partiality (true God is no respecter of persons)
- without hypocrisy (no such law leaven in grace heaven)
The grace of our Lord Jesus→Christ [be/IS] with you all. Amen.
