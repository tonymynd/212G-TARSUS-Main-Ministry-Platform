# MEEKNESS 
8th aspect of The Fruit comes after Faith = 7th; before Temperance = 9th, and 10 more aspects to go till "charity" The Greatest. Why? Seems we needed a Faith upgrade to help us get to and through Meekness unto Temperance.

To ALL: Grace, Mercy and Peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.

Thank you for doing the Big Walk in the Spirit with me, so we can have Fruit. Yes, we are patiently doing the Walk, as if we are collectively a seed that grows at its own pace, to give the most ripe and mature fruit. Then we can run, that we may obtain 1Co 9:24

---

What can we now learn about meekness, besides carnal mind think it’s weakness?

The Greek for “meekness” is ‘praotes’: meaning mildness, gentleness; Yet not both the kindness & unkindness (severity) which “gentleness”(‘chrestotes’) was in Midst of 9, by still having “goodness and severity” (ends with ouch); But rather the other “gentleness” (‘epiekeia’) of “Christ” (not Jesus) in 2Cor 10:1, which is “meekness and gentleness”. Seems we cannot have the correct sort of gentleness without firstly having meekness…

SO, Paul’s epistles, being the “gospel of Christ” (a beseeching that has meekness and gentleness) use only ‘praotes’ kind of meekness; Not like 3 mentions of another kind of meekness (praytes) used twice by James (re soul of soul/spirit) and last by 1Peter 3:15 = both soulish “meekness and fear”. Fear is not what God has given us. So we have to get beyond soulish 1Peter, into spiritual 2Peter, before seeing Luke 22:32’s ‘epistrepho’ conversion of Peter; As if a “converted” upgrade (from ‘strepho’ converted to ‘epistrepho’ converted) for Peter, by having read all Paul’s epistles, like 2Timothy 1:7’s “God hath not given us the spirit of fear, but

of power,

and of love,

and of a sound mind” (3rdly a Sound Mind = Self-Control = Temperance).

A sound mind(‘sophronismos’) is from ‘sophronizo’ = teach to be sober, is from ‘sophron’ = temperate, which is by the curbing of one’s soulish desires & impulses.

So in our Intro Chapter lists (Gal 5:22,23 & 2Pet 1:5-10), the first list of 9 Fruit takes us FROM Love→TO→Temperance; Then the 2nd list also adds Temperance as 3rd of 7 things to add to your faith (your faith only saves your soul, of soul/spirit). And to get to unto Temperance = 9th, we first go through, to become Meekness = 8th Fruit aspect. So…

Let’s now walk thru 9 mentions of meekness in Paul’s epistles to see what we can learn:

- 1Cor 4:21 KJV asks puffed up Corinthians: what will YE (of ye/you)? Shall I come to YOU (of ye/you) with a rod (staff, sceptre, to BEAT DOWN) -or- in love and in the spirit of meekness.

SO, meekness is spirit, of soul/spirit, coming to you, of ye/you. Seems “rod” Paul mentions herein later becomes a “rod of iron” in Revelation, with “fierceness of wrath” iff one like Son of man should come with clouds, resulting in ww “wail”. So the obvious answer to his contrast question: rod or spirit of meekness, would be spirit of meekness. For previously in 1Cor 4:14 Paul noted he wasn’t writing to shame, but warn [admonish].

- 2Cor 10:1 KJV clarifies the purpose of Paul’s 2nd writing unto Corinthians (in Achaia = trouble) is to better “beseech” with the “meekness and gentleness of Christ”, and notably then (in the Greek) as the better of 2 kinds of “beseech” and 2 kinds of “bold” in 10:1,2.

SO, the spirit of meekness has a better “beseech” & a better “bold” aspect, as if then beseeched by God himself, via an ambassador for Christ (to whom the word of reconciliation has been committed), which in 2Cor 5:20 ‘parakeleo’ beseeches in Christ’s stead: be ye reconciled to God (in that Christ, of through Jesus→Christ→God=3rd of 3).

- Gal 5:23 puts Meekness as 8th Fruit of Spirit, after Faith –and- before Temperance.

SO, to have a Sound-Mind = Self-Control, first requires have a spirit of meekness, the ‘praotes’ = spiritual meekness, as if an upgrade from ‘praytes’ = soulish meekness = both meekness and fear. Fear hath torment. So it’s allegory as if an upgrade from the weakness meekness of the soul mode unto the strengthened meekness of the spirit mode.

- Eph 4:1-3 KJV “I therefore (re Eph 3), the prisoner of the Lord (Jesus Christ: 3:14), beseech YOU that YE walk worthy of the vocation (calling) wherewith YE are called (invited), With lowliness (humbleness of mind) and meekness, with longsuffering, forbearing one another in love; Endeavouring to keep the unity (oneness) of the Spirit in the bond of peace (allegory like ligaments binding body parts together)”.

SO, per Eph 3, the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ wants YOU strengthened in the inner man, by Faith (7th Fruit), so YE may be rooted and grounded enough in Love (1st Fruit), to then be able to also comprehend the breadth, and length, and depth*, and height of the fulness of God, by first have the fulness of the stature of Christ, which has meekness and gentleness in it’s beseech aspect, along with longsuffering, forbearing one another in love, thereby endeavouring to keep the “unity of the Spirit”, in “the bond of peace”, of “Grace to YOU, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ”: Eph 1:2.

*in Revelation 21:16 there’s not [yet] any depth, only equal breadth, length, height.

- Col 3:12 KJV lists meekness (8th Fruit) as the 4th in a list of 7 things for the new man to Put On after the old man(‘anthropos’) firstly Puts Off 2 lists of things, in order for the new man to thereby also be reNEWed in the ‘epignosis’ knowledge (more than ‘gnosis’ knowledge), which notably only comes after (not in) the image mode of image/likeness, as then being made which comes after created mode of created/made.

SO, to put on meekness firstly requires put off these things in Col 3:5’s list1: fornication, uncleaness, inordinate affection, evil concupiscence, covetousness (idolatry); Because it’s due to such things (law worketh) wrath comes upon children. So then, also put off these things in Col 3:8,9’s list2: anger, wrath, malice, blasphemy, filthy communication, lying.

- 1Tim 6:9-12 seems to scold Timothy for desiring to be affluent, having “love of money” (‘philarguria’ = avarice), which causes people to fall into temptations, snares, sorrows, by err from the faith (aka from the grace, since the law is not of faith, and whatsoever is not of faith is sin); Then tells Timothy he’s still an ‘anthropos’ man of God who should flee such things, and follow after righteousness, godliness, faith, love, patience, meekeness. Then allegory says after you first fight the good fight of faith (grace, since law is not of faith), then “lay hold on eternal life”, whereunto thou art “ALSO called”, as if the ALSO denotes a higher calling than the high calling of God in CJ = still FightClub mode, and why 1Tim 6:17 says charge them who’d still be $ rich that they be not highminded.

SO, let’s also notice we’re now in “the epistle from Laodicea” (Col 4:16) = 1Timothy (1Tim 6:21 KJV <end note>), which CJs at Colosse were told to ALSO read; Perhaps because it begins mention of the phrase “God our Saviour”, also mention of “the end of the commandment” phase (1Tim 1:5 KJV), also what God our Saviour will have, as being more than “good and acceptable”, such as all men (‘anthropos’) saved and come unto the (‘epignosis’) knowledge of the truth (more than ‘gnosis’ knowledge, shall vanish away).

- 2Tim 2:24-26 notes it’s in meekness a servant of the Lord (aka bishop) should patiently instruct (as if training children, through both elementary and high schooling phases) “them who oppose themselves” (as children vs children), so they (having childish feuds, even as adults) may recover themselves (return to soberness) out of the snare (of retaliate, fight, quarrel, debate, do combat) of the d-evil (law).

SO, it’s as if apostle Paul is still scolding & instructing his childish bishop Timothy, to re-mind him meekness is not weakness, but a strength the inner man should have, and thereby be able to avoid the foolish & unlearned questions & contentions which only gender to strifes (not to peace).

- Tit 3:2 written to another bishop, notes speak evil of no man, to be no brawlers, but gentle (‘epieikes’ = equitable, fair, mild, like the pure wisdom from above [GraceToU from God] in James 3:17 is gentle), shewing all meekness unto all men (anthropos).

SO, such connects gentle (‘epieikes’) to meekness (‘praotes’), and we also find that gentle in the general epistle of James, as second of seven aspects of God’s pure wisdom, is 1)peaceable, 2)gentle, 3)easy to be intreated, 4)full of mercy, 5)full of good fruits, 6)without partiality, 7)without hypocrisy. That describes grace, not law.

In summary, meekness (praotes) is 8th Fruit aspect found only in Paul’s epistles, which helps us attain temperance, also found in 2Peter, where Peter admits reading all Paul’s epistle; Allegory as if it’s how Peter got upgraded from ‘strepho’ to ‘epistrepho’ converted. Interesting.
