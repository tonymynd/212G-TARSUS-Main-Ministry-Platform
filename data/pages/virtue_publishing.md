VIRTUE  
Three best wishes unto ALL: grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.  
  
“…add to your faith VIRTUE…” ~ 2Pe 1:5 AKJV  
  
Love → Joy → Peace → Longsuffering → Gentleness → Goodness → Meekness → Faith → Temperance → Diligence → Virtue → Knowledge  
(Attribution and Spiritual Map on the comments)  
  
POINT: New Virtuous Man “that striveth for the mastery(excellence) is temperate in all things” is not about following rules (Touch not; taste not; handle not; Which all are to perish with the using;) But is called to GLORY (true worshiper Joh 4:24) and VIRTUE 1Pe 1:3 (perfected man ‘aner’ Eph 4:13) who does the perfect Will of God “I will have mercy and not sacrifice” from the allegory of “be ye merciful” Lk 6:36 = “be ye therefore perfect” Mt 5:48 as our Father; by being ‘epistrepho’ converted Luk 22:32 (having reached spiritual adulthood from children ‘strepho’ converted) is when and then able to strengthen brethren:  
  
“According as his divine power hath given unto us all things that [pertain] unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue:” ~ 2Pe 1:3 AKJV.  
  
Called to glory and virtue, the journey unfolds as a refining process, where faith matures from understanding to innerstanding into action. The higher virtue, as the perfected man 'aner' embodies, is no mere a passive attribute but an active expression of the divine nature imparted to us. In this pursuit, the Spirit leads us from hints of the healing grace, like the 'dynamis' virtue flowing from Jesus’ garment when touched to the transformative 'arete' virtue that crowns the spiritual maturity outlined in 2Pe 1:5.  
  
So, we are called not only to receive but to grow, strengthening others as we ourselves are perfected in mercy and truth, we now carry the mind of Christ, “is not I, but Christ that liveth in me”, hence the exhortation list noted in Philippians 4:8 KJV:  
- whatsoever things are true,  
- whatsoever things [are] honest,  
- whatsoever things [are] just,  
- whatsoever things [are] pure,  
- whatsoever things [are] lovely,  
- whatsoever things [are] of good report;  
- if [there be] any VIRTUE, and if [there be] any praise,  
think on these things.  
  
What else can we learn re virtue?  
As we know now, there are two of everything in the bible narrative, and “virtue” is no exception. Two kinds of virtue in geek mode:  
- Virtue ‘dynamis’: (power) came out of Jesus when touched in Mk 5:30; Lk 6:19; Lk 8:46  
- Virtue ‘arete’: add to your faith virtue 2Pe 1:5  
  
Such difference can be as different as the two kinds of man, where one needed to be converted through two stages of conversion:  
  
Two Types of Man: Old Man vs New Man  
- Old Man: judge-mental and a liar (man ‘anthropos’ Rom 3:4 which I was) prone to corruption (Eph 4:22, Col 3:9). Such child/man exhorted to 1st ‘strepho’ converted Mat 18:13 as little children, yet still in jeopardy every hour b/c even children of the kingdom get cast out Mat 8:12. So, an upgrade is necessary to avoid judgment (neither to receive it nor give it), hence the call to “be no more children” b/c “When I was a child, I spake as a child, I understood as a child, I thought as a child: but when I became a man, I put away childish things(laws).”  
  
- New Man: called to righteousness, by letting go of the old man with his lies and deceptions, prone to error, to fall, trying to earn God by his own earthy wisdom unaware that the “the law is not faith”. This is why, add your faith virtue ‘arete’ as the capacity to self-regulate (aka temperance), to know what to do and what to say at the right time in the right place without having to judge, but rather edify, hence the need of all previous aspects of the Fruit of the Spirit in Galatians 5.  
  
Spiritual maturity requires to move from babes still in believing mode with potential of believing lies, this why we are told twice “believe it not” Mat 24:23; 26 b/c is not believing what sets free, but knowing John 8:32  
  
Virtuous man ‘aner’ Eph 4:13 is able now to run the race that is set before us, being aware that he is now purged once and forever from his old sins, no more aware of law nor remembrance of again of sins Heb 10, being transformed by the renewal of mind Rom 12:2.  
  
Virtue ‘arete’ is mastery in the capacity to know how and why his grace is sufficient (no law required) in all qualities of Spiritual Life, to be fully equipped to reach the end:  
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [is/be] with you all. Amen.
