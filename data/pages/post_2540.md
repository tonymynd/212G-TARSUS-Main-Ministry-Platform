## Content

"not under the law, but under grace" ?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
Let's notice this Romans 6:14 revealed change of "not under the law, but under grace" so that "sin shall not have dominion over you" doesn't NT occur until Romans, which is then the gospel of "Christ", of through Jesus → Christ is the end of the law: Rom 10:4.
What 
many don't notice is that in Romans forward, as then the gospel of Christ, there is then no more mention of Son of man, only of Son of God for Jesus. 
Previously in Matthew to Acts Jesus was both Son of man and Son of God, with two different voices, as he noted in John 5:25-29.
Son of God voice says "live".
Son of man voice says "judgment [krisis] also".
(seems that is allegory like both grace & law also)
So it's like a change 
from 2 Sons Jesus: both Son of man & Son of God (in Mt - Acts), 
to 1 Son Christ: only Son of God (in Romans forward).
Seems to explain why with Son of man Jesus it was both good and bad, new and old; But with Son of God Jesus it's all good, all new.
What are your insights about such ?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
