## Content

TWO kinds of "good" & "goodness" in Greek for contrast:
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God, our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Good: kalos & agathos in Greek for contrast:
- 
Jesus said God is good [agathos]: Lk 18:19.
- Paul said good [kalos] also has evil [kakos] present: Romans 7:21.
note: 
when made under law, Jesus called himself the Good [kalos] Shepherd in John 10:10,14 KJV..
Goodness: chrestotes & agathosyne in Greek for contrast:
- Paul allegory satire noted in Romans 11:22 KJV, that if perceive Law as God, then what to then behold is (both) conditional ("if") goodness [chrestotes] and severity (attached).
- Paul noted 6th fruit of Spirit is goodness [agathosyne]: Galatians 5:22,23 KJV.
note:
5th fruit of spirit, called "gentleness" [chrestotes] is called "goodness" [chrestotes] in Rom 2:4 KJV.
interesting mystery details in Greek to notice.
Could such be as if allegoric contrast of law & grace?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
