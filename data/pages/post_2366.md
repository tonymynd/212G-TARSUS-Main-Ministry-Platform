## Content

"God our Saviour": 
will not both save and destroy after, like LORD [Yehova] did,
but will both save and aware after, as "the only wise God".
Let's investigate this contrast & conclusion re "God our Saviour":
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
The phrase "God our Saviour" occurs 6x in 1Tim 1:1 - Jude 1:25.
(1Timothy 1:1, 2:3; Titus 1:3, 2:10, 3:4; Jude 1:25)
1Timothy 2:3,4 notes God our Saviour will have:
ALL men [anthropos] saved (by grace through faith),
AND 
come unto the (higher) knowledge [epignosis] of the truth.
So that is all saved and made aware after.
So this differs from what the LORD [Yehova] did,
both saved people out of Egypt and destroyed afterward,
which Jude 1:5 reminds of, as if in contrast to God our Saviour.
So then Jude 1:25 contrast concludes this:
The "only wise God" is "God our Saviour":
Jude 1:25
"To the only wise God our Saviour, be glory and majesty, dominion and power, both now and ever. Amen".
What are your insights about "God our Saviour" ?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
