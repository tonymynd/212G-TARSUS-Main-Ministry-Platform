### 15. Peace (Part 1)
PEACE: 3rd Fruit of the Spirit and 15th aspect of "things above" study (links to the series of study on the comments)

When I first started studying "the fruit of the Spirit", I thought it would be a simple matter of looking up dictionary definitions for each aspect, Yet, as I did more research the more I found myself returning to them for a more profound understanding. The reason thereby was that as I apply these concepts with simple dictionary understanding, did not have sufficient effect or flat out ineffective, I was missing much interconnectivity with other mysteries in the bible. The same is true for "peace".

As I continued my research about "things above" (Col 3:2) which includes fruit "the fruit of the Spirit", walking through each aspect from love to peace provided beneficial growth, yet left me with unanswered questions and a desire for deeper understanding of their interconnections. My studies led me to examine parallel passages in Galatians 5 that discuss different modes of  'walk' and fruit bearing.

An intriguing distinction emerged between types of "walk" mentioned there. Likewise, realized multiple words in Greek translated as "peace" in the New Testament (AKJV Bible), unveiling layers of meaning.

Walk → Run
WALKing in the Spirit through 17.Love→16.Joy→**15.Peace** … leads us closer to Temperance = SelfControl = “_against such there is no law_”: Galatians 5:22→23. YET in Galatians 5 KJV there’s even two kinds of “walk” in Greek mode (neither yet a “run, that ye may obtain”):

- “Walk”: ‘peripateo’: Galatians 5:16 = before fruit of the Spirit: as if an adventure Walk
- “walk”:  ‘stoicheo’: Galatians 5:25 = after fruit of the Spirit: more like an orderly walk

Interesting how there is a before Walk & after walk, where Gal 5:25 is both, live & walk in the Spirit once in orderly walk mode.

YES, 1Corinthians 14:33 KJV says
“_For God is not_ [the author] _of confusion, but of peace 'eirene'_ ”.

YET, as we’ve noted about other New Testament words, like “_love_”, there’s sometimes as many as 7 kinds in the Greek mode to sort out. So also there’s 6 kinds of NT “_peace_”: 
- eirene
- eireneuo
- siapao
- phimoo
- hsychazo
- siago
The most commonly used being the feminine noun ‘eirene’ = _peace_ x89, _one_ x1, _rest_ x1, _quietness_ x1; as in 1Cor 14:33.

BUT, like “_Walk_” & “_walk_” in Gal 5, there’s “_Peace_” & “_peace_”, one being Capitalized; Perhaps like “_heaven_” & “_Heaven_”, or “_earth_” & “_Earth_” in Genesis 1 of KJV, perhaps to allegorically distinguish _created_ (‘bara’) & _made_ (‘asah’), where only what’s “_made_” or “_let_”  is what God saw _good_ x6, then _very good_ 7th time (before 7th day in Genesis 2).

The distinction between "made" and "created" is important, as "made" indicated formation only, does not includes destruction, whereas "create" speaks of a creator/destroyer.

So then, let’s notice this: in doing the Walk (‘peripateo’) in the Spirit, Led of the Spirit, it’s firstly as if in “ye” (do err) mode, of ye/you; Whereby “_if **ye** be led of the Spirit **ye** are not under the law”_. Eg: In Romans 6:14’s ye/you, it’s “_For sin shall not have dominion over **you**: for **ye** are not under the law, but under grace”._ So it’s kinda like a transition, from being _under the law_ to being _under grace_, even for ye, of ye/you, in KJV.

It seems to be getting to technical when looking at Greek & KJV about such, but there’s lots of twists in every good “_mystery_”, and lots to “_learn_” about each “_fruit_”; Especially when considering the “_allegory_” aspect. For when still in the duality mode of PluralGod, it seems there’s [[GMP/Notes/two of everything]] to sort out, even two kinds of “_above_”.


Perhaps, for now, we could allegory picture “_not under the law, but under grace_”, as this:
OT Law(_under law_)←|→NT BetterLaw: J→C(_under grace_)→↑ _established with grace_ ↑. *

* "with grace" A higher concept beyond the notions of being "under law" and "under grace". This concept is being "with grace". The term "under" in both "under law" and "under grace" implies a certain subjection or submission (still at risk and potential for an undertaker). Being "with grace" signifies a companionship or partnership with grace, a state where grace is not just a covering but an integral part of one's life. [end of  Part 1]

 I hope this initial discussion of peace as the fifteenth aspect of "things above" has offered beneficial perspective as we journey together towards "charity" the greatest. Stay tuned for Part 2, where our search through related passages will continue.
