Part-iality Error of Making Sides and Taking a Side for a Sides War  

Two divisional parts: their part (autos: bafflng bwd wind) and your part (humas: you) are mentioned in 1Peter 4:14. At first glance "your part" seems the better part of two parts; Especially when looking at the consequence of playing their part mentioned in Revelation 21:8: "shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the [second death](TwainShew2.md#Deaths)"... Such would be a fall, a draw back to perdition (Gal 5:4 & Heb 10) instead of a [go on to perfection](Hebrews7.md) (Hebrews 6:1>13:25).  
  

Leave the sides war part-iality to go on unto perfection  

But when going on unto [perfection](Hebrews7.md), neither part of any [sides war](RevelatorySermons15.md) is perfect (whole); Rather it's part-iality on both parts, which has side effects to both sides, the most notable side effect being the grave, and thereby victory goes to the law: the strength of sin's death sting, as is noted in 1Corinthians 15:55, 56. So it's a major oops to be inducted into such a hall of shame on [high](Highminded.md) beneath the hall of fame [above](RevelatorySermons21.md), especially if told God hath provided some better thing for [us](Usward.md), which is neither of [them vs them](TwainShew6.md#Thems) oppose themselves, and neither of [Adam1 vs Adam2](TwainShew.md#Adams) nor [Law1 vs Law2](TwainShew4.md#Laws) if as in Adam all die and by using laws [all](TwainShew.md#Alls) perish.  
  

When perfection comes, then that which is in part (sides wars) shall be done away  

[Paul](Paul.md) says when that which is perfect is come, then that which is in part shall be [done away](Vanish.md). So when wholeness or [allness](GodShew2.md) comes, both parts of part-iality is done away. He goes on to say he put away [childish](GodShew.md#nomorechildren) things (laws, which make sides wars) when he became a [man](TwainShew.md#Blesseds): 1Corinthians 13. So the first of [seven criteria for being born again](BornAgain.md) is it happens to man of child/man. For God did not say let us make child, rather God said "let us make man": Genesis 1:26. For a child is a know not and differs nothing from a servant(slave), but a man should also take note "that no man is justified by the law in the sight of God". So man of child/man is allegorically grace of law/grace. But perfection is not being right of law/grace, but rather being all grace and no law at all, in the same manner [that God](GodShew2.md) is all light and no darkness at all. We could also say all life and no death at all.
