Second Tomb of Lazarus in Larnaca, Cyprus?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ. 
Legend reports that Lazarus of Bethany, whom Jesus raised from the dead after four days in the tomb (John 11), later went to Cyprus, about AD45, to flee continuous death threats of Jewish priests (John 12:10), and was a bishop there until about AD75.
Seems, legend reports, a tomb found underground had an  inscription of Four Days Lazarus on the lid, so a 9th century church called Church of St. Lazarus was rebuilt over it in Larnaca, Cyprus.
The church and lower level tombs are now a tourist attraction for people visiting Cyprus, where Larnaca has the main Cyprus airport.  
I had never before heard of this legend, nor of Larnaca, Cyprus,  being the second tomb of Lazarus who lived and died twice.
What are your insights about such?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
