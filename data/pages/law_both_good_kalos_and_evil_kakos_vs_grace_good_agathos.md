LAW: both good [kalos] and evil [kakos] vs GRACE: good [agathos]
Grace, Mercy, Peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
GRACE is good [agathos]; But never good [kalos] nor evil [kakos].
LAW is both good [kalos] and evil [kakos]. So in law mode evil is also present with good. So both goodness and severity if law as God, but goodness [chrestotes] conditional ("if") on you being good, else severity for you also: as noted in Romans 11:21,22.
Maturity discerns (to avoid) both good and evil of law: Heb 5:14.
For along with law good [kalos] comes law evil [kakos]: Rom 7:21.
So it's "evil [kakos] concupiscence" (of law) in Colossians 3:5.
Paul noted in Romans 7:21 (Greek mode):
I find then a law, that, when I would do good [kalos], evil [kakos] is present with me.
So as a good [kalos] law Pharisee, Saul also had an evil aspect. Hence a need for change from law good [kalos] and evil [kakos] Saul to Paul ministering the gospel of the grace of God is alway and only good [agathos].
So when brethren say God is always good, I now perceive what they are saying is God is always and only grace good [agathos]. The God of all grace is always all grace (allegory: no law at all).
What are your insights about good [kalos] vs good [agathos] ?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
