AWARENESS: KJV uses both "love" & "charity" for Greek 'agape';
Perhaps as if to allegory contrast "wax cold" vs "never faileth".
Grace, mercy, peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Modern bible readers (since Y2Ks) have only read "love" for 1Cor 13 which said readers call the "love" chapter, which in KJV is the "charity" chapter describing a "more excellent way" noted in 1Cor 12:31... 
What they don't know is their modern bibles (including NKJV) contradict themselves by saying both "love" of many shall "wax cold" (of 3 modes: Cold ←> LukeWarm → Hot) in Matthew 24:12 and "love never fails" in 1Cor 13:8...
This is a contradiction, and why KJV uses "love" for Mt 24:12 and "charity" for 1Cor 13:all; to aware charity "thinketh no evil": 1Cor 13:5 is why/how "charity never faileth": 1Cor 13:8, in contrast to "love" of many "shall wax cold" is to allegory say it fails for many. 
Matthew 24:12 (words of Jesus):
And because iniquity shall abound, the love [agape] of many shall wax cold. 
1Cor 13:8 KJV (words of Paul, apostle of Jesus Christ):
Charity never faileth: but
whether (law) prophecies, they shall fail,
whether (law) tongues, they shall cease,
whether (law) knowledge [gnosis], it shall vanish away.
In KJV, it not only uses "charity" (not love) for 1Cor 13:all, but also uses "charity" (not love) in Colossians 3:12-15 to note "charity" can be new man put on even above forgiving one another, and thereby as 8th above 7; Like in Eph 4:4-6 Father is noted as 8th above 7.
So, in addition to not contradicting itself by using love & charity, KJV also notes charity (not love) can be new man put on as the 8th above 7 things, twice noted in Col 3:12-15 & 2Peter 1:5-11 KJV. This 8th above 7 aspect is also allegory noted in James 3:17 to aware wisdom that is from above is first pure, then 7 things.
Sadly, rather than awareness of such things in KJV, many beLIEve and say KJV is ancient English for using both ye/you, not aware KJV often uses both ye/you in same verses for allegory contrast, as it also does with love/charity for agape, and Ghost/Spirit for pneuma. Many like to bash KJV by say it's translators often miss-translated.
What are your insights about why KJV uses both love & charity?
