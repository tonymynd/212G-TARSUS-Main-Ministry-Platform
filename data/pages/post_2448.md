## Content

Of CHRIST JESUS & JESUS CHRIST, ONLY Jesus Christ is Saviour.
ONLY Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever.

PONDERING about CHRIST JESUS & JESUS CHRIST Bible mentions:
Loving Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God, our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ. It's interesting The New Testament of our Lord and Saviour JESUS CHRIST also makes mention of CHRIST JESUS 57x, and of being "IN CHRIST JESUS" 40x; Where-In no condemnation to them, for the love of God is inseparable there-in: Rom 8:1-39; YES, But...
A mystery to ponder is why does mention of CHRIST 
JESUS only occur from Acts 19:4 to 1Pet 5:14, Never before Acts 19 Nor after 1Pet 5, which is then only of our Lord & Saviour JESUS CHRIST?
Why is CHRIST JESUS only mentioned 57x & only by 3 Bible writers: Luke 1x, Paul 54x, 1Peter 2x; But in comparison JESUS CHRIST is mentioned 196x, and by all NT writers?
Why does Acts note that after Saul becomes Paul, then Paul also  goes through further change from CHRIST JESUS mode in Acts 19:4 to JESUS CHRIST mode in Acts 28:31; So then in Romans forward always first clarifies he's now in JESUS CHRIST mode?
Why do Paul & 1Peter first clarify they are an apostle of JESUS CHRIST prior to mention of both CHRIST JESUS & JESUS CHRIST?
Why does Paul 1Cor 1:1-4 clarify grace to them in CHRIST JESUS is given by JESUS CHRIST; And 2Cor 5:18 clarify God has reconciled us to himself by JESUS CHRIST?
Why does Paul always turn mention of CHRIST JESUS around to JESUS CHRIST in all his epistles, even in Hebrews? 
Why does Phil 2:5-11 mention both CHRIST JESUS & JESUS CHRIST, but lastly clarify every tongue should confess that JESUS CHRIST is Lord to the glory of God the Father?
Why does Heb 13:8 clarify (of CHRIST JESUS & JESUS CHRIST) only JESUS CHRIST is the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever?
Why do some modern bibles change JESUS CHRIST to CHRIST JESUS in some places, and many remove word "Christ" from end?
Why does the end Jesus said to endure unto, to be saved (only), say:
The grace of our Lord JESUS CHRIST be with you all. Amen.
