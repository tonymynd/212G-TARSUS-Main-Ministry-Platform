Current Event: Interesting Phrase: 'The day of four Popes':
1. John the XXIII... dead (being sainted)
2. John-Paul II... dead (being sainted)
3. Benedict... still alive (quit before dead)
4. Frances... alive (Time's man of the year)
Not 
to accuse, nor condemn, nor vilify; But to be curious:
What if we compared 4 popes to 4 horses of Revelation?
Iff this plausible, it would make John-Paul II as if red horse.
Something interesting about this John-Paul II:
In 1999, and pending Y2K, John-Paul II said this:
- 'the l-o-n-g tradition of sacrifice must continue'...
- 'in the third millennium'
My Bible seems to suggest wrong on both accounts,
- will of God is: "I will have mercy, and not sacrifice";
- "the third day" (of AD→) is perhaps not millennial*.
*so I do not use a Third Millennium Bible, but the KJV.
So, what followed John-Paul II's 1999 f-law saying was:
- 9/11 (as if a red horse witch removed peace from earth)
- a decade of un-winnable war (with plenty of sacrifices)
So, why would John-Paul II be given saint-hood, by RCC?
Why would 1000s, including world dignitaries, flock to such?
Could day of 4 popes be like Amos 5:18's day of the LORD:
Woe unto you that desire the day of the LORD! 
to what end [is] it for you? 
the day of the LORD [is] darkness, and not light. 
IFF 'the day of four popes' could be like-end unto four horses,
it'd make Benedict as a black horse, Frances as a pale horse.
But again I say, Not for accusation, Nor condemnation to any.
Rather a Horeb-bull thought, when hearing about such a day.
So let's abound as race pace of grace does: Romans 5:20 KJV:
Grace "much more" abounds than any off-ending f-law abounds;

"The grace of our Lord Jesus→Christ [be] with you→all. Amen"
