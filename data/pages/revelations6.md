It involves seek and find, and see-king according to all the clues, finding grace to help (for law hinders), and taking heed of what His grace says, sorting out [first/last Adams](TwainShew.md#Adams) as a comparison, not a mixture; But then also going on to Christ of Adams/Christ, to be reconciled in that Christ (of false/true Christs rise) unto that God cannot lie nor die. When Jesus says many shall come and shall deceive many, he is not kidding, for to kid is to deceive, and deception is not the part played by grace, but the part played by law, given by Moses. But law is simply a counter part in a shew, the part to be done away once revelation of the truth is made known. God, His grace, can neither be tempted (lawed) nor tempteth anyone. And the point of the shew is for you to learn, know the truth, get understanding, so you also will never be tempted, duped, deceived, suckered, hooked, victimized, bullied by anyone.  For it is not believe the lie/truth which makes you free, but kNOW the truth shall make you free. The truth is grace is sufficient (no law required), and grace with you all is how it ends, and the last Amen to that is what makes both the Bible and you all one thing: Holy (not Holy Holy). So when it comes to "that Jesus Christ is come" ("came" already), it's not first + last, nor the first shall be last, nor any last state worse, but rather grace and truth, grace for salvation, truth for awareness of it. It is not the first + last trumps by which we are changed, from (law) glory to (grace) glory: 2Cor 3, from this to that, and by "that" Spirit (2Cor 3;17). Revelation (about Revelation) #20  

  

Revelation is "prophecy", as mentioned seven times therein  
But first and last described as this prophecy (of this/that)  
  

**[Grace unto you](GraceUntoYou.md), and peace,  
from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ**  

  

Seven times in Revelation we're told it's a book of "prophecy" ("this prophecy"):  

(i) Revelation 1:3 - Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time is at hand  
(ii) Revelation 11: 6 - These have power to shut heaven, that it rain not in the days of their prophecy: and have power over waters to turn them to blood, and to smite the earth with all plagues, as often as they will  
(iii) Revelation 19:10 - And I fell at his feet to worship him. And he said unto me, See thou do it not: I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren that have the testimony of Jesus: worship God: for the testimony of Jesus Christ is the spirit of prophecy.  
(iv) Revelation  22:7 - Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book.  
(v) Revelation 22:10 - And he saith unto me, Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand.  
(vi) Revelation 22: 18 - For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book, If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues that are written in this book:  
(vii) Revelation 22: 19 - And if any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life, and out of the holy city, and from the things which are written in this book.  
  
So, seven times in Revelation we are told it is about "prophecy"; but first and the last time we are told it is "this prophecy". And when looking into such things are an "allegory" (Galatians 4:24), we often find allegorical things in the Bible mentioned as [this/that](CurrentEvents.md), in the fashion of a [twain shew](TwainShew.md) about before/after, old/new, first/last, law/grace, fear/love, great/greater, good/better, etc. So we can safely acknowledge "this" prophecy is not from ["that" God](ShewBread5.md#IAMTHAT), nor ["that" Jesus Christ](GodShew3.md#Second), nor from ["that" Spirit](TwainShew6.md#Spirits) of 2Cor 3:17 by whom we are all changed [from (this) glory to (that) glory](TwainShew3.md#Glorys). That glory, the glory of God's grace, which isn't right of letter/spirit of prophecy as many suppose, but higher of high/higher is harmless; Not prophecy (law), but prophesy (grace). For prophecy speaks of both comfort and discomfort, but prophesy speaks of edification and exhortation unto comfort only.  
  
Of this/that types of prophecy, "this prophecy" is the letter (law) killeth. Such [prophecies shall fail](ShewBread4.md#psf), since "the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy": Revelation 19:10, and we're told the letter killeth, but the spirit giveth life. But until we get beyond what's right of center/right, the life given by Jesus' spirit of prophecy can still be life of life + death. So we go on, from letter/spirit of prophecy to prophesy edifies up-right and exhorts higher unto comfort only. For neither this prophecy nor that prophecy is safe or harmless, but harmful. Only what's higher of high/higher is harmless. So even our new high priest, of old/new high priests, went higher to become harmless.  
  
In the Bible we have [this/that gospels](TwainShew3.md#Gospels); "this" gospel of many deceivers in Matthew 24, "that" gospel in Galatians 2; and preaching any other gospel than "that" results in being "accursed" (Galatians 1), since grace + law is as blessed + cursed = accursed. So it is not far fetched to consider the same allegorical "this" prophecy of this/that also produces what ["this" gospel](CurrentEvents.md) of this/that does: the "beginning of sorrows" (laws). On the contrary that of this/that produces the end of sorrows (laws). For example Christ of Jesus Christ is noted as "the end of the law". And if "[law worketh wrath](lawphoto.md)", then such is allegorically Christ: the end of wrath. Indeed, when looking at what's provided and appointed for them/us, it says God hath not appointed "us" to wrath (law). And perhaps that is why there is an ever increasing cry for [just us](ShewBread2.md#justus), and the Lord (now "that" Spirit) is longsuffering to "[us-ward](Usward.md)"; the reason being so that: none perish by grace, instead of this: all perish by law.
