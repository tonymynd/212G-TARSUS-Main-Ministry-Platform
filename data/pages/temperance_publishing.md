# **Chapter 10: Temperance**
[[#Contents]]

TEMPERANCE
9th Fruit of the Spirit (8 more before the greatest)

Love→Joy→Peace→Longsuffering→Gentleness→Goodness→Faith→Meekness→Temperance (End of Walk) → Diligence

For years, I was taught that temperance in Gal 5:22, was merely about fleshy self-control—like quitting smoking or curbing appetite. But what about the hidden things in unperfected mind? Judging others, trying to be right while making others wrong, guilt-tripping, lying, being full of wrath, warring with one another, and such like? CLEARLY, Spiritual Temperance is much higher than OT "discipline". 

---

We’ve now finally made it through the Big “Walk in the Spirit”, being “led of the Spirit”, notably to be “not under the law” in Galatians 5, through “fruit of the Spirit”, from Love→to→Temperance. For in Gal 5:22,23 KJV when we finally achieve Temperance, then it says: “against such there is no law”. Yet that is not the end of Galatians, nor the end of Pauline & General epistles to learn from.

So, what can we now learn about Temperance?

Since it’s only mentioned thrice, in lists, we’ll have to go fishing for info.

A definition of Temperance, in Strong’s Greek Concordance, is ‘egkrateia’ (feminine noun) generally meaning self-control: virtue of one who masters his desires and passions, especially his sensual appetites. So it’s about mastery of fleshy (legalistic) soulish desires, as if by upgrading from soul mode to spirit mode of soul/spirit.

The noun Temperance is akin to the verb Temperate: ‘egkrateuomai’ meaning exhibiting self-government, as being continent and more stable in one’s conduct.

Both Temperance and Temperate seem allegory related to being spiritually Sober, aka allegory as if no longer drunken with law, but having grown in grace and in truth to the point of no longer needing laws to govern one’s behaviour.

Temperance, Temperate, Spiritual Sobriety also seem allegory related to having a “sound mind” (self-control, self-governmnet), notably 3rd of 3 things God given to us (in contrast to fear) noted in 2Timothy 1:7 KJV’s “God hath not given us the spirit of fear, but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind”.

It seems such is also the theme to Galatians (bewitched churches soon removed from the grace of Christ = the end of the law), due to their fleshy lust for laws not [yet] being crucified. So following 9th fruit of the Spirit in Gal 5:23,24 is “against such there is not law”. “And they that are Christ’s have crucified the flesh with the affections and lusts”…

Seems this lust for laws is common among soulish churches today, which tend to constantly draw people BACK to the cross, and as if keep them captive there, rather than going on from the cross to what’s also beyond the cross, aka not only first reconciled to a death, but also further more saved by his (God’s eterna) life, as noted in Romans 5:10’s A & B, where 5:10B is called “much more”: “For if, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life”. Then, after 4 mentions of grace being “much more”, Rom 5:21 concludes grace reigns THROUGH righteousness UNTO eternal life by JC our Lord.

Works of the flesh, to overcome by being led of the Spirit, from under the law to under grace, are first listed in Galatians 5:19-21. Then a “But” to reveal contrary things (flesh vs Spirit) are being contrasted. So let’s quickly re-read Galatians 5:19-24 KJV:

“Now the works of the flesh are manifest, which are [these]; Adultery, fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness, Idolatry, witchcraft, hatred, variance, emulations, wrath, strife, seditions, heresies, Envyings, murders, drunkenness, revellings, and such like: of the which I tell you before, as I have also told [you] in time past, that they which do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God. But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, Meekness, temperance: against such there is no law. And they that are Christ's have crucified the flesh with the affections and lusts”.

Temperance (noun) is 1st mentioned in Acts 24:25, following mention of righteousness; And last mentioned in 2Peter 1:5,6 following mention of knowledge(‘gnosis’) leading to patience after temperance. So it reasons it takes knowledge of righteousness to bring about temperance also having patience (stedfast endurance). So in our introduction chapter we noted two lists for Temperance: Gal 5:22,23 & 2Peter 1:5-10, where after attaining Temperance as 9th fruit, we then also add Temperance to our faith as 3rd of 7 upgrades to our faith, since our faith only saves the soul of soul/spirit, noted in 1Peter 1.

So as Paul says, in Gal 2:20, upon being crucified with Christ it’s then no longer he that lives, but Christ (the end of the law) lives in him, and the life he lives in the flesh is by the faith of* the Son of God (aka no longer his own faith). That faith doesn’t waver.

*KJV uses faith “of” the Son of God, not faith in the Son of God.

Temperate (verb) occurs thrice, firstly in 1Corinthians 9:25 where Paul says: “every man that stiveth for the mastery (in they/we contesting) is temperate in all things“.

Then secondly in Titus 1:7-9 where Paul says: “For a bishop must be blameless, as the steward of God; not selfwilled, not soon angry, not given to wine, no striker, not given to filthy lucre; But a lover of hospitality, a lover of good men, sober, just, holy, temperate; Holding fast the faithful word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to convince the gainsayers“.

Thirdly in Titus 2:1,2 “But speak thou the things which become sound doctrine: That the aged men be sober, grave, temperate, sound in faith, in charity, in patience“.

In Titus 2:12 it seems to allude to temperance as if thereby teaching us to say NO to worldly lusts (perhaps as if grace saying no to laws that cause evil concupiscence Rom 7:7;8).

So also having a “sound mind” is another kind of self-control, via renewal of the mind, from under the law to under grace, similar to the Walk in the Spirit from Love to Temperance.

It’s not something that happens overnight, but takes some time to be not conformed to this world, and to consistently say no to worldly desires and lusts of the flesh. For without some form of self-control we’d likely lose any fight of faith in resisting the d-evil, which is still adversarial, even if called “the good fight of faith”, until we grow in grace beyond being adversarial. Renewal of the mind helps us to let go of fear; For God hath not given us the spirit of fear, but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind(self-control).

Renewal of the mind, to have a sound mind (aka the mind of Christ = the end of the law), notably makes us then able to prove all three aspects of the will of God, as noted in Romans 12:2 A & B; 12:2A being not conformed to this world, but transformed by renewing of your mind, and 12:2B being then able to prove all 3 (not just 2) aspects of the will of God: “good, and acceptable, and perfect”. For in Mt 5:48 JC did not just say be good, nor even be acceptable, but said be “perfect” as your Father in heaven is perfect. This perfect(teleios) in Mt 5:48 is the same as the perfect in Rom 12:2; Yet notably not the same perfect(katartizo) noted in 1Pet 5:10 = more perfect by “the God of all grace”.
