## Content

Bible Mystery: 
Something to notice about "The Gospel According to John":
John is the only "Verily, verily" account of 4 gospel accounts.
Mt, Mk, and Lk all say "Verily"; but John says "Verily, verily".
"Verily, verily" means "Amen, amen", as if "double minded".
James 1:8 clarifies double minded is "unstable" both "ways".
"Amen, amen", in Numbers 5:22, is unto Blessed and cursed.
Galatians 1:8,9 clarify result of Blessed + cursed = "accursed".
So James 3:10 says Bless + curse ought not come out mouth.
Such "Verily, verily" is like "Master, master"; As if "two masters".
No man, Nor servant, can serve "two masters": Law & Grace.
Mt 23:8,10 clarifies "one" is your "Master": "Christ": Grace.
So it's "Grace to you" when "the gospel of Christ": Rom's 1.
Law & Grace are so contrary they cannot co-exist in peace.
So it's "Grace to you, and peace" from God our Father: Rom 1:7;
For "God is not [author] of confusion, but of peace": 1Cor 14:33.
Luke 12:51 notes two Sons Jesus said "Nay" to peace.
John thrice notes there was "division" because of him.
Let's keep such in mind when reading what John wrote.
