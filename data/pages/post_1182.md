## Content

Conditional vs Unconditional. When contrast Law vs Grace, not mix contrary = oxymoron, it's as if 2 kinds of "goodness": 
- "goodness" of Law as God: Romans 11:21,22, is conditional "goodness"('chrestotes') and "severity" attached. By Law if not always good, then it becomes "severity" = "spare not". Ouch.
- "goodness" of Grace as God is a different "goodness"('agathosune'), like 5th fruit of Spirit: Galatians 5:22,23, is neither 
conditional nor has any severity attached. Also...
Although "goodness" occurs 9 times in NT, it's in a 5/4 ratio of firstly 5 'chrestotes' goodness, then 4 'agathosune' goodness. 
Coincidence about this, is although there's 9 fruit of the Spirit, unconditional 'agathosune' goodness doesn't occur until 5th fruit, after midst of 9. It's also like 5/4. So even though first fruit of the Spirit is Love, it seems to take get beyond midst of 9 fruit to 5th before it becomes unconditional grace goodness.
