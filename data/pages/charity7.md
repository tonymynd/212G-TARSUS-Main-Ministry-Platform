Such is biblical charity which notably profits withal, rather than non profit law charities such as save the children fund, which collects about 12 million dollars a month, but hasn't saved one child. On the contrary the news reports that natural hunger world wide has never been greater, especially among children, and spiritual hunger among children is not even being addressed by such a charity. Furthermore the end of the commandment: you, is charity out of pure heart and "a good conscience", so a conscience that is [purged of sin](Hebrews10.md), speaks of being purged of law (imputes sin). For shedding of blood (sacrifice, which is of the law) does not purge all things, only "[almost all things](Hebrews10.md)". So if basing your salvation on sacrifice, even better sacrifice, your conscience will never be fully purged, because sacrifice is of the law, which is why God will not have sacrifice, but mercy. When studied out, we find God never ever desired nor ever took any pleasure in sacrifice; And the will of God to be done on earth prior to receiving the promise is "I will have mercy, and not sacrifice". Furthermore the end of the commandment: you, is charity out of pure heart and a good conscience and "faith unfeigned", so grace unfeigned, or pure wisdom from above without hypocrisy. Heaven wants to know if you really got it: understanding or whether you are just feigning it; so there will be a test of endurance to see if you can endure to the end (of law) once and for all, not some only.  
  
21. The test only charity can pass: holiness with sobriety (1Tim 2:15). Salvation soundness via Pauline constructive prophesy is followed by a test only charity "never fails" can pass: the holiness with sobriety test.  It is said the woman (church) who was deceived, being in the transgression, shall be saved in childbearing if she and they have and continue in three things:  
   (i)  faith (the law is not of faith)  
   (ii) charity (for only charity never fails a test)  
   (iii) holiness with sobriety (grace without another drink of deadly law)  
So, obviously those who go with love in the debate of love or charity, shall surely fail the holiness with sobriety test since the law to love is a "law" and holiness with sobriety is about grace without another drink of deadly law ever.  
  

[Converted Paul](PaulTheApostle.md) [The Apostle](Paul.md) says:  
"I do not [frustrate](RevelatorySermons24.md) (law) the grace of God".  

  
He notes adding "law worketh wrath" to "grace is sufficient" is an oxymoron with a [latter end worse](LatterEnd.md) like life plus death added is a dead end, for law makes grace "no more grace", as death makes life no more eternal. And when it's no more grace, rather a perverted mixture of contrary things like [mercy and sacrifice](ShewBread5.md#willx2), then grace cannot be of [force](Hebrews10.md) to give eternal life and immortality. For to mix law and grace is to mix mortal and immortal. And since grace "is" and law "added", the only plausible combination is grace + law(added) = Immortal + Mortal(added). So as Paul notes in 1Corinthians 15, biblical order is natural first, [spiritual afterward](HowbeitAfterward.md): law first, grace after (John 1:17); And [mortal law](ShewBread9.md#Expired) [must first die](RevelatorySermons1.md) for the body of Christ to be raised ([graced](GodShew2.md#Allness)) immortal and joined with the head of the body as one: "he that is joined to the Lord is one Spirit(God)". So as Paul notes the head of every [man](TwainShew.md#Blesseds) ([adult](GodShew.md#nomorechildren)) is [Christ](Reconciliation.md) (the end of the law), and his head is God (grace), in which things are an [allegory](Allegory.md) with a moral: "grace is sufficient" (no law required). And such things were written aforetime for our "[learning](OneLiners.md#BibleTranslators)": Romans 15:4, as a mystery to solve in time, not in eternity. For there is neither mystery nor time for it in eternity.  
  
22. Converted Peter says have fervent charity (1Pet 4:8). Fervent charity speaks of intense spiritual growth: purity and maturity with intense zeal. So we're talking intense earnest fulness of Christ: "the end of the law" to the max; which dovetails with Paul's mention law is ready to vanish and shall vanish. For as far as God and Son are concerned the law is not only nailed for being enmity, liar, thief, accuser of the brethren, ministration of condemnation, and ministration of death; But being blotted out to the point of vanish. In short, [law](Law.md) is against [us](Usward.md). Our [hallowed name](ShewBread6.md#Hallowed) is [grace](GraceUntoYou.md).  
  
23. Greet ye one another with a [(holy) kiss of charity](HolyKiss.md) (1Pet 5:14). Converted [Paul](Paul.md) uses the term "[holy kiss](ShewBread4.md#HK)" for his Pauline epistle greeting to "brethren" of "Grace unto you" from "God our Father". Converted Peter notes his epistles written to strengthen brethren after his conversion (by reading Pauline epistles) say the same thing all the epistles of brother Paul. Yet for clarity Peter says: "Grace unto you and (then) peace by multiplied". So his "grow in grace" is as saying grow in God, "the God of all grace" (no law at all). Paul also salutes with JC grace, is with you all, against none of you.  
  
An holy kiss of [charity](GMP/Notes/GodShew/Charity.md) greeting to brethren all has nothing to do with ass kissing anyone above you on some ladder to heaven that dead ends short of entering, lest they shit on you from above; Nor anything to do with kissing the ring of anyone such as the Pope; Nor even a physical kiss. It's an allegoric kiss that is written or spoken to edify > exhort > comfort fellow brethren all with only [grace > mercy > peace](TheShew.md#Chapter1) "from" God. Yet peace "with" God is notably [through](through.md) Jesus > Christ "our" Lord, which is to say we all have to have the same Lord (Jesus > Christ: the only foundation laid which is [the same yesterday, to day, and for ever](ShewBread.md#contraryto)) in order to have [peace with God](GodShew5.md). So Pauline Epistles (including Hebrews and Revelation), and the Holy Bible containing Old and New Testaments end with:  
  

The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ with you all. Amen.
