## 1: How and why philadephia is after godliness
We can say that brotherly kindness (philadelphia) succeeds godliness (eusebeia), since philadelphia already carries a closely proximal meaning embedded in compassion and PITY (eusplagchnos), as seen in Ephesians 4:32—"be ye kind one to another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another..."—and 1 Peter 3:8—"Finally, be ye all of one mind, having compassion one of another, love as brethren, be pitiful (eusplagchnos)." This is a quality also related to godliness, which involves “PITY toward God.” This demonstrates that philadelphia is higher than godliness in its outward expression, as godliness reflects the reception of God’s character (a vertical reception—to be God-like, since such kingdom of God is WITH you and IN you [Luke 17:21], but not yet the horizontal love toward the brethren, philadelphia).

Therefore, we can infer that once we have acquired godliness, we are then equipped to extend philadelphia—a love that embraces all brethren without partiality, in alignment with the character of God (James 3:17).

## 2. add virtues to "put on" re philadephia
When speaking of brotherly love 'philadephia' became aware how "bowels of mercies" is listed among virtues to "put on," alongside kindness and humbleness of mind, which are mentioned before "forbearing one another" and "forgiving one another" in Colossians 3:12-14, something to consider when building study.

## 3. ⬇️ _Requires a tender heart to move outward_

**2. Pity (Eusplagchnos)**
- _Tenderhearted_, moved with inward compassion.
- Seen in Ephesians 4:32 and 1 Peter 3:8 as a key virtue in loving others.
- Emotional sensitivity that enables relational grace.
## 4. Brotherly Kindness (Philadelphia)
- Love rooted in shared identity as brother from the same Father.
- Extends mercy relationally.
- Expresses God's character **to others**—horizontally.
## 5. Kindness of charity, higher than brotherly kindness*
\* (why moving to charity and avoid getting stuck in philadephia)
The sources clarify that while brotherly kindness is good, "the kindness of charity is only found among grace us folk" and is "merciful, to all, not both merciful + merciless to some," suggesting that brotherly kindness itself may not inherently be entirely "without partiality" like charity is
