**ADVENTURES of Simon, son of Jonas, called Peter, Satan, Cephas.**

- in four gospels he's 1 of 12 chosen from many, in top 3 at transfiguration; Yet also called Satan and told he'd later get 'epistrepho' converted; For he denied Jesus thrice & went back fishing for fish instead of fishing for men.

- in Acts, after seeing Jesus ascend to the clouds he organizes an election whereby Matthias replaces Judas, so the upbraided eleven could be a new dirty dozen, upon whom came the Holy Ghost. Then he's a Holy Ghost preacher who does miraculous things; Yet he was not at all merciful to Ananias & Saphira, and still dissimulated at where they were first called Christians in Acts 11. His dissimulation even carried away Joses from (surnamed Barnabas), who tried to be mediator between Peter & Saul (later called Paul in Acts 13).

So it reasons Peter is still not yet 'epistrepho' converted,

 not even when they were first called Christians. So

God chooses Saul to become Paul: his witness unto all men (including Peter).

- in Acts 12 Peter's bound and in jail during Easter (a day that can seem like a 1000 yrs), but loosed (like Satan bound and loosed in Revelation), and although details are scarce, Peter seems to go underground (as an escaped fugitive of the Roman Empire) using the name Cephas, perhaps later surfacing in Italy as first Pope (according to Roman Catholic Chruch), whose bishop, John, seems to bewitch churches Apostle Paul established.

So it reasons Peter is still not yet 'epistrepho' converted

 as Jesus foretold he would be in Luke 22:32. So

nothing is written about Peter from Romans to Hebrews

except five mentions in Galatians, none being flattering.

- however in the general epistles of Peter we find change, evidence he'd read all Paul's epistles, and he then seems to say similar things as Paul. Eg: Then, more like Paul, he's a JC writing to CJs, saying the Paul things like "Grace unto you, and peace"; Also love one another with a "pure heart", and greet brethren with a holy kiss of "charity" (grace agape that doesn't wax cold, nor ever fail); And do it fervently (hot zeal, not feigned).

So mention of CJ, which began in Acts, ends in 1Peter 5,

where it notes the God of all grace make you perfect after ye suffer awhile

(perhaps b/c Paul had noted in 2Timothy 3:12 that all who will live godly in CJ shall suffer).

- in 2Peter3 he even uses allegory, like Paul did, saying be not ignorant one day is as a thousand years and a thousand years as one day, almost as if he'd read Revelation (perhaps also authored by Paul, if it has Paul's token at the end, which is always hand signed by Paul to signify it)

So then, it reasons Peter, called Satan, did get 'epistrepho' converted;

But before such was like a dog returned to his own vomit. Interesting stuff.

Peter's last words:

"grow in grace, and [in] the knowledge of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ.

To him [be] glory both now and for ever. Amen".
