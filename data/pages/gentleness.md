My pondering re Midst first reminded me of what’s in midst of Genesis 1:1, which is a Menorah Seven in the Hebrew language, read left←right, reverse of English. Midst of Seven is 1 thing called: eht being 2 things: alaph/tav re a PluralGod called ‘elohyim:

So, perhaps this _**Gentleness**_ being the **midst** of 9 has some significance we may get enlightened about.  Let’s study it; then also discuss it:

There’s only two mentions of “gentleness” in NT. Such are different in Greek:

-        Gentleness1: 2Cor 10:1 “_Now I Paul myself beseech you by the meekness and_ _gentleness_ (‘epieikeia’) _of Christ, who in presence_ [am] _base among you, but being absent am bold toward you_: …”

-        Gentleness2: Gal 5:22 “_But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering,_ _gentleness_ (‘chrestotes’),_goodness, faith_, …” 

So, in English both are gentleness, but in Greek as different as ‘epieikeia’ & ‘chrestotes’. The ‘epieikeia’ gentleness, of Christ, is first rendered as requested “clemency”, by Jewish orator Tertullus, from Roman governor Felix, in Acts 24:4, where Paul is on trial, and the governor Felix is referred to as a Judge who’s hearing both sides of this trial matter. So it’s kinda like Paul is first beseeching Corinthians using this “gentleness” aspect.

Now, also looking at ‘chrestotes’ kind of “gentleness”, it’s also rendered as “goodness”, “kindness”, “good”. Yet it’s notable this “goodness” aspect of this “gentleness” = 5th fruit aspect notably differs from the 6th fruit aspect = “goodness” = ‘agathosyne’ = uprightness of heart and life; Which is yet to be discussed as next chapter. So perhaps Greek renderings, as if inside of and more than English renderings, hereby becomes as if first comparing 2 kinds of “gentleness”, then also as if comparing 2 kinds of “goodness”:

- gentleness1: 2Cor 10: of Christ, was 1st rendered as clemency requested of governor.

- gentleness2: Gal 5: also rendered as goodness, is still only 1st of 2 kinds of “goodness”.

Gentleness2 (midst) = Goodness, but ‘chrestotes’ goodness = 5th is not goodness = 6th.

-        goodness1: ‘chrestotes’ = 5th aspect, now being looked at, as if the midst

-        goodness2: ‘agathosune’ = 6th aspect, yet to be looked at, as beyond midst

love joy peace longsuffering **gentleness2=goodness1** goodness2 faith meekness temperance

Oh boy, now seems as if it’s getting more complicated, rather than simpler, and more like a mystery having lots of twists, rather than a comfy notion of gentleness just being “gentle”; Yet not like gentle1 (‘epios’ = affable) in 1Thess 2:7 & 2Tim2:24, but another gentle2 (‘epieikes’ = patiently equitable) in Titus  3:2 (to all men) & in James 3:17 (as 2nd of 7 apsects of wisdom from above). Oh shit, it’s like we’ve got twain of everything to sort out: twain gentleness x2, twain goodness x2, twain gentle x2, to learn something.

Perhaps it’s why we need to be led of the Spirit, unto spiritual aspects of fruit, rather than just use our natural (carnal, flesh) thinking about such things. For even when spiritual, of natural/spiritual=after, there’s still “_comparing spiritual thing__s_ _with spiritual_”, after the after. When spirit mode of soul/spirit, there’s still “_spirit of error_” & “_spirit of truth_” to contrast. Order of twain **important**, for 1John 4:6’s contrast order of truth←&→error ends error; Like Jesus’ and/or Mt 12:30-37 twains all end as bad as saved←or→damned.

So what can we learn about this gentleness2, of twain, being as if goodness1, of twain?

Goodness1: as the ‘chrestotes’ of Gentleness2, in midst of 9 fruit:

-        Romans 2:4’s goodness (‘chrestotes’: goodness1 of gentleness2), of goodness, forbearance, longsuffering of God, notes goodness of God leadeth to repentance. It’s said to ‘anthropos’ man (“liar” in Rom 3:4). It’s about how such a man who judges others (eg homosexuals & lesbians) is also as “inexcusable” as them who are “without excuse”. Judging them is not how to escape the judgment of a law God who has no accuse-others and excuse-me partiality. So repentance is needed.

-         Romans 11:22’s goodness  (‘chrestotes’: goodness1 of gentleness2) is goodness of a God who spared not; So says behold (both) goodness and severity of (this God), who’s goodness toward thee is conditional upon thee continuing in such goodness, lest also be not spared, described as cut off=hewn down.

Such goodness of a God who “_spared not_” is not [yet] your Father is “_merciful_”. OMG.

So, perhaps it’s why we should ponder about this gentleness (goodness) being the midst of 9. For it seems to be about a Plural God having both goodness←&→severity, even potential severity after goodness; Kinda like Mt 18:23-35’s forgiven←&→unforgiven. It’s like the order is reversed. If both, should be severity first, goodness after. It’s beginning to seem like Law, being both good←&→evil, ends with _evil_: reverse of _live_.

Seems we’re looking at gentleness that’s also something other than gentle, thereby goodness that’s something other than good. Perhaps if we now also look at this gentleness2 (chrestotes) = goodness1 (chrestotes) as kindness (chrestotes), we`ll learn more.

Kindness (chrestotes) x4: as gentleness2`s goodness1 (how weird is this?)

1- 2Cor 6:6’s _kindness_ is listed among things like ‘gnosis’ knowledge (not epignosis) on one side and Holy Ghost (not Spirit) on other side, midst sandwiched between pureness→kindness←love unfeigned; So it may not be either, yet as if in midst of both; Kinda like CJ a Mr In-Between as a mediator between God←&→men in 1Timothy 2:5.

2Cor 6:6 “_By pureness, by knowledge, by longsuffering, by_ _kindness__, by the Holy Ghost, by love unfeigned_,”

2- Eph 2:7’s _kindness_ is equally as strange, speaking of such kindness toward us (reverse of them), to be shewn in _ages to come_ through _Christ Jesus_ (reverse of Jesus Christ)… 2:10 then saying we (not them) got created (not made) in Christ Jesus (them), which this God BEFORE ordained that we should walk in them (not yet us). How weird is this? …

For even Plural God in Gen 1 never said let us create(bara) man, but let us make(asah) man; Yet then created man in his image (reverse of likeness)… as if first play out a  reverse scenario: “_in our image_” as if reverse before of before/after, after being “_after our likeness_”. Perhaps our likeness should be _Jesus Christ_, not _Christ Jesus_. Seems why Paul turns CJ around to JC in Hebrews. For JC is NT foundation to build upon: grace & truth. CJ as if reverse: law & lie. So in 1Pet 5:10’s before/after, CJ is before, as if created of created/made, thereby imperfect. After _made perfect_ is by _the God of all grace_.

3- Col 3:12’s _kindness_ is low on a list of upgrades, only 2nd of 7 topped off with charity. Previously 3:10 noted putting on the new man, of old/new, is as being renewed in the ‘epignosis’ knowledge, to be changed from the former corrupt state of the old man. Col 3:12 also mentions _the elect_, and false Christs arise to seduce even _the elect_ if possible. So the upgrades listed are to take _the elect_ above the potential of being seduced on high. So it’s kinda like getting above(epi: higher) the above(ano: high), as if it’s after the after: After spirit of soul/spirit, also try the spirits: compare spirit of error & spirit of truth, and after spiritual of natural/spiritual, also compare spiritual things with spiritual. For law is both natural & spiritual, but when spiritual it can be _spiritual wickedness_ in high places.

So Paul’s writings say:

Be not highminded←_**mind not high thing**__**s**_→be not highminded

This is quite an interesting and somewhat complicated study of gentleness in midst of 9.
