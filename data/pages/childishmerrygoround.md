And in Lk 6:36 it clarifies "be ye merciful ([all mercy and no sacrifice at all](ShewBread5.md#willx2)) as your Father in heaven is merciful".  
  
What never faileth is allegorically neither of [two children](TwainShew2.md#Childrens) in Rom 9, but rather "be no more children" in Eph 4, since even children of God are oft tempted and tossed to and fro, but above such is what cannot be moved because it is totally aware of all the wiles of devilish law law. What never faileth is also neither of [two servants](TwainShew6.md#Servants) in Rom 6, but rather "thou art no more servant" in Gal 4, since even servants of righteousness seem to unaware Christ is the end of the law "for righteousness". What never faileth is also neither of [two laws](TwainShew4.md#Laws) in Rom 7 & 8, but rather "ye are not under the law" in Gal 3, since even the better sort of two laws gives place to law worketh wrath and produces all manner of [concupiscence](Concupiscence.md).  
  
So we need to leave the childish merry go round of law law, of children tossed to and fro, for grace that is [pure](RevelatorySermons7.md), which is not even grace of law/grace, for such still gives place to law, which is as giving place to the devil. The [higher](RevelatorySermons21.md) exhortation is "neither give place to the devil". So even those who say they are grace of law/grace usually find themselves in trouble sooner or later, especially those still hung up on the cross (cursed every one that hangeth on a tree) rather than going on through it to risen and seated above the law. Many so called grace ministers are also still hung up on sacrifice, which is "of the law" (Heb 10). So what never fails is God's will to have mercy, and not sacrifice; neither of [two sorts of sacrifice](TwainShew6.md#Sacrifices). For even the better sort gives place to law. So His Grace will not have it, as is clarified in God's will: "[I will have mercy, and not sacrifice](ShewBread5.md#willx2)". So the end is chuck full of allegoric [Pauline clarity](GMP/Notes/GodShew/Charity.md#Pauline), even unto the last amen being a singular "Amen" to JC grace with you all (against none of you); And the end which God declared from the beginning is notably also void of law, no law law at all in His grace.  
  

The "[grace](ShewBread6.md#Hallowed)" of "[our](ShewBread2.md#justus)" Lord "[Jesus Christ](ShewBread.md#contraryto)" with "[you](YeYou.md)" => "[all](RevelatorySermons6.md)". [Amen](TwainShew.md#Amens).
