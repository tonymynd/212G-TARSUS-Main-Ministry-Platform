## Content

1Corinthians 15:22's "as in Adam", then "all" x2, asks ths question:
"For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive"? 
Further-more... 
Paul's "allegory" meaning of such question being CHOOSE: 
- 
"as in Adam" (as in law = sin condition)  all die = extinction
OR (not and) 
- "in Christ" (in grace = no sin condition) all live = salvation.
BUT you can't have two all's, not even if a before/after.
FOR have two all's is double minded for "all"; So then,
it's a law OR grace choice, aka pick only one for "all".
Then for a further mind SHOCKER:
Taking this info to 1Cor 15:45-47, "as in Adam all die" then includes BOTH first man "Adam" (living soul) & last "Adam" (quickening spirit). 
POINT: 
Neither first "Adam" Nor last "Adam" are [yet] the true "Christ".
For
if you do further greek study it gets more MIND-BLOWING:
last Adam = second man='anthropos'="liar" in Romans 3:4.
So then,
Even when spirit mode (of soul/spirit)  there's still this to do:
Comparing two kinds of spirit: spirit of error vs spirit of truth.
So Paul says
even when spiritual, of natural first, spiritual afterward, still this: "comparing spiritual things(plural) with spiritual(singular)".
Interesting mystery we're solving, by sorting out all the twists.
