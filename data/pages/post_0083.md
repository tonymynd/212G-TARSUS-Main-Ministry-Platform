## Content

Bible Mystery: Format of Apostle Paul's Epistles:
- Greeting: Grace & Peace to you from God & JC.
-- Discussion: comparison & contrast of law/grace.
- Salutation: The grace of JC [be] with you all. Amen.
The 
(Romans...Philemon) Pauline Epistle Format seems to:
- open and close with JC, like the NT also does in the KJV*
- sandwich discussion between grace greet & grace salute
- quarantine mention of law as if looking at a deadly virus
- use both CJ & JC, but clarify Paul's a JC writing unto CJs
- use "the" Lord JC at the begin, but "our" Lord JC at the end
- use dialectic logic to come to a conclusion from discussion
- have the same conclusion as the NT ending in Revelation
*modern Bible versions remove "Christ" from their ending
When considering all the writings of the NT writers,
and considering Mark is called the surname of John,
it then seems like Paul is in the midst of these seven:
Matthew, Luke, John→ PAUL ←James, Peter, Jude;
And Paul writes more NT than all six others combined,
especially if Paul may have also written the Revelation,
ends with Paul's hand written "token" in every epistle.
In the midst of 13 epistles having this interesting format
Rom,1&2Cor,Gal,Eph,Phil,COL,1&2Thes,1&2Tim,Tit,Phil
is an epistle that should also be read by the Laodiceans,
which happens to be the lukewarm church of 7 in Rev.
Interesting stuff to ponder, especially when
doing the end run of a human race along with patience,
is still a race to run, and at the much more grace pace.
