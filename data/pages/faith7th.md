\**Chapter 8**

**Faith: 7th Fruit of the Spirit**

Love→Joy→Peace→Longsuffering→Gentleness→Goodness→Faith = 7th fruit of Spirit.

Faith is a HUGE topic. The word “faith” occurs 247 times, yet only twice in OT. So it’s primarily a NT topic, now our 7th fruit of the Spirit topic to study, ponder, learn about.

NT “faith” in Greek is always ‘pisits’, except when called “little faith”, which may be allegory for law faith, or childish belief. The word “faith” (‘pistis’: feminine noun) is described as a conviction of the truth of anything: belief; Also English rendered as “assurance” x1,  “belief” x1, “fidelity” x1, as if continually assuring us of our salvation through sanctification of the Spirit unto belief of the truth, which involves let go of lies we may have firstly erroneously believed to be true, such as a childish Boogie Man.

So beyond “_belief of the truth_” is “_know the truth_”, as an upgrade from believe to know.

But first, let’s Review:

As we’ve been _Led of the Spirit_, through _Fruit of the Spirit_, beginning with _Love_ being the “_commandment_” (rule, ordinance, law) to _Love_ _one another_; We’ve now seen there was a notable goodness1/goodness2 difference from 5th(midst) & to 6th fruit,

Perhaps(?) a 5/4 ratio change (like ‘chrestotes/agathosune’ 5/4 ratio of NT “goodness”):

- FROM these 5: _Love, Joy, Peace, Longsuffering, Gentleness_(goodness1)=midst

- TO-WARD these 4: _Goodness, Faith, Meekness Temperance_,

Perhaps(?) as if a transition per se, maybe allegory as if the Spirit has been leading us  forward: From _under the law_ →To→ _under grace_ has _better things_, as if upgrading. For Galatians 5:18 KJV had said “_if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law_”; And Romans 6:14/15 had said _ye/we_ _are not under the law, but under grace_. Seems, our Walk in the Spirit maybe as if From needed governance / To less needed governance, b/c  we’re being fed spiritual food we can digest & apply, by what we’ve been learning.

So, as we’ve seen in the previous chapter, there was an interesting NT 5/4 ratio of goodness1/goodness2, which may(?) even allegory suggest there’s a 5/4 ratio of 9 fruit.  For 5th=midst _gentleness_ fruit still notably had both “_goodness and severity_”; And not as contrasting goodness or severity, but as if commanding Love’s goodness1 being conditional on u be good, else severity = cut off. So 5th_gentleness_ fruit aspect was still kinda like law = _both good and evil_, by still having both _goodness and severity_.

So it seems first 5 fruit aspects may(?) have been allegory portraying still under the law, till past midst. Then 6th as goodness2 was as _goodness_ only; Perhaps allegory as if going on From under law / To under grace as if go on to _better things_ God provided _for us_, of them/us; Or as if going From left hand(side) / To right hand(side) of plural “hands” of a PluralGod on high. Something to ponder as we now go on to our discussion about _faith_.

Re faith:

So now, when look at, study about “_faith_” fruit, it may become as if Paul’s allegoric faith upgrade: “_from_ _faith /_ _to_ _faith_” in Romans 1:16,17; Where Paul first notes his gospel (of Grace & peace) is now “_the gospel of Christ_” (as if gone through Jesus→Christ), notably a gospel in which he’s “_not ashamed_”.

Why?

First he 1:16 says it’s the power (dunamis) of God unto salvation, to the (singular) Jew first, and also to the Greek; Then also 1:17 says: For “_therein_ (the gospel of Christ) _is the righteousness of God revealed_ _**from**_ _faith_ _**to**_ _faith_”; Perhaps as if from “_great faith_” under law to greater faith under grace, as if growing in faith, _by grace_, from justified “_**by**_ _faith_” as circumcision were, to justified “_**through faith**_” as uncircumcision are, as noted in Romans 3:29,30, about how one God is both the God of the Jews _**by**_ _faith_ & the God of the Gentiles _**through**_ _faith_.

Clever wording.

But what does it mean?

Seems Paul suggests _**by**_ _faith_ is _by promise_, so not of the law, nor by works of the law; And _**through**_ _faith_ is as going or growing from _your faith_ to _the faith of the Son of God_. So in our Introduction Chapter 1 we had 2 lists of upgrades, first 9 fruit of the Spirit in Gal 5:22,23, then also 7 things to “_add to_ _your faith_” in 2Pet 1:5-10; Since in 1Peter 1 it said, re “_your faith_”: it’s only for the _salvation of your soul_, of _soul/spirit_, perhaps as if to suggest there’s also a _spirit_ salvation to go on unto, beyond salvation of your soul. For even when in _spirit_ mode, of _soul/spirit_, there’s still this to do: “_comparing spiritual things_ (plura) _with spiritual_”. Why? Because law is both _natural_ & _spiritual_, yet also _both good &_ _evil_, so law may also be _natural_ _distress_ & _spiritual_ _wickedness_ afterward.   

Re: their faith:

Seems, on the topic of _faith_, most _faith_ preachers use Hebrews 11, wherein “_**by**_ _faith_” is mentioned 17 times, and “_**through**_ _faith_” also mentioned 5 times; Yet most _faith_ preachers, who say you can’t please their _rewarder_ God without their faith (from Hebrews 11:6), never seem to mention Paul’s conclusions in Hebrews 11:13,39 are:

-        11:13 these all died (1st of 2 lists of OT law faith heroes), “_not having received the promises_”, and

-        11:39 these all (2nd list, also all died), having obtained a good report “_through faith_”, (but yet stil) “_received not the promise_”:

Seems as if Paul’s revealing oops1 & oops2, both by faith & through faith, if still law faith. So Heb 11:40 finally concludes _God provided some better thing for us_ (of them/us), as if something better than all dying and neither receiving the promises, nor the promise.

For Hebrews 10:36 previously talked of “_receive the promise_” after _do the will of God_, which seems to be _I will have mercy, and not sacrifice_, as if to allegory say have _faith_ in grace, not in law. For Gal 3:11 said what’s evident from evidence is that no man justified “_by the law_”, and Heb 10:8 says sacrifice and offering, for sin, is offered “_by the law_”.

So re faith, or rather firstly re what’s “_not of faith_”,

let’s connect these dots in two different verses by Paul:

-        _whatsoever is NOT OF FAITH is sin_: Romans 14:23 KJV,

-        _and the law is NOT OF FAITH_: Galatians 3:12 KJV.

Seems why, in John 5:45 KJV, JC pointed out to law believers that he (as grace & truth) would not accuse them, but one they trusted (aka had faith in), Moses (law giver), does accuse them to the Father. So it’s like revealing law = accuser of brethren, and can’t be trusted. Also seems, by his twice stoop to write on ground, in John 8, where they brought a woman caught in adultery, such awareness of law = accuser, _convicted their conscience_. Interesting to notice “_conscience_” begins in John 8:9, and can be “_evil conscience_” till last mention of “conscience” is only a “_good conscience_” toward God in 1Peter 3:21.

So people in general, whose _faith_ still in law, are more like _am I_ (?), than like _I am_ (!).

Paul’s allegory example of such: “_when_ _**I am**_ (grace) _weak, then_ _**am I**_ (law) _strong_”.

It’s interesting to notice sometimes a verse may say “_am I_” instead of “_I am_”.

Re: faith may fail, if not upgraded:

Seems faith can get tried, perhaps even fail if not upgraded. One example is Peter, in Luke 22:31-34, where he’s prayed for, so his faith will not fail, and foretold he’ll later get a conversion upgrade (from ‘strepho’ _converted_: Mt 18:3) to ‘epistrepho’ _converted_; So then he’ll _strengthen_ (not weaken, nor dissimulate) _brethren_. When Peter implies he’s got faith to go to prison & death, he’s foretold he’ll deny Jesus thrice before the cock crows. Later we see Peter among 7 gone fishing, when things didn’t work out how they’d thought (seems they’d assumed Israel would get restored). Yet even after told to feed the lambs & sheep by resurrected Jesus, we later see Peter killing (not forgiving) Ananias & Sapphira, bringing _great fear_ (not love void of fear, nor grace) on the whole church. Etc. Seems Peter much later reports he got faith upgraded by read all Paul’s epistles in 2Pet 3.

Re our faith:

In Hebrews 12:2, Paul calls “Jesus” the “_author and finisher of [our]_ _**faith**_”. Yet Son of man Jesus fanatics (as I once was) can ASSUME this means _Son of man_ Jesus, by still being UnAware _Son of man_NEVER occurs in epistles of Paul, who cleverly reveals, in Romans 3:4 (Greek mode), to “_let God be true_”, we should let every (‘anthropos’) man be a “liar”. So, _Son of man_=’anthropos’=”_liar_”, as I finally realized I was a liar, when I was still in _Son of man_ Jesus mode. Seems why Paul clarifies, 3 times: “_the just shall live by_ _**faith**_”, yet also clarifies, in Galatians 2:16, he means “_the_ _**faith**_ _of_ _Jesus Christ_” and more specifically “_the_ _**faith**_ _of_ _Christ_”, of through Jesus→Christ, since that Christ is “_the end of the law_”, and because it’s evident “_no man is justified by the law_”.  So, in Gal 2:20, Paul clarifies the _**faith**_ he now lives by, upon being crucified with Christ, is “_the_ _**faith**_ _of the Son of God_” (not Son of man=’anthropos’=”liar”, which I once was). So when looking at Jesus as _author and finisher of our_ _**faith**_, it’s only _Son of God_ Jesus.

So when I finally asked the God who “_upbraideth not_” for His pure wisdom (James 1:5), in my heart it was as if I was finally & humbly asking for His all truth, of _the only true God_, and even if it made me a liar (who still needed to learn much more of His truth), which it kinda did, but not in a condemning way from _that God_ who doesn’t upbraid. That God never accuses nor condemns, but does reveal things that made me go OMG.

Re: unity of the faith: Eph 4:11-15

Looking closer, and in more context, this is interesting to notice: Paul first mentions five kinds of ministries, all as plural: _apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachers_; and as being given for 3 things:

- _for perfecting of the saints_,

- _for the work of the ministry_,

- _for edifying the body of Christ_;

Then says

“_**Till**_ _we all come in the unity of faith_”,

yet only as the first of 3 things:

- _in the unity of faith, AND_

- _of the knowledge_ (‘epignosis’. not ‘gnosis’) _of the Son of God_ (not Son of man),

- _UNTO a perfect_ (teleios: brought to it’s end) _man_ (‘aner’, not ‘anthropos): as the _measure_ of the _stature_ of the “_fulness of Christ_” (as if then graceful, merciful, peacefu). Then Paul clarifies

such is by “_be_ _no more children_”, so then he tells childish Ephesians to _**grow up**_, as if by go on, through Jesus→Christ = end of the law, to be like that Christ, who’s not childish, nor just a man, but a perfect ‘aner’ man, who’s put away his childish things (laws). A few verses later he talks of _put away lying_, as if allegory for put away lawing one another.

So then,

“_unity of the faith_” is important, but only 1/3 of 3 things about the _fulness of Christ_. 

Re faith to remove mountains = “nothing” yet:

In Mt 21:19-21, Jesus law-cursed a fig tree for having no fruit (only leaves) when he was hungry for fruit. When disciples marvelled how the fig tree presently withered away, he both _answered and said to them_: “_verily I say unto you_ (of ye/you), _if ye have faith, and doubt not, ye shall not only do this_ [which is done] _to the fig tree, but also if ye shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed, and be thou cast into the sea; it shall be done_”.   
Sounds like powerful faith stuff, even if it’s not literal, but allegory.

-        seems Israel might be the allegory for a fig tree he cursed for not having fruit, even though Mk 11:11-14 notes it wasn’t even time for figs; so it’s allegory as if  he was saying no man should eat the law of Israel hereafter for ever

-        seems he didn’t specify a mountain to be removed, but was on Mount of Olives journeying from Bethany to Jerusalem; Yet since he said “also” for such, perhaps allegory meant also say to their law (has a curse) go jump in the dead sea

Yet, also re faith to “remove mountains” (plura):

After Paul mentioning a “more excellent way” in 1Cor 12:31, he then notes in 1Cor 13:2,  even if I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries, and have all (‘gnosis’) knowledge, and have _all faith, so that I could remove mountains_, and have not charity (of these three: faith, hope, charity: 1Cor 13:13 KJV), I am (still as if) nothing [yet].

WOW.

Seems Paul is allegory saying, if we haven’t yet “_from faith to faith_” upgraded, from faith in law to faith in grace, even the law faith to remove mountains still counts as if nothing yet. Why? For what’s evident from the evidence is that no man is justified by the law.

SO,

Even though 1Cor 13:13 mentions _faith_, of these three abide: _faith__, hope, charity_, he also allegory clarifies if we look at such three as if being great, greater, greatest; Only charity is “_greatest_” of 3. So perhaps, concerning “_great faith_” of law, we should leave such, lest our _hope_ mode become hopeless by law faith instead of hopeful by grace faith.

Re “_shield of faith_”:

When looking closer at the whole “armour of God” in Ephesians 6:13-17 KJV, it’s interesting to then notice this “_shield of faith_” that is “_able to quench all the fiery darts of the wicked_” seems like the only piece of such armour that is to be taken (analambano) “_above_ (epi) _all_”, perhaps as if different, or more than the three armour things mentioned before & two armour things mentioned after the shield of faith. As such, it may cleverly allegorize the upgrade to grace faith we’ve been learning about, to then be, when fully UpGraded, like a pure grace force field of also receive no harm (more than just do no harm); Perhaps as if ”much more” than any protective police shield (badge) & bullet proof vest worn by so-called peace officers, or any hand held swat shield to hide behind.

Perhaps the shield of faith we can/should take above (epi) all other pieces of armour, even higher than ‘ano’ above, may become as if neither of defensive nor offensive, but above both defence & offence of sides wars. For pure grace doesn’t make sides to have sides wars, and doesn’t take either side in any sides war.\
