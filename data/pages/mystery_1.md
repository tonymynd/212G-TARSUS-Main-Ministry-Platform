**Biblical Mystery(s)**

**Introduction**

The word “mystery” (‘mysterion’) occurs 27 times & “mysteries” (‘mysterion’) 5 times, for a total of 32 mentions; And interestingly only occurs in the New Testament.

Even more interesting is by whom it occurs: by Jesus and by Paul; Notably never by John, similar to how “revelation” (‘apokalupsis’: to uncover and reveal) is never used by John. So then, although “mystery” occurs 4 times in the book called Revelation, which many suppose to be written by John, clues lead to Paul being the writer of The Revelation (uncovering) of St John the divine (aka: uncovering a saint named John thought to be divine). For although John’s writings do reveal some pretty important stuff, he tends to leave out the details, thereby leaving readers confused. So Paul, who writes more NT writings than all 7 other NT writers, notes God is not [author] of confusion, but of peace.

So before we begin the topic of “mystery”, let’s notice John’s gospel account uses “Verily, verily”: allegorically akin to “Master, master” = two many masters for a man. For John’s gospel account portrays him as more beloved, but only “according to John”.  It seems why John’s gospel account is sandwiched between Luke & Acts, then followed by 14 grouped epistles of Paul, who clarifies “there is no respect of persons with God”: aka true God’s pure wisdom has no partiality: a constant theme in both OT & NT.

Before we begin the topic of mystery(s), let’s notice Paul TheApostle: his witness unto all men(‘anthropos’=”liars” in Rom 3:4): aka all men(‘anthropos’=liars) whom God our Saviour will have both saved (aka graced) AND come unto the knowledge(‘epignosis’) of the truth: 1Tim 2:4, knowledge which is above and beyond the ‘gnosis’ knowledge that shall “vanish away” in 1Cor 13:8 & Heb 8:13…

Apostle Paul, says, in 1Cor 4:1 KJV: “_Let a man so account of us, as of the ministers of Christ, and **stewards of the mysteries** of God_”. Seems a steward of the mysteries both reveals and conceals, yet the revealing of what’s concealed is ALSO in plain sight, but perhaps only to be seen when spiritual of natural first, spiritual afterward. Hence the topic of born again, of water and of Spirit, firstly for man (of child/man) to SEE the kingdom of God, then also ENTER the kingdom of God.

But the most IMPORTANT thing Paul says about mysteries, is in 1Corinthians 13:1,2,3 KJV, which notably uses “charity” (grace ‘agape’) not “love” (law ‘agape’):

**_13:1_**_Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and have not charity, I am become [as] sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal._

**_13:2_**_And though I have [the gift of] prophecy, and understand all mysteries, and all knowledge; and though I have all faith, so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity, I am nothing._

**_13:3_**_And though I bestow all my goods to feed [the poor], and though I give my body to be burned, and have not charity, it profiteth me nothing._

So, although Solomon the OT wise guy says: “_with all thy getting, get understanding_”, Paul clarifies under-standing is not our goal; For even when “_not under the law, but under grace_”, it’s still ↓“under”↓. For even forgiving one another is still one & another = duality, aka NotYet ONEderful, which is graceful, merciful, peaceful=3rd of 3, not just 2/3 =0.666.
