Fruit of Spirit STUDY continued

15. Peace (Part 1)
Three best wishes unto ALL: Grace, mercy, [and] peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord
love→joy→PEACE→longsuffering
PEACE: 3rd Fruit of the Spirit and 15th aspect of "things above" study (links to the series of study on the comments)
When I first started studying "the fruit of the Spirit", I thought it would be a simple matter of looking up dictionary definitions for each aspect; Yet, as I did more research, the more I found myself returning to them for a more profound understanding. The reason thereby was that as I apply these concepts with simple dictionary understanding, did not have sufficient effect or flat out ineffective, I was missing much interconnectivity with other mysteries in the bible. The same is true for "peace".
As I continued my research about "things above" (Col 3:2) which includes fruit "the fruit of the Spirit", walking through each aspect from love to peace provided beneficial growth, yet left me with unanswered questions and a desire for deeper understanding of their interconnections. My studies led me to examine parallel passages in Galatians 5 that discuss different modes of 'walk' and fruit bearing.
An intriguing distinction emerged between types of "walk" mentioned there. Likewise, realized multiple words in Greek translated as "peace" in the New Testament (AKJV Bible), unveiling layers of meaning.
Walk → Run
WALKing in the Spirit through 17.Love→16.Joy→15.Peace … leads us closer to Temperance = SelfControl = “against such there is no law”: Galatians 5:22→23. YET in Galatians 5 KJV there’s even two kinds of “walk” in Greek mode (neither yet a “run, that ye may obtain”):
“Walk”: ‘peripateo’: Galatians 5:16 = before fruit of the Spirit: as if an adventure Walk
“walk”:  ‘stoicheo’: Galatians 5:25 = after fruit of the Spirit: more like an orderly walk
Interesting how there is a before Walk & after walk, where Gal 5:25 is both, live & walk in the Spirit once in orderly walk mode.
YES, 1Corinthians 14:33 KJV says
“For God is not [the author] of confusion, but of peace 'eirene' ”.
YET, as we’ve noted about other New Testament words, like “love”, there’s sometimes as many as 7 kinds in the Greek mode to sort out. So also, there’s 6 kinds of NT “peace”:
- eirene
- eireneuo
- siapao
- phimoo
- hsychazo
- siago
The most commonly used being the feminine noun ‘eirene’ = peace x89, one x1, rest x1, quietness x1; as in 1Cor 14:33.
BUT, like “Walk” & “walk” in Gal 5, there’s “Peace” & “peace”, one being Capitalized; Perhaps like “heaven” & “Heaven”, or “earth” & “Earth” in Genesis 1 of KJV, perhaps to allegorically distinguish created (‘bara’) & made (‘asah’), where only what’s “made” or “let” is what God saw good x6, then very good 7th time (before 7th day in Genesis 2).
The distinction between "made" and "created" is important, as "made" indicated formation only, does not include destruction, whereas "create" speaks of a creator/destroyer.
So then, let’s notice this: in doing the Walk (‘peripateo’) in the Spirit, Led of the Spirit, it’s firstly as if in “ye” (do err) mode, of ye/you; Whereby “if ye be led of the Spirit ye are not under the law”. Eg: In Romans 6:14’s ye/you, it’s “For sin shall not have dominion over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace”. So, it’s kinda like a transition, from being under the law to being under grace, even for ye, of ye/you, in KJV.
It seems to be getting too technical when looking at Greek & KJV about such, but there’s lots of twists in every good “mystery”, and lots to “learn” about each “fruit”; Especially when considering the “allegory” aspect. For when still in the duality mode of PluralGod, it seems there’s two of everything to sort out, even two kinds of “above”.
Perhaps, for now, we could allegory picture “not under the law, but under grace”, as this:
OT Law(under law)←|→NT BetterLaw: J→C(under grace)→ ↑ established with grace ↑. *
"with grace" A higher concept beyond the notions of being "under law" and "under grace". This concept is being "with grace". The term "under" in both "under law" and "under grace" implies a certain subjection or submission (still at risk and potential for an undertaker). Being "with grace" signifies a companionship or partnership with grace, a state where grace is not just a covering but an integral part of one's life. [end of Part 1]
I hope this initial discussion of peace as the fifteenth aspect of "things above" has helped us to prepare the journey that we'll walk together towards "charity" the greatest. Stay tuned for Part 2, where our search through related passages will continue in...
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [is] with you all. Amen
