**🚀 Focused Strategy and Expansion**

I would definitely advise a small **startup company** to be as **narrow and as focused as possible**.

If you look at the **original Amazon business plan**, there was **no hint of anything other than books** in it. It was not on my radar. I was thinking, I wanted to build an **online bookstore**—that was it.

So, it's been kind of **one foot in front of the other**, and then, sometimes, we expand into new areas in **two ways**.

1. **Customer Need Backward:** We work from a **customer need** to the **skills** that we need.
2. **Skills Forward:** From a **skill set** we already have, we move to a new set of customers.

---

### Expansion Examples

**Kindle** is an example of a **customer need** backward approach. We didn't have any hardware skills **ten** years ago when we started this. We had to hire somebody to lead our hardware group and then build a hardware team. That's a **customer backward approach**.

For the **Skills Forward** approach, a great example of that is **Amazon Web Services (AWS)**. We had the skills to do AWS; we had probably **more distributed computing expertise than anybody else in the world**.
