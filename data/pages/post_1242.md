## Content

Here's an interesting explain (in story fashion) of Matthew 9:13's "I will have mercy, and not sacrifice" = allegory. Go learn what it means.
(note: Mt 9:13 is from Psalms 40:6; Ps 51:16; Hosea 6:6)
If someone says, I will be your friend for a million dollars, and you pay him a million dollars, you will never know if you have a true friend. Almost nothing worse could be done to wreck any possibility of a true friendship. Getting over 
the damage caused by such an arrangement, and trying to find your way back to authenticity would be a feat. And if you can't afford a million dollars, and someone else steps up and says, I will give the million dollars to him so you don't have to. I will buy that friendship for you -- guess what, the friendship which was paid for will still leave you guessing what sort of relationship you have. God doesn't desire sacrifice... that's OT, and repeated in the NT by Jesus. Go learn what this means -- God doesn't (and didn't) desire sacrifice. You don't buy love and you don't send someone to buy love. If you buy into religion's arrangement, you will always wonder, somewhere in there, what sort of monster you appeased.
~Wendy Francisco
