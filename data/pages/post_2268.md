## Content

GIFT[dorea] of God & GIFT[doron] of God & GIFT[charisma] of God???
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God, our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
Interesting to notice the "GIFT" of God DIFFERS in Greek:
in 
John 4:10 "gift" of God is dorea
in Romans 6:23 God's "gift" of "eternal life" is charisma
in Ephesians 2:8 the salvation "gift" of God is doron
Romans 5:15-18 uses several different Greek words for GIFT:
15 But not as the offence, so also is the FREE GIFT [charisma]. For if through the offence of one many be dead, much more the grace of God, and the GIFT [dorea] by grace, which is by one man, Jesus Christ, hath abounded unto many.
16 And not as it was by one that sinned, so is the GIFT [dorema]: for the judgment was by one to condemnation, but the FREE GIFT  [charisma] is of many offences unto justification.
17 For if by one man's offence death reigned by one; much more they which receive abundance of grace and of the GIFT [dorea] of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ.)
18 Therefore as by the offence of one judgment came upon all men to condemnation; even so by the righteousness of one the FREE GIFT came upon all men unto justification of life.
In Romans 6:23 it notes the GIFT [charisma] of God is "eternal life" (which is notably) "THROUGH Jesus → Christ our Lord"; 
Later noting in Rom 10:4 Christ is the end of the law for righteousness (the free gift that came upon all men in Rom 5:18)
So not only the FREE GIFT of righteousness BY Jesus Christ; But the GIFT of eternal life THROUGH Jesus→Christ (aka in Christ mode);
For 
Jesus mode was only "change of the law" (Mt 5:43,44; Heb 7:12),
but in "Christ" mode it is "the end of the law" for righteousness;
Which Paul noted is NOW manifested without the law: Rom 3:21.
Seems the point is by "eternal life" being the GIFT [charisma] of God, both of His righteousness, by grace, by Jesus Christ and through Jesus → Christ, it helps us put an end to thought of dying; Especially by having no mention of law (sin, death) in the end of the story:
Which is notably the grace salutation of Paul (not of John):
The "grace" of our Lord Jesus → Christ [be/is] with you all. 
Amen.
