Marianne, glad you’ve been thinking about this 8th aspect.
I’ll address your punny comments below.

For now, Let’s review & summarize it a bit:
- 8th = and Father who is above(epi) all, including above one God in Eph 4:6, is like...
- 8th = perfectness(teleiotes) of charity[grace agape], is above(epi) forgiving in Col 3:14, is like...
- 8th = perfection(teleiotes) to go on unto by leaving 7 things in Heb 6:1-3, is like...
- 8th = charity, noted as ‘epignosis’ knowledge\* of our Lord JC in 2Peter 1:7,8;
\*epignosis is above [more than] ‘gnosis’ knowledge\*\* of our Lord JC in 2Peter 3:18.
\*\*gnosis shall “vanish away” when it comes to “charity never faileth” in 1Cor 13:8;
and ‘gnosis’ knowledge that shall vanish away is also rendered as “science” in English.

Glad to hear you’re thinking about this,
Because this:
‘epignosis’ knowledge of the truth is what God our Saviour will have all come unto in 1Tim 2:4,
and ‘epignosis’ knowledge of the truth, to also come unto, is in addition to all men being saved.

Let me share one more scenario of 8, from James 3:17,
and reversing the numbering, as if the last shall be first,
to help shew it’s not so hard, but EASY to in-treat pure grace:

1\. first pure wisdom (aka pure grace)
2\. is then peace-able,
3\. gentle,
4\. EASY to be intreated,
5\. full of mercy,
6\. full of good fruits,
7\. without partiality (no respect of persons),
8\. without hypocrisy (no leaven in that higher heaven)

This pure wisdom, described in James 3:17, is free for the ASKing in James 1:5, and is “liberally” (simply, openly, frankly, sincerely) given to all who ask that God who “upbraideth not” for such wisdom; With the only condition being that when (not if) we do ask, we should then be desiring the singleness of all truth, and not be double minded when asking, as noted in James 1:6-8, which notes, in Greek, that even an ‘aner’ kind of man (of ‘anthropos’/’aner’ kinds of men) is still “unstable in all his ways”, if still having plural “ways”. So I liken it as being the “more excellent way” in 1Cor 12:31, described as “charity” in 1Cor 13, which “thinketh no evil”: neither to punish←nor→forgive. 

That God, who [[upbraideth]] not, to ask, seems to be a higher God than the God on high. So perhaps higher than the Majesty on high which Jesus SAT right of in Heb 1:3, and perhaps even higher than the God which Christ SITTETH right of in Col 3:1, which is still only ‘ano’ above of two ‘ano’ & ‘epi’ above(s) in Col 3:1-14. Mind Blowing.

Now, Marianne, to address your comments, such as you say you’re getting ‘older’:
I think of you as getting newer of older←&→newer; Until in Christ, of thru J→C, it’s “all things new”, for the old has passed away. Such takes us from an Old Testament to a New Testament. Yet also, in that Christ, being reconciled (a reconciling process, beyond Christ), as if being reconciled unto that God who cannot lie nor die, which seems to be above both Jesus mode (since one of 2SonsJesus, the Son of man, is a liar), and Christ mode (since even the true Christ, of false/true Christs arising, still died). So even beyond Jesus taking us from BroadWay of Many to NarrowWay of Few, there’s also Christ taking us from old/new duality to all things new; Yet ALSO that God in that Christ who is reconciling the world to himself, as if unto “eternal life” that is life void of death, not just life after death. So not just a new way, of old/new, but a new AND living way, of he is not God of the dead, but God of the living. No dead to even resurrect in that God is light having no darkness at all (perhaps allegory for grace having no law at all, or as if awareness having no ignorance at all). Mind Blowing.

As to your punny ‘feeling like a chemist factory’, let’s consider the “elements” that shall melt (away) with fervent heat as not being the elements on the periodic table, but as allegoric f-law elements such as fear, doubt, worry, lack, etc. So as we grow in grace, even beyond the ‘gnosis’ knowledge unto the ‘epignosis’ knowledge of the truth, we be-come more and more un-attached to the denser things of earthy matter, to go on unto the spiritual things.

As to your notice of the last verse in Col 3 being contradicting, yes it is, but let’s notice it’s in reference to “serve”, aka still servant mode. It helped me to notice Apostle Paul uses a dialectic logic, which looks at both sides of competing sides, then concludes neither of such sides. Yet it’s kinda like searching for hidden manna in his writings, which even Peter said have some things hard to be under-stood. So let’s consider our goal is not just to under-stand, but also go above and beyond such under & stand of under-stand, to above & seated, where there’s neither under the law nor under grace; For even under grace is still under. So the heart should be esablished WITH grace, as in Heb 13:9, which is not with “meats” such as under the law or under grace. Interesting stuff.

As to your punny ‘nervous system does not get stronger’, let’s consider Paul’s allegory that: when “I Am” (grace & truth) is weak, then “Am I” (law & lie) is strong. So maybe it’s a good thing your ‘nervous’ system is not getting stronger. LOL. In the science of the nervous system there’s two kinds of nerves: motor or efferent nerves carrying mind thoughts to the whole body, and sensory or afferent nerves, which are more body than mind. So Paul talks of using all our God given senses to discern (a-void) whatever is both good and evil (because it ends evil), and James talks of bridle the whole body by becoming the perfect kind of ‘aner’ man who does not off-end. So there’s renewing of the mind, and perfecting even the ‘aner’ kind of man (of anthropos/aner kinds of men). Interesting stuff.
