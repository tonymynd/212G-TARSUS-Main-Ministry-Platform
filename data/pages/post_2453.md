BEMA judgment ?

Share your insights re 2Cor 5:10's 'bema' judgment of good or bad (ends bad)
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ. 
2Cor 5:10 is last of 12 mentions of 'bema' = judgment seat; Also end of judged good or bad. In Greek it's grace good [agathos] or law bad [kakos = evil, wicked, bad] which Rom 7:21 noted was still present when law good [kalos}, as law's both good [kalos] & evil [kakos] ends bad. So it 
requires put away both law good & law evil to BE only God's good [agathos], Jesus said is "one", not twain.
2Corinthians 5:10
For we must all appear before the judgment seat [bema] of Christ; that every one may receive the things done in his body, according to that he hath done, whether it be good [agathos] or bad [kakos].
My thoughts:
Thank God 2Cor 5 goes on to mention positive things like the love of Christ constraineth us in 5:14, and if any man be in Christ (of through Jesus→Christ is the end of the law) he is only a new creature (not still both old & new) in 5:17, and in 5:18 God hath reconciled us to himself by Jesus Christ (aka by grace & truth came by JC), and in 5:19 to wit "that God" was in "Christ" (is the end of the law: Rom 10:4) reconciling the world [kosmos] to himself (is only & always agathos good), not (law) imputing their trespasses unto them, and has given us the ministry and the word [logos] of reconciliation (aka even more than redemption); So that in us, as ambassadors of Christ, it's as if God himself may thereby beseech: be ye reconciled to God (who can't lie nor die).
So then, the 'bema' judgment seat of Christ 2Cor 5:10 which said we must all appear before was as if a judgment of whether grace or law (still ended bad); But thanks to grace of God which came by Jesus Christ, and his Christ thereof Jesus→Christ is the end of law for righteousness, so then the only judgment for all is grace good [agathos], the kind of good Jesus said God is only & always one, not both good [kalos] and evil [kakos] ends bad.
Please share your insights about 2Cor 5:10 & also 5:11→21.
The grace (only) of our Lord Jesus→Christ be/is with you all. Amen.
