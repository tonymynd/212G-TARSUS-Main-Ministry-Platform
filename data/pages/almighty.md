Since you like to study about Bible Mystery, here’s one:

**Bible Mystery: Re the “Almighty”, in Revelation:**

1st mention: Genesis 17:1.

The LORD = the **Almighty** God: appears to Abram when 99,

And says “_walk before me, and be thou perfect_”:

- LORD (‘Y@hovah’) = _darkness, and not light_: Amos 5:18.

- Abram←OldName is not yet Abraham→NewName.

- “_walk before me_” = Driven (not Led = follow me).

- So it’s allegorically Almighty Law (aka not Grace).

A NT mention: 2Corinthians 6:16-18 by Apostle Paul:

- the “living God” \* (allegory: living Law) = the Lord **Almighty**

- this Law God wants to be “a Father” to you also, of ye/you,

\*in Heb 10:31 “living God” = plural hands = fearful←&→fall

Now to Revelation:

 **these phrases, used 'five' times in Revelation,**

**are like "_five in one house divided_**, **2 against 3 & 3 against 2";**

mentioned in Luke 12:52, allegorize 5 books of Moses = called Law.

-Revelation 1:4 "_which is and which was and which is to come_"

-Revelation 1:8 "_which is and which was and which is to come_" 

-Revelation 4:8 "_which was and is and is to come_" =  MIDST\*

-Revelation 11:17 "_which art and wast and art to come_"

-Revelation 16:5 "_which art and wast and shalt be_"

\*notice this “_Lord God **Almighty**_” in 4:8 reverses is and was

**"Lord God Almighty" in Rev 4:8 also occurs five times:**

-Revelation 4:8 as "was, and is, and is to come"

-Revelation 11:17 as "art, and wast, and art to come"

-Revelation 15:3

-Revelation 16:7

-Revelation 21:22

Talk about con-fusion, especially in 4:8 & 11:17:

-4:8 Lord = was, God = is, Almighty = to come.

-11:17 Lord = art, God = wast, Almighty = art to come.

No wonder it talks of no rest yet in Revelation 4:8;

And talks of peace taken away in Revelation 6:4,

And no Eph 3:18 depth yet in Rev 21:16 so nigh end.

Turns out "Almighty" refers to "wrath" in Rev 19.

John the Baptist had spoken of "_[[wrath to come]]_",

and perhaps why John the Baptist got beheaded;

For 1Thessalonians 5:9 clarifies this about God:

"_God hath not appointed us to wrath_" (law). For

"_law worketh wrath_", so  not wrath(law) to come for us.

1Timothy 1:2 says Grace, mercy, and peace from God.

So let's all endure (thru such f-law con-fusion) unto TheEnd:

_The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ_ [be] _with you all. Amen_.

That's why Matthew 24:13 said "_endure unto the end_",

and Matthew 24:6 said "_the end is not yet_" if sorrows.
