## Content

EASTER(s)?? FYI: As still 2Easters for East/West churches, there was 2Passovers for Saducees/Pharissees at time of Jesus. Easter(s) & Passover(s) for Hebrews are moveable (changable) holidays. It still represents division, not unity.
For Christians Easter is first Sunday after paschal full moon that occurs on or after vernal equinox. If full moon falls on a Sunday, Easter is following Sunday. The holiday can occur between March 22 & April 25. But in 325AD the council of Nicaea 
established Easter would be celebrated on Sunday. Before it was celebrated on different days in different places. 
For Jews Passover (Pesach in Hebrew) is their holiday commemorating Hebrews' exodus from Egypt, lasts 7 days in Israel & among Reform Jews, but 8 days elsewhere. It begins 15th of Nisan, 7th month in Jewish calendar. It ends 21st of Nisan in Israel & for Reform Jews, 22nd of Nisan elsewhere. 
Yet there were 2Passovers at time of Jesus: one for Sadducees & one for Pharisees. So 2sabbaths for Jews that week. Seems why John 7:43 says there was division (not peace) because of him (being 2Sons: of God & of man).
The Easter & Passover gap can be large, as in 2005: Easter was March 27 & Passover was April 24. Western Christian churches & Eastern Orthodox churches oft celebrate 2Easters on different dates. On rare occasions West & East dates match, such as they did on April 8th of 2007.
Conclusion: it's a confusing mess, not only for Jews vs Jews, but also for Orthodox vs Christians.
Point: God is not the author of confusion, but of peace.
