## Content

Bible Mystery: Contrasting Two Biblical Gods: Law & Grace.
- Grace: hath said: 
"I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee". 
- Law: hath said: behold
"I, 
even I, will utterly forget you, and I will forsake you"
- Grace: "that God":
"that God is light, and in him is no darkness at all". 
- Law: "the LORD":
"the day of the LORD [is] darkness, and not light".
To wit, "that God" was in Christ, 
- reconciling the world unto himself, 
- not imputing their trespasses unto them; and 
- hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation. 
- Grace: the just judge ("higher" than the heavens: "harmless")
- Law: the "unjust judge" (the Majesty on "high": not harmless)
SO THEN, the end to endure unto, notably already written, is:
"The GRACE of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you all. Amen"*.
*only in the KJV, for modern versions have removed "Christ".
