What is highminded (high mindedness)? Allegorically it is lawminded (law mindead).  

Highminded is often simply defined as proud or haughty. But I noticed both "proud" and "highminded" are listed among "unholy" things in 2Timothy 3 we're told to "from such turn away". This caused me to look further into the [allegoric](Allegory.md) meaning of "highminded". I found highminded is not only similar to [double minded](ShewBread3.md#dblem): James 1:8 & 4:8, but it also seems to describe center and right [sides](RevelatorySermons15.md): [division](Division.md) of plural divided heavens "on high", including the new high priest [standing](Revelations7.md#21) at the right hand of GOD (LORD, Majesty) on high, which is Law of [Law or Grace](TwoGods.md); Since His Grace is merciful [only](Hebrews5.md): Luke 6:36, not both merciful and vengeful. The [will of God](GodShew4.md) oft stated in OT & NT is I will have [mercy](Mercy.md), and [not sacrifice](Sacrifice.md); Which is to allegory say: I will have [grace](GraceUntoYou.md), and [not law](Law.md), when studied out. For in Matthew 9:13 it's notably given as a "go" [learn](OneLiners.md#BibleTranslators) what Hosea 6:6 [meaneth](RevelatorySermons25.md).

Be not highminded: Rather "from such turn away"  
Romans 11:20 ... 1Timothy 6:17 ... 2Timothy 3  

High minded is similar to being puffed up with law knowledge, as if being lion of Judah prideful, rather than being edified up and exhorted up higher by [charity](GMP/Notes/GodShew/Charity.md): grace knowledge. Of the [two sorts of knowlege](TwainShew4.md#Knowledges) to biblically compare and contrast: Law knowlege and Grace knowledge, it's notably Law knowlege that is both "ready to vanish": [Hebrews 8:13](Hebrews9.md) and "shall [vanish](Vanish.md)": 1Corinthians 13:8. The reason charity(grace knowledge) never faileth nor vanishes is because charity(grace) thinketh no evil(law). Charity is also notably to be put on above forgiving one another by law, for true forgiveness is only plausible by abolition of the law, since law imputed sin also imputes unpardonable sin, and law is also the strength of sin's death sting. So as long as highminded law is given place, a high place, there'll continue to be sinners, unpardonable sin both here and hereafter, and a death sting. Hence we are exhorted to give no place to the devil(law), rather than to struggle or wrestle with it and more often than not lose the battle. Eg: I fought the law and the law won. It's better to flush law as dung rather than fight it, which gives it strength, to apply a death sting to you. Allegorically law is biblically "am I", and grace is "I am". So it is allegorically Pauline written "when I am (grace) weak, then am I (law) strong". For law hind-ers grace, makes it no more grace, rather grace + law ends bad, as bad as blessed + cursed = accursed, and as bad as life + death is as saved + destroyed after, which is as bad as life + death = dead end.  
  

Wickedness is what occurs in high places in Ephesians 6: 12  
For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers,  
against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against [spiritual wickedness](HowbeitAfterward.md) in high [places].  
So then, be not highminded, mind not high things, also speaks also of mind not wickedness: laws.  

  
When a bible study is done about wickedness, it seems to reveal the root of wickedness is the law.
