Details re preparation day(s):  
- Mt 27:62 reveals chief priests & Pharisees came to Pilate "the day after preparation" to request the tomb be guarded, to assure the body couldn't be stolen & a resurrection claimed.  
- Mk 15:42 reveals it was "preparation day" when Joseph of Arimathaea requested the body to bury.  
- Lk 23:54 says it was "preparation day" he took it down, wrapped it in linen, laid it in a sepulcher.  
- Jn 19:14 reveals it was "preparation of the passover", not preparation day for their weekly sabbath  
- Jn 19:31 reveals the (passover) sabbath following "preparation" was an "high day", and Jews wanted bodies down before sabbath began (at evening), because of their law in Deuteronomy 21:23 notes if anyone hanged, the body should not remain on the tree overnight.  
- Jn 19:42 again reveals they put the body in the tomb because of the Jews' "preparation" [day].  
- Mk 16:1 reveals two Marys bought spices for anointing after the (passover) sabbath, which would be on the Friday, since everything closed on Thursday sabbath & Saturday sabbath.  
- Lk 23:55,56 & Lk 24:1 reveal two Marys watched the body entombed (Wed), then returned (home) & prepared the spices they'd bought (Friday), rested on the sabbath (Saturday), came back to the tomb with spices early Sunday morning (before sun rise). But the body already gone, so they never got to do their spices anointing thing.

So it's not correct to ass-u-me crucifixion occurred on Good Friday nor last supper occurred on (Maundy) Thursday as many are still erroneously taught in many churches.

Unknown to many Christians is there were 2sabbaths the week of crucifixion. 1st of 2sabbaths was an annual sabbath (Passover: Pesach: also called hag hamatzot: an annual eight day Jewish spring festival of unleavened bread), an "high (holy) day" which occurred Thursday of crucifixion week. 2nd of 2sabbaths was their weekly sabbath (considered higher), which occurred on Saturday (every week).
