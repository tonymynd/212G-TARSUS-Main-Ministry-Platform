Faith is a HUGE topic. The word “faith” occurs 247 times, yet only twice in OT. So it’s primarily a NT topic, now our 7th fruit of the Spirit topic to study, ponder, learn about.

NT “faith” in Greek is always ‘pisits’, except when called “little faith”, which may be allegory for law faith, or childish belief. The word “faith” (‘pistis’: feminine noun) is described as a conviction of the truth of anything: belief; Also English rendered as “assurance” x1, “belief” x1, “fidelity” x1, as if continually assuring us of our salvation through sanctification of the Spirit unto belief of the truth, which involves let go of lies we may have firstly erroneously believed to be true, such as a childish Boogie Man.

So beyond “belief of the truth” is “know the truth”, as an upgrade from believe to know.


But first, let’s Review:

As we’ve been Led of the Spirit, through Fruit of the Spirit, beginning with Love being the “commandment” (rule, ordinance, law) to Love one another; We’ve now seen there was a notable goodness1/goodness2 difference from 5th(midst) & to 6th fruit,

Perhaps(?) a 5/4 ratio change (like ‘chrestotes/agathosune’ 5/4 ratio of NT “goodness”):
- FROM these 5: Love, Joy, Peace, Longsuffering, Gentleness(goodness1)=midst
- TO-WARD these 4: Goodness, Faith, Meekness Temperance,
Perhaps(?) as if a transition per se, maybe allegory as if the Spirit has been leading us forward: From under the law →To→ under grace has better things, as if upgrading. For Galatians 5:18 KJV had said “if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law”; And Romans 6:14/15 had said ye/we are not under the law, but under grace. Seems, our Walk in the Spirit maybe as if From needed governance / To less needed governance, b/c we’re being fed spiritual food we can digest & apply, by what we’ve been learning.

So, as we’ve seen in the previous chapter, there was an interesting NT 5/4 ratio of goodness1/goodness2, which may(?) even allegory suggest there’s a 5/4 ratio of 9 fruit. For 5th=midst gentleness fruit still notably had both “goodness and severity”; And not as contrasting goodness or severity, but as if commanding Love’s goodness1 being conditional on u be good, else severity = cut off. So 5th gentleness fruit aspect was still kinda like law = both good and evil, by still having both goodness and severity.

So it seems first 5 fruit aspects may(?) have been allegory portraying still under the law, till past midst. Then 6th as goodness2 was as goodness only; Perhaps allegory as if going on From under law / To under grace as if go on to better things God provided for us, of them/us; Or as if going From left hand(side) / To right hand(side) of plural “hands” of a PluralGod on high. Something to ponder as we now go on to our discussion about faith.


Re faith:

So now, when look at, study  “faith” fruit, it may become as if Paul’s allegoric faith upgrade: “from faith / to faith” in Romans 1:16,17; Where Paul first notes his gospel (of Grace & peace) is now “the gospel of Christ” (as if gone through Jesus→Christ), notably a gospel in which he’s “not ashamed”.


Why?

First he 1:16 says it’s the power (dunamis) of God unto salvation, to the (singular) Jew first, and also to the Greek; Then also 1:17 says: For “therein (the gospel of Christ) is the righteousness of God revealed from faith to faith”; Perhaps as if from “great faith” under law to greater faith under grace, as if growing in faith, by grace, from justified “by faith” as circumcision were, to justified “through faith” as uncircumcision are, as noted in Romans 3:29,30, about how one God is both the God of the Jews by faith & the God of the Gentiles through faith.

Clever wording.


But what does it mean?

Seems Paul suggests by faith is by promise, so not of the law, nor by works of the law; And through faith is as going or growing from your faith to the faith of the Son of God. So in our Introduction Chapter 1 we had 2 lists of upgrades, first 9 fruit of the Spirit in Gal 5:22,23, then also 7 things to “add to your faith” in 2Pet 1:5-10; Since in 1Peter 1 it said, re “your faith”: it’s only for the salvation of your soul, of soul/spirit, perhaps as if to suggest there’s also a spirit salvation to go on unto, beyond salvation of your soul. For even when in spirit mode, of soul/spirit, there’s still this to do: “comparing spiritual things (plura) with spiritual”. Why? Because law is both natural & spiritual, yet also both good & evil, so law may also be natural distress & spiritual wickedness afterward.


Re: their faith:

Seems, on the topic of faith, most faith preachers use Hebrews 11, wherein “by faith” is mentioned 17 times, and “through faith” also mentioned 5 times; Yet most faith preachers, who say you can’t please their rewarder God without their faith (from Hebrews 11:6), never seem to mention Paul’s conclusions in Hebrews 11:13,39 are:
- 11:13 these all died (1st of 2 lists of OT law faith heroes), “not having received the promises”, and
- 11:39 these all (2nd list, also all died), having obtained a good report “through faith”, (but yet stil) “received not the promise”:
Seems as if Paul’s revealing oops1 & oops2, both by faith & through faith, if still law faith. So Heb 11:40 finally concludes God provided some better thing for us (of them/us), as if something better than all dying and neither receiving the promises, nor the promise.

For Hebrews 10:36 previously talked of “receive the promise” after do the will of God, which seems to be I will have mercy, and not sacrifice, as if to allegory say have faith in grace, not in law. For Gal 3:11 said what’s evident from evidence is that no man justified “by the law”, and Heb 10:8 says sacrifice and offering, for sin, is offered “by the law”.

So re faith, or rather firstly re what’s “not of faith”,
let’s connect these dots in two different verses by Paul:
- whatsoever is NOT OF FAITH is sin: Romans 14:23 KJV,
- and the law is NOT OF FAITH: Galatians 3:12 KJV.

Seems why, in John 5:45 KJV, JC pointed out to law believers that he (as grace & truth) would not accuse them, but one they trusted (aka had faith in), Moses (law giver), does accuse them to the Father. So it’s like revealing law = accuser of brethren, and can’t be trusted. Also seems, by his twice stoop to write on ground, in John 8, where they brought a woman caught in adultery, such awareness of law = accuser, convicted their conscience. Interesting to notice “conscience” begins in John 8:9, and can be “evil conscience” till last mention of “conscience” is only a “good conscience” toward God in 1Peter 3:21.


So people in general, whose faith still in law, are more like am I (?), than like I am (!).
Paul’s allegory example of such: “when I am (grace) weak, then am I (law) strong”.
It’s interesting to notice sometimes a verse may say “am I” instead of “I am”.

Shield of faith of grace much more effective than any law force shield; faith without works is death (is law) Many years believed that faith required actions to please God rewarder, for so long did or failed to do as told "touch not; tase not; handle not; Which all are to perish with the using;", my faith in law was failing...

Re: faith may fail, if not upgraded:

Seems faith can get tried, perhaps even fail if not upgraded. One example is Peter, in Luke 22:31-34, where he’s prayed for, so his faith will not fail, and foretold he’ll later get a conversion upgrade (from ‘strepho’ converted: Mt 18:3) to ‘epistrepho’ converted; So then he’ll strengthen (not weaken, nor dissimulate) brethren. When Peter implies he’s got faith to go to prison & death, he’s foretold he’ll deny Jesus thrice before the cock crows. Later we see Peter among 7 gone fishing, when things didn’t work out how they’d thought (seems they’d assumed Israel would get restored). Yet even after told to feed the lambs & sheep by resurrected Jesus, we later see Peter killing (not forgiving) Ananias & Sapphira, bringing great fear (not love void of fear, nor grace) on the whole church. Etc. Seems Peter much later reports he got faith upgraded by read all Paul’s epistles in 2Pet 3.


Re our faith:

In Hebrews 12:2, Paul calls “Jesus” the “author and finisher of [our] faith”. Yet Son of man Jesus fanatics (as I once was) can ASSUME this means Son of man Jesus, by still being UnAware Son of man NEVER occurs in epistles of Paul, who cleverly reveals, in Romans 3:4 (Greek mode), to “let God be true”, we should let every (‘anthropos’) man be a “liar”. So, Son of man=’anthropos’=”liar”, as I finally realized I was a liar, when I was still in Son of man Jesus mode. Seems why Paul clarifies, 3 times: “the just shall live by faith”, yet also clarifies, in Galatians 2:16, he means “the faith of Jesus Christ” and more specifically “the faith of Christ”, of through Jesus→Christ, since that Christ is “the end of the law”, and because it’s evident “no man is justified by the law”. So, in Gal 2:20, Paul clarifies the faith he now lives by, upon being crucified with Christ, is “the faith of the Son of God” (not Son of man=’anthropos’=”liar”, which I once was).
So when looking at Jesus as author and finisher of our faith, it’s only Son of God Jesus.

So when I finally asked the God who “upbraideth not” for His pure wisdom (James 1:5), in my heart it was as if I was finally & humbly asking for His all truth, of the only true God, and even if it made me a liar (who still needed to learn much more of His truth), which it kinda did, but not in a condemning way from that God who doesn’t upbraid. That God never accuses nor condemns, but does reveal things that made me go OMG.


Re: unity of the faith: Eph 4:11-15

Looking closer, and in more context, this is interesting to notice: Paul first mentions five kinds of ministries, all as plural: apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachers; and as being given for 3 things:
- for perfecting of the saints,
- for the work of the ministry,
- for edifying the body of Christ;

Then says
“Till we all come in the unity of faith”,
yet only as the first of 3 things:
- in the unity of faith, AND
- of the knowledge (‘epignosis’. not ‘gnosis’) of the Son of God (not Son of man),
- UNTO a perfect (teleios: brought to it’s end) man (‘aner’, not ‘anthropos): as the measure of the stature of the “fulness of Christ” (as if then graceful, merciful, peaceful). Then Paul clarifies:
such is by “be no more children”, so then he tells childish Ephesians to grow up, as if by go on, through Jesus→Christ = end of the law, to be like that Christ, who’s not childish, nor just a man, but a perfect ‘aner’ man, who’s put away his childish things (laws). A few verses later he talks of put away lying, as if allegory for put away lawing one another.

So then,
“unity of the faith” is important, but only 1/3 of 3 things about the fulness of Christ.


Re faith to remove mountains = “nothing” yet:

In Mt 21:19-21, Jesus law-cursed a fig tree for having no fruit (only leaves) when he was hungry for fruit. When disciples marvelled how the fig tree presently withered away, he both answered and said to them: “verily I say unto you (of ye/you), if ye have faith, and doubt not, ye shall not only do this [which is done] to the fig tree, but also if ye shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed, and be thou cast into the sea; it shall be done”.
Sounds like powerful faith stuff, even if it’s not literal, but allegory.
- seems Israel might be the allegory for a fig tree he cursed for not having fruit, even though Mk 11:11-14 notes it wasn’t even time for figs; so it’s allegory as if he was saying no man should eat the law of Israel hereafter for ever
- seems he didn’t specify a mountain to be removed, but was on Mount of Olives journeying from Bethany to Jerusalem; Yet since he said “also” for such, perhaps allegory meant also say to their law (has a curse) go jump in the dead sea

Yet, also re faith to “remove mountains” (plural):
After Paul mentioning a “more excellent way” in 1Cor 12:31, he then notes in 1Cor 13:2, even if I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries, and have all (‘gnosis’) knowledge, and have all faith, so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity (of these three: faith, hope, charity: 1Cor 13:13 KJV), I am (still as if) nothing [yet].


WOW.

Seems Paul is allegory saying, if we haven’t yet “from faith to faith” upgraded, from faith in law to faith in grace, even the law faith to remove mountains still counts as if nothing yet. Why? For what’s evident from the evidence is that no man is justified by the law.


SO,

Even though 1Cor 13:13 mentions faith, of these three abide: faith, hope, charity, he also allegory clarifies if we look at such three as if being great, greater, greatest; Only charity is “greatest” of 3. So perhaps, concerning “great faith” of law, we should leave such, lest our hope mode become hopeless by law faith instead of hopeful by grace faith.


Re “shield of faith”:

When looking closer at the whole “armour of God” in Ephesians 6:13-17 KJV, it’s interesting to then notice this “shield of faith” that is “able to quench all the fiery darts of the wicked” seems like the only piece of such armour that is to be taken (analambano) “above (epi) all”, perhaps as if different, or more than the three armour things mentioned before & two armour things mentioned after the shield of faith. As such, it may cleverly allegorize the upgrade to grace faith we’ve been learning about, to then be, when fully UpGraded, like a pure grace force [[Field]] of also receive no harm (more than just do no harm); Perhaps as if ”much more” than any protective police shield (badge) & bullet proof vest worn by so-called peace officers, or any hand held swat shield to hide behind.

Perhaps the shield of faith we can/should take above (epi) all other pieces of armour, even higher than ‘ano’ above, may become as if neither of defensive nor offensive, but above both defence & offence of sides wars. For pure grace doesn’t make sides to have sides wars, and doesn’t take either side in any sides war.
