## Content

Our Daniel has passed away.
To all: Grace, Mercy and Peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
He was from Canada, but I lived in USA for many years, and started exploring more about religion, first in search for salvation as I was going through rough times. I visited many established denominations, of which I am also thankful for their kind reception and teachings, yet none could completely help me innerstand the many mysteries contained in the Word, at first, I was a NIV student, then, through investigation, learned AKJV was best for Bible research. 
Did receive help from several student friends, and with their support, I grew in the knowledge of God; for which I am also forever thankful. But eventually, I reached a point where even they could no longer help me innerstand certain things, like why Paul used "Christ Jesus" and "Jesus Christ," sometimes even in the same verse.
One night, I was testing a new kind of search technology that displayed results in a Neuronal 3D visualization (like Google’s now-deprecated “Wonder Wheel”). I searched for various combinations of terms including “Christ Jesus,” and finally, one result appeared: 
godshew.org.
 That in itself was amazing, considering that at the time there were thousands of religious websites. 
Godshew.org
 is now offline, showing only some registrar ads, and is accessible only through 
archive.org
 (last known good snapshot: Jan 19, 2017, link in the comments).
I remember that moment vividly. I had been asking God for answers, and suddenly, 
godshew.org
 appeared with a single page: ChristJesus.htm, introducing the mystery: "Christ Jesus is not Jesus Christ."
What an amazing adventure it was to discover the studies of Daniel Ted Miles. 
FINALLY, I could begin to answer many mysteries found on the AKJV Bible. It literally saved my life, I had been overwhelmed by denominational confusion—so many baptisms that, in reflection, it almost became comical. I was even tackled into the water once for a baptism, LOL.
Now I know that baptism (from the Greek baptizo) means “to submerge.” For years, I thought that it only referred to physical water. But bible is an allegory Gal 4:24, so the second baptism, is to be submerged in the Word of God: "That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word." Ephesians 5:26 AKJV.
I would be forever thankful by the amazing grace that flowed through our brother Daniel Miles, for his kindness and EXACT and amazing bible shares, that helped me significantly to reach the end:
The grace of Lord Jesus Christ [IS] with you all. Amen.
