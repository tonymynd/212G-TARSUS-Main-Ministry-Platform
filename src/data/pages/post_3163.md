## Content

OLD TESTAMENT ← BC (BACKward time)? 
Should we still go there in NEW TESTAMENT → AD time?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Although Titus 1:2 KJV notes the God which "cannot lie" promised "eternal life" before the world [aionios] began, there's no "eternal life" mentioned in the OT, only in the NT. 
(note: OT "everlasting life" ends in Romans 6:22, when contrasted with NT "eternal life" = free "gift"[charisma] of God in Romans 6:23)
So, let's contrast OT ← & → NT:
OT is "time past", now accounted as backward←BC of BC/AD. 
Go there in forward→AD called a "draw←back to perdition".
Hebrews 10:39 KJV
But we are not of them who draw back unto perdition;
but of them that believe to the saving of the soul.
So, we should remind ourselves AD → only goes forward.
OT is allegory as if cold mode of 3 seek & find modes:
cOLD←>LukeWarm→Hot.
So, Jesus said in Mt 24:12 re love [agape]: 
because iniquity shall abound, 
the love [agape] of many shall wax cold. 
OT was called "law and prophets". 
In contrast NT "grace and truth" came by "Jesus Christ" (John 1:17), sent by "the only true God" to kNOW. (John 17:2,3).
Paul's Epistles: Romans → forward, Greet:
Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ.
So, 
we ought to focus fwd on NT Grace and peace of true God. 
OT wise guy Solomon noted in Ecclesiastes about OT law, that it had "No thing new" and was "all vanity and vexation of spirit" 7x. 
In contrast, in NT Christ, of through Jesus→Christ = end of law, it's "all things new" & by old things (of OT) "passed away" (2Cor 5:17).
OT had a LORD[Yehova] Jews called God, but also noted many bad things about such LORD[Yehova], 
Eg: 
- his day comes as "destruction" (not salvation): Isaiah 13:6; 
- he created "darkness" & "evil": Is 45:7; 
- he will forget & "forsake" you: Jeremiah 23:39; 
- he would bring "worst" & no peace to seek: Ez 7:24,25; 
- his day is "darkness, not light" (aka law, not grace): Amos 5:18.
- he both saved and afterward destroyed: Jude 1:5.
In contrast NT God[Theos] is called 
- "the only true God": John 17:2,3, 
- the "God of all comfort": 2Cor 1:3, 
- the "God of peace": Rom 15:33, 
- the God who "upbraideth not": Jam 1:5, 
- the "God of all grace" (aka no law): 1Pet 5:10, 
- that God is "light" has no darkness in him: 1John 1:5, 
- "the only wise God our Saviour" (doesn't also destroy): Jude 1:25.
OT had plural & contrary scriptures, as if describing a God in conflict with himself, by having both wrath & mercy, which is like an oxymoron: two contrary things mingled. 
In contrast,
NT helps us learn the positive script-u-are (as our true reality).
Hopefully this information will help friends still posting OT law stuff to consider leaving OT law, to be in Christ is all new, nothing old:
2Cor 5:17 KJV
Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new.
Interesting biblical "mystery" details for our awareness sake.
The grace of our Lord Jesus→Christ [be/is] with you all. Amen.
