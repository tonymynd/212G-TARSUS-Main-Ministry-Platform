## Content

PLURAL vs SINGULAR biblical allegory contrasts:
Holy Bible contains Old and New "Testaments" (plural); But Hebrews 8:13 notes in say new it makes old vanish away, then only new.
Plural "generations" of Adam (Genesis 5) are not the singular "generation" (Matthew 1) of Jesus Christ.
Jesus was firstly "twain" (plural): 2Sons: both Son of man and Son of God in Mt-Acts, but beyond Acts there is no more mention of Son of man, only Son of God. So Ephesians 2:15 explains of twain (plural), he abolished law to make in himself of twain (plural), one (singular) new man, thereby peace. 
Plural & contrary "scriptures" are not the singular script-u-are. So Galatians 4:30 asks: what saith the "scripture". 
Paul put away "childish things" (plural) to be "man" (singular): 1Cor 13:11 (allegory as if put away laws to be grace). 
So 
in 2Timothy 3:15-17 KJV Paul uses both "scriptures" (plural) and "scripture" (singular) as if to contrast "child" and "man".
Christ is the "seed" (singular), not "seeds" (plural): Gal 3:16.
Plural "churches" are not the singular "church" called the singular "body" of Christ.
