AUTHOR OF REVELATION: JOHN OR PAUL?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord. 
Most ass u me John was author of book of Revelation because it says John 5x therein, but here's evidence for Paul as author.
THE 
END: is not John's, but Paul's grace "salutation", which Paul noted he always penned with his "own hand" to sign-ify it's his writing. So, Rev 1:1 noted his angel sign-ified it. Eg:
2Thessalonians 3:17,18 KJV 
The salutation of Paul with mine own hand, which is the token in every epistle: so I write.
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
Revelation 22:21 KJV 
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
TITLE: official pre-1900 Oxford KJV title was: "The Revelation of St John The Divine", allegory meaning apokalypsis: uncover & reveal about a saint named John thought to be divine. So not by John, but about John, aka that he'd "turned" back to law "behind" him.
"The revelation of Jesus Christ" (Rev 1:1) occurs in Galatians 1:12, by Paul, never by John. So, "revelation of Jesus Christ" noted in Rev 1:1 occurs in epistles of Paul, whom Jesus Christ appointed as his minister (angel), to minister the grace of God, as the gospel of Christ, of through Jesus → Christ is the end of the law. 
Seven angels (ministers of 7 churches) are listed in Acts 20:4, notes they accompanied Paul into Asia (later called Galatia) to establish seven churches in grace. Seems John wrote to such angels, which bewitched them with another gospel (Gal 1:6; 2:9; 3:1). It turned all in Asia away from Paul (2Tim 1:15). So Paul wrote Revelation as a spoof (gotcha) on John.
Scholars now say Revelation has an 'epistle' format, and like Paul's epistles it opens and closes with grace: unique to Paul, never John.
The term "revelation" [apokalypsis] is never used by John, but occurs 8x by Paul in his epistles, also by Peter. 
John 15:15 noted Jesus said he would henceforth not call his disciples servants, but friends, because a servant is a know not. So John would not likely call himself "servant" (know not) in Rev 1:1.
DATE: many say book of Revelation was written about 90AD, by John, but that does not compute since Rev 11 mentions the "temple" which got destroyed in AD70; So, written prior to that, probably about AD64 by Paul (whose death was about AD67).
Revelation 2 & 3 notes 7x "He that hath an ear (singular), let him hear what the Spirit saith UNTO THE CHURCHES". Galatians 1 notes Paul the apostle is writing "UNTO THE CHURCHES". So what the Spirit saith unto the churches is in Galatians, not in Revelation.
Rev 1:10-16 reveals John "TURNED" to what was calling from "behind", aka to a high priest of law with a twoedged sword (executes vengeance & punishment: Ps 149:6,7) out his mouth; Which is neither Jesus Christ (grace & truth) nor the logos of God which in Heb 4:12 is sharper (smrter) than any twoedged sword.
So,
Peter notes in 2Pet 2:22 it is happened according to the proverb, "The dog is TURNED to his own vomit again". 
In 2Peter 3 Peter notes he'd read all Paul's epistles, which would include Hebrews, and perhaps even Revelation. For in 2Peter 3:8 he gives a time allegory not to be ignorant of: 1day is as 1000yrs & 1000yrs is as 1day. Hmmm. 
Seems this time allegory may be used in Rev 20, about devil bound 1000yrs, perhaps being 1day, then released to deceive all nations. 
What are your insights about the author of Revelation?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
