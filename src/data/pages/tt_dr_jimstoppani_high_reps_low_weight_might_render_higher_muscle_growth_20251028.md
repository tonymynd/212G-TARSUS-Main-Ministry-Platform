You may have heard that whether you train with heavy weight for low reps or light weight for high reps,
as long as you train to muscle failure, both will stimulate muscle protein synthesis similarly
and therefore muscle growth similarly. However, that's not the full story. The research study that
discovered this had young men perform four sets of the leg extension to muscle failure,
using either 90% of their 1 rep max weight, which is a weight they could lift for about 3 to 4 reps
or using 30% of their 1 rep max weight, which was a weight they could lift for around 20 to 30 reps.
The researchers measured muscle protein synthesis before the workout, four hours after the workout,
and 24 hours after the workout. Although muscle protein synthesis rates at the four hour mark
were the same for both weights, only the 30% weight, the light weight for high reps,
elevated muscle protein synthesis for 24 hours. The heavy weight in low reps did not. If you extend
muscle protein synthesis for longer, you can expect greater muscle growth. So based on this study,
it appears that training with heavy weight for low reps may not best for building muscle,
but training with lighter weight for higher reps may encourage greater muscle growth. Tag
someone who's convinced that heavy weight builds more muscle.
