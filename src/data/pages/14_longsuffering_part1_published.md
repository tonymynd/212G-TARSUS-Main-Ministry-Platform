14. Longsuffering  
Grace, mercy, [and] peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.  
  
Finally, after some longsuffering journey now have time to work on our next aspect:  
  
17.Love→16.Joy→15.Peace→leads us to & through 14.Longsuffering→unto 13.Gentleness….  
  
Yet, prior to Gentleness, let’s see what can we now learn about Longsuffering?  
  
Seems longsuffering is Greek rendered as both a  
- noun: ‘makrothymia’: patience, endurance, constancy, steadfastness, perseverance, forbearance, longsuffering, slowness in avenging wrongs: longsuffering x12, patience x2 = 14.  
- verb: ‘makrothymeo’: to be of a long spirit, not to lose heart, to persevere patiently and bravely in enduring misfortunes and troubles, to be patient in bearing the offenses and injuries of others: be patient x3, have patience x2, have long patience x1, bear long x1, suffer long x1, be longsuffering x1, patiently endure x1 = 10.  
  
Either way, noun or verb, it has a lot to do with patience & endurance. The verb is also rendered as endured. Perhaps it’s like saying, as Paul says in Hebrews 6:15 about what God sware to Abraham: Surely blessing I will bless thee, and multiplying I will multiply thee. So, AFTER he had patiently endured, he obtained the promise. So Paul also says in Hebrews 10:36, ye have need of patience, that, AFTER ye have done the will of God, ye might receive the promise. Perhaps he’s firstly allegory referring to the Seed: Christ: the end of the law; Which allows us to thereby also lay hold on eternal life [higher calling], after fighting the good fight of faith [lower calling]).  
  
So to get to what comes AFTER, we firstly l-o-n-g-suffer.  
  
Longsuffering occurs in both these lists as the noun: ‘makrothymia’:  
- Galatians 5:22,23 list (Fruit to Walk thru):  
Love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance.  
- Colossians 3:12-15 list (UpGrades to put on):  
  
Bowels of mercies, kindness, humbleness of mind, meekness, longsuffering, forbearing one another, forgiving one another, charity.  
So longsuffering is both a fruit to learn about AND an upgrade to apply while patiently enduring unto charity never faileth. For in 1Cor 13:4, the very 1st of 15 things listed about charity is charity suffereth long = verb ‘makrothymeo’, and 14th thing is charity endureth all things, prior to 15th thing: charity never faileth. Yet first mention of charity begins in 1Cor 8:1, where it notes charity edifieth, and in contrast to ‘gnosis’ knowledge puffeth up. So the ‘gnosis’ knowledge is not what comes after, but before; For it’s the ‘gnosis’ knowledge in 1Cor 13:8 that shall “vanish away” when it comes to never fail.  
  
A verse oft heard about longsuffering is 2Peter 3:9, given after his time allegory in 3:8 about not being ignorant of this one thing, that one day [is] with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day… 3:9 says:  
  
The Lord is not slack concerning his promise, as some men count slackness; but is longsuffering to us-ward, not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance.
  
So the patience and endurance of the Lord is not slow nor somehow delayed, but has a purpose of providing time for all to come to a change of mind, so none should perish.  
  
Yet perhaps we can also learn there’s also an end to suffer in the midst of longsuffering. For in 1Peter 5:10’s ye/you, portrayed as before/after, “suffer awhile” only occurs in “Christ Jesus” mode (which also has begin: Acts 19 & end: 1Peter 5), after which “the God of all grace” makes you perfect, and also stablishes, strengthens, settles you.  
  
Seems “longsuffering” occurs in both OT ←&→ NT;  
  
Yet in NT, mention of it begins in Romans 2:4 ←and→ ends in 2Peter 3:15,16:  
  
Romans 2:4 KJV: Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness and forbearance and longsuffering; not knowing that the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance?  
  
2Peter 3:15,16 KJV: “And account the longsuffering of our Lord [is] salvation; even as our beloved brother Paul according to the wisdom given unto him hath written unto you; As also in [all] epistles, speaking in them of these things; in which are some things hard to be understood, which they that are unlearned and unstable wrest, as [they do] also the other scriptures, unto their own destruction”.  
  
So last NT mention of longsuffering says account longsuffering of our Lord as salvation.  
  
But let’s notice before this Peter talked of how we, of they/we, should “look for new heavens* and new earth wherein dwelleth righteousness”;  
  
*YET even “new heavens” is still plural “heavens”, yet perhaps law←→grace heavens instead of law←→law heavens if wherein the new earth dwelleth righteousness.  
  
Then surprisingly after this 2Peter 3:18 says “But grow in grace, and [in] the knowledge* of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ”;  
  
*BUT this “knowledge” is still the ‘gnosis’ knowledge, which in 1Cor 13:8 KJV shall “vanish away”, when it comes to ‘epignosis’ knowledge of how “charity never faileth”.  
  
Wow, this is a lot of stuff to assimilate, as we longsuffer together through it all. For just when we think we’ve grown in grace, and in the ‘gnosis’ knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ, along comes something more to learn, and often by unlearn stuff we’ve believed.  
  
So, re ‘epignosis’ knowledge, 1Timothy 2:3,4 notes God our Saviour (perhaps as if the 3rd of 3 Saviours) “will have all men* saved, AND come unto the (‘epignosis’) knowledge of the truth”. Such ‘epignosis’ knowledge seems like a higher knowledge than ‘gnosis’ knowledge, kinda like ‘epistrepho’ converted is like a higher converted than ‘strepho’ converted, and like ‘epi’ above is like a higher above than ‘ano’ above in Col 3:1-15.  
  
*men=’anthropos’=liar: Rom 3:4, includes Son of man=’anthropos’: Mt-Rev; Adam2 (Lord from heaven) = second man=’anthropos’: 1Cor 15:47; CJ=mediator man=’anthropos’: 1Tim 2:5, in whom all who live small g godly (perhaps childish) suffer: 2Tim 3:12 & 1Pet 5;10. Yet only a little while, in ye [do err] mode.
