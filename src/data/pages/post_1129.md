## Content

WHAT IF, re: "living God" in Hebrews 10:31... 12:22 KJV? What if it's Apostle Paul's satirical allegory for living Law? For plural (left/right) "hands" of such "living God" (as if living Law) are cleverly (allegory) called "fearful" & "fall" in Heb 10:31.
Eg: Hebrews 10:31 KJV:
"It is a FEARFUL thing to FALL into the HANDS of the living God".
QUESTION: Where would u fall from, to fall into such hands?
ANSWER: Perhaps, as Apostle Paul previously Galatians 5:4 "allegory" portrayed, notably "unto the (bewitched) churches": whosoever of you (of ye/you) are justified "by the law"; 
ye (do err) are "fallen from grace" (even to ye of ye/you). 
What then? Paul says: Then Christ (the end of the law) is become of no effect unto you (of ye/you)... allegory as if even the right side of ye/you is then fallen from grace = above law.
CLARITY: Such is not posted to accuse, condemn, nor fear any; But to aware all, that in Galatians 4:22-24, Paul noted it's all "allegory" in both "covenants", to help bewitched "brethren" recover from a fallen state of f-law, by become aware of it.
FOR ME it was an OMG moment to notice such things; Which the Spirit of grace and truth GENTLY revealed, and not for any condemnation whatsoever, but for awareness sake only.
It then begs a further question: What about "mount Sion": the city of the "living God": described in Hebrews 12:22?  Seems, although Heb 12:18-24's contrast of Sinai→Sion = "better", it may not [yet] be what's best of good→better→best=rest. The "better" things of plural God may not be the rest of the story. For in Heb 12:23 KJV this "God" is still a "Judge".
Seems message is we have to get beyond LukeWarm, of
cOLD-LukeWarm-Hot in the learning game of seek & find, if it's allegory like OT Law -NT Better Law- Grace (is neither).
Perhaps we may have to also Dare Compare these:
- the "living God": Heb 10:31...12:22  = as if living Law
- "God of the living": Mt 22:32; Mk 12:27; Lk 20:38 = Grace 
Wishing only Grace→mercy→peace that lasts to you all, until u become a graceful→merciful→peaceful U (grown in grace) that no longer needs commanding nor governing by laws.
