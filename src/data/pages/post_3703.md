Add PATIENCE (2 Peter 1:6, AKJV)
The word "patience" in this verse is translated from the Greek ‘hupomone’, meaning steadfastness, constancy (patient continuance), and endurance. It describes one who is not swerved from his deliberate purpose or loyalty to faith and piety, even when facing great trials and sufferings, this explains why is right after temperance.
Patience(hupomone) vs. Patience(makrothumia)
- Hupomone: Is stronger than temperance, asif tempered temperance(makrothumia); Is the temper which does not easily succumb under longsuffering; is the self-restraint which does not hastily retaliate a wrong. 
- Makrothumia: Longsuffering is about enduring others' wrongs without seeking revenge.
Key Differences
Hupomone: steadfast endurance, not swerved by cowardice or hopelessness(despondency), able to continue, carries the sense “patient continuance” and “patient waiting”
Makrothumia: Longsuffering(Self-control), which does not succumb to wrath and revenge(retaliation), maintaining self-control in the face of provocation.
Thus, hupomone is about persevering through hardships, while makrothumia is about enduring others' wrongs without seeking revenge and able to stop harm from others: "harmless"

One key realization in studying Gal 5:22;23’s (Fruit of the Spirit) and 2Pe 1:1-10 (7 things to add to your faith) is that PATIENCE(huponome) stands in the midst, right before godliness, brotherly kindness, and charity, and is only then and when “ye shall never fall”, allegory of ye of ye/you shall never fall (fail) like charity that never faileth 1Cor13:8. Seems we needed to innerstand all other aspects before getting to patience. 
From now and on, we can move higher in our large project, passed through “Set your affection on things above(ano: high)” now reaching to a higher: “above(epi: higher) all these things [put on] charity”
Set your affection things above(ano) → above(epi) all these things [put on] charity
This change made on the project title is explained in our spiritual grow through all things above(ano) as we become aware of:
- Be not highminded ← mind not high things → be not highminded; so, now that we have gone thru ‘ano’ high things which will vanish, as we are told “be not highminded”, so we can now go into the pivotal point:
“and to temperance PATIENCE; …” ~ 2Pe 1:5 AKJV  
Patience(huponome) is in the midst of seven things to add to your faith, and what is in the midst can turn back to law if not properly discern. 
“For ye have need of patience(hupomone), that, after ye have done the will of God, ye might receive the promise.” ~  Heb 10:36 KJV 
So, to receive the promise is only after doing the will of God “I WILL have mercy and not sacrifice”. It is impossible to receive the promise(grace) if mercy is not rendered. 
Kind of like those at war, with slogan “show no mercy” to enemy, by not being aware that “God of all grace” and his wisdom from above Jam 3:17 includes “full of mercy and good fruits” who “sent not his Son into the world to condemn (to law) the world; but that the world through him might be saved(graced) Jhn 3:17; Re enemy, or enmity is also abolished EPh 2:15.
Without patience(hupomone) in mercy, enmity remains, and the promise cannot be received.
Patience(hupomone) is the test of grace-based endurance where we ought to be found “approved unto God" for such test. 
Patience is the critical midpoint—either it propels one forward into charity(grace), or it regresses into legalistic endurance if our innerstanding about patience is not complete.
