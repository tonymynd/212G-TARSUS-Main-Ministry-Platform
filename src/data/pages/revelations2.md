So only for the sake of a shew, not as being real.  
For in God is one, there aren't two of everything.  
**

  

**[Grace unto you](GraceUntoYou.md), and peace,  
from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ  
  
**

Of [twain](TwainShew6.md) revelations noted in the title and the first verse:  
(i) The Revelation of St John The Divine (from title)  ... law (portrayed in book of Revelation)  
(ii) The Revelation of Jesus Christ (from 1st verse),   ... grace (portrayed in gospels and epistles)  
as two contrary things that cannot co-exist in peace: law vs grace, only one is clarified as a given, God given: "The Revelation of Jesus Christ, **which God gave** unto him" (Revelation 1:1a); And God given for a stated purpose: "to **[shew](shew.md)** unto his [servants](AboveAServant.md) things which must shortly come to pass" (Revelation 1:1b). So the only reason for such contrary things is a "shew": a comparative teaching, and not for accusation or condemnation, nor for blame or shame, but for awareness sake only; And it's a shew unto "servants" (children: Galatians 4, prophets: Hebrews1), which are kNOW not. So in modern satire we'd say the shew is for them who didn't get the memo about change. It's now  
  appt'd time for AD of BC/AD is accounted as going forward ... enduring unto the end written  
  appt'd time for NT of OT/NT is a better testament having a better hope of all living happily  
  appt'd time for us of them/us is whom God hath not appointed to wrath, but given the victory  
  appt'd time for grace of law/grace is our salvation, not our destruction; our peace, not our war  
  appt'd time for man of child/man and blessedness of man speaks of no sin being law imputed  
  appt'd time for friends of servants/friends because servants know not, but friends do know  
  
    In Luke 16:16 change is from law and prophets (for children) -to- kingdom of God (for man).  
    In John 1:17 change is from law given by Moses -to- grace and truth came by Jesus Christ.  
    In John 15:15 the change is from servants (know not) -to- friends (have things made know)  
    In Galatians 4 the change is from a child = a servant (slave) -to- a son = not a servant (free)  
    In Hebrews 1 change is from prophets (spake law: time past) -to- Son (spoke grace: last days)  
  
So what's being shewn is not shewn to friends (to whom things are made known) ... grace folk: us  
but rather being shewn unto servants (who "know not" what their master doeth) ... law folk: them    Grace: I will never leave you, nor ever forsake you ... Friend  
    Law: I will utterly forget you, and I will forsake you ... Enmity  
  
So, we have the uncovering of a saint named John (whose surname was "Mark": Acts 12, as well as one of two sons of "thunder"), probably sainted by popish Cephas (whom John called Father). And for comparison, since the title suggests John is not only divine, but "the divine", God gave the uncovering of Jesus Christ, who is God sent, as His version of what a divine oughta be, or more like God than the other wise of twain being uncovered, even the "express image" (charakter) of God if God were a person (Hebrews 1:3), the same: yester day, to day, and for ever (Hebrews 13:8); Or as "the apostle" Paul also suggests: "God in the flesh", but also clarifies God is "excepted" (exempt, because God can't lie nor die). So either way it's a shew: a comparative teaching, for our learning.  

  
So, The Revelation (uncovering, disclosure) of Jesus Christ is a given, perhaps the only (God) given of twain revelations, and perhaps the other wise is as James 3 said about such: not from above, not pure (grace), but "earthy, sensual, and devilish" wisdom; Or perhaps the only one to be thrown out of a popularized twain such as baby and bath water. And the purpose mentioned, for  uncovering Jesus Christ, is to "shew", or kinda like a comparative teaching method, and to shew "things" (plura), perhaps this and that, rather this or that since such things are contrary, as are two ways JC did shew in Mt 7: one leads to life, the other to destruction (the ruinous sort: the sort dinosaurs had: extinction). And perhaps, as Paul points out in Gal 4, such "things" are an allegory. And such things are for shew "unto his servants" (again plura), which will hopefully become significant to all readers as they begin to see the allegory of such things and how the mystery of his will is made known unto us by uncovering both John and Jesus Christ, comparing the two, perhaps see which is witch, and why one is perhaps better, or greater, or more like God is.
