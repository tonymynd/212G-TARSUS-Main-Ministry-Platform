## Content

Although grace and truth came by Jesus Christ, we eventually notice there are some differences between his Jesus mode and his Christ mode. Here's just a few:
- in Mt-Acts Jesus is still 2Sons: Son of God + Son of man; But beyond Acts there is no more mention of Son of man, only Son of God. Paul then calls it "the gospel of Christ" of "through our Lord Jesus Christ"
- in Lk 13:32 Jesus noted he was not [yet] perfected, but shall be; As 
if to say he was not [yet] the Christ, but shall be
- in Mt 10:34 & Lk 12:51 Jesus first said he'd neither give nor send peace on earth; And Jn 7:43 noted there was "division" (not peace) "because of him". So Rom 5:1 notes we have peace with God "through our Lord Jesus Christ", for in his Christ mode we can then see "much more" than before
- Jn 3:17 clarifies God did not send his Son of condemn (law) the world, but that the world might be saved (graced & truthed) "THROUGH him"... further clarified as "THROUGH his name"... further clarified as "THROUGH our Lord Jesus Christ" to then be a man in Christ wherein all things are new.
- Jesus was only called Saviour of Israel, twice in Acts; But Christ is called Saviour of the world, also noted twice.
