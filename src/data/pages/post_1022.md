## Content

Bible Lambs to contrast (in Greek mode). Then pick one only.
- Lamb('amnos') of God takes away sin of the world: John 1:29. 
- Lamb('arnion') takes away peace, having wrath: Revelation:all. 
Discussion: 
- 
The Lamb('amnos') of God taketh away ('airo' = remove) the sin of the world. So he doesn't just forgive & cover up sin, but removes it. For if sin only "covered", as in Diaper Blessed of Romans 4:8, then Mt 10:26; Lk 8:17; Lk 12:2 say what's "covered" gets uncovered.
- In Revelation's apocalypse scenario the "Lion of the tribe of Juda" is not a tame Lion; And "Lamb" is always rendered as 'arnion' (not 'amnos'); For it's "one like Son of man" (not Son of God), and has wrath: Rev 6:16. God hath not appointed us to wrath: 1Thess 5:9.
- Jesus is 2Sons (Son of man + Son of God) in Mt, Mk, Lk, Jn, Acts; But "Son of man" never occurs beyond Acts 7:56, and Romans is "the gospel of Christ", of "through (our Lord) Jesus Christ".
