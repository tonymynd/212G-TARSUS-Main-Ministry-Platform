3 Ways to Evaluate Your Business Model… and Which One Determines Your Company’s Future

## Transcript

There's three ways to assess a business model. When I go into a company, I will ask these
questions of the top people. Number one, if you have a good business model, your sales and your
income are going up a steady, steady week by week, month by month, year by year, that's a good
business model. You don't need my help. The second business model is where your sales are up and
sometimes you are successful, sometimes you're not successful, but basically you worry about money
all the time. You never have too much money and then suddenly you lose a customer or somebody
cheats you and you're out of money. That's the second part. Your business model is fluctuating.
That's no good. It's like writing one of those roller coasters in the park.
Now, every day, every week is an adventure and it's not a good adventure. The third type of
business model is where your sales are going down. Your sales and your profits are declining and
that means your business model is broken.
