## Content

Isaiah? let's notice things about (not against) Isaiah:
Isaiah = Old Testament = law & prophets = time past = BC. 
So 
- Hebrews 1:1,2 clarifies "God" has Not spoken to us in time past, Nor by prophets, but in last days, by Son (not Sons).
For
- 
Isaiah was a prophet of the "LORD" (differs from "God" in Noah's Ark story); And in Amos 5:18 "the day of the LORD is darkness, and not light" (allegory for it's law, and not grace). So when the Son (of God) came he said, in Luke 11:35, take heed the light in thee be not darkness (to LORD believers).
So
- We ought not even try to make Isaiah 61:1-3 agree with Luke 4:18-19; For Is 61 had a day of vengeance, but Lk 4 doesn't. So in Lk 5:36 Jesus noted the new "agreeth not" with the old.
- Regarding the "prophecies" of Isaiah, both good and bad, 1Cor 13:8 notes "whether there be (law) prophecies, they shall fail", whether there by (plural) tongues, they shall cease, whether there be ('gnosis') knowledge, it shall vanish way".
Clarity: not said to speak against Isaiah nor LORD believers, but help aware time does not go backward, and when we put our hand to the plow we ought not look back, but only forward.
Grace, mercy, and peace (to all), from God, our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
