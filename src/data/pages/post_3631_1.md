## Content

DILIGENCE
Three best wishes unto all, grace, mercy and peace from God our father and Jesus Christ our Lord. 
It is now the time to explore the concept of “diligence” in our path to charity:
Love→Joy→Peace→Longsuffering→Gentleness→Goodness→Meekness→Faith→Temperance → giving all diligence → virtue 
(Attribution and Spiritual Map on the comments)
POINT: the concept of grace-led “diligence” (not law-driven diligence) is to know and innerstand how we were reproved(hindered to do grace diligence) by the schoolmaster (Gal 3:23;24) that failed every student as the law made nothing perfect (Heb 7:19, Heb 10:All) In contrast, grace approves us through STUDY ‘spoudazo’ to shew thyself approved 2Ti 2:15 (diligently seeking 1st KofG and his righteousness), this is necessary to come out darkness(ignorance) and stop believing lies by knowing the truth (shall make YOU free, Joh 8:32) through building upon the foundation: Jesus Christ: “grace and truth” Joh 1:17.
What can learn about diligence?
“beside this, giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue;” 2Pe 1:5 AKJV
The “besides this” referred to things given once have escaped corruption (law) 2Pe 1:3,4. So, is not diligence under the law (vexation of the spirit) but diligence to make yOUR calling* and election sure elected from the dead to be awaken unto righteousness via Christ as the end of the law for righteousness. 
*This calling is even higher than a “good fight” where we’re ALSO called to lay hold on eternal life, 1Ti 6:12 (there is no fighting in peace)
For us to have the capacity into “giving all diligence” it was necessary to have a fully mature Fruit while Walk in the Spirit Galatians 5. It seems that “giving all diligence”  requires energy that is not hindered by contrary things, hence the exhortation to lay aside every weight, and the sin which so easily besets us, Heb 12:1, as letting go of the past (the old) and the anxiety of things to come (the future) because NOW is our salvation, this is how our Father, the God of all grace, after we suffered a while (as we went through longsuffering 4th aspect of Fruit), “make you perfect, stablish, strengthen, settle you.” 1Pe 5:10
Seems that grace diligence is much more than just doing a good work on what we do, this is an oversimplification. Diligence requires a special set of aspects, perhaps this the reason why we needed to go through all 9 aspects of the Fruit of the Spirit, because only when we get to temperance it says "against such there is no law " as if we have finally laid "aside every weight" to increase the pace to "run" that we may OBTAIN: 
“Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.” ~ Heb 4:16 AKJV
Bad End: diligence under the law: a win-lose deal (all die)
Happy End: give all diligence with grace: a win-win deal (all made alive)
Thanks be to God we have completed a detailed study of all nine aspects of the Fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22;23 AKJV) we are now equipped to increase the speed from “Walk in the Spirit” to “give all diligence” asif exercising our senses to discern(avoid) good and evil, Heb 5:14.
The word for diligence occurs 10 times in the AKJV Bible, only once in Old Testament, and 9 times in the New Testament, so is mostly a New Testament topic, although OT instance is interesting:
“Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it [are] the issues of life.” ~ Pro 4:23
The word “diligence” occurs nine times in New Testament, translated from the Greek as ‘ergasia’ and ‘spoude’ (noun) & ‘spoudazo’ (verb) e.g. “study” 1Ti 2:15
Diligence ‘ergasia’ is also translated as gain, craft and work; includes monetary gain through divination Act 16:16 and the selling of crafted religious figurines Act 19:24; ‘ergasia’ also occurs as law-diligence in Luk 12:58 a work performed in a trial where one might become wretched trying to defeat an adversary in court; lest he defeats you and end up into prison (a time waster if lose or judge the other if win, Inexcusable Rom 2:1), we can boldly determine, this diligence is law business, a work performed to deceive or a gain-lose deal, not yet “grace and truth” where we all gain, a win-win deal.
Diligence ‘spoude’, speed, by implication, eagerness, earnest care, forwardness, occupation, in other words, implies a much more positive, energetic, and enthusiastic work towards a happy end. Yet, diligence ‘spoude’ if not preceded by matured Fruit of the Spirit which includes temperance, still can become a law-problem by being premature (imp-ertinent, still in child mode), or being excessively bold or eager to the point of anxiety and striving not yet peaceful. This is why a Walk in the Spirit is necessary for a more graceful diligence that is no longer emotional, but performs the work with patience, with kindness, taking rest, and by being careful while “led” Gal 5:18; seems grace ‘spoude’ also translated as earnest care, which God puts in us:
“But thanks be to God, which put the same earnest care(diligence) into the heart of Titus for you.” ~ 2Co 8:16 AKJV
Rom 12:8’s diligence 'spoude' as a better diligence (from good/better/best) although still has law-ruling part, exhorts to render mercy with cheerfulness, then we have Jud 1:3 "all diligence" which still exhorts to “earnestly contend for the faith” still a struggle, not author of peace yet. 
 Then we have "all diligence" in 2Co 8:6;7 as a work of grace to be finish in you, implied as 'not yet finished' perhaps b/c still needs to upgrade its love (waxes cOLD, it fails Mat 12:24) to charity ‘agape’ (NEVER fails 1Co 13:8) AND its 'gnosis' knowledge (still gets puffed up 1Co 8:1) to ‘epignosis’ knowledge, after this upgrades then the work of grace is finished:
“Till we all come in the unity of the faith, and of the knowledge ‘epignosis’ of the Son of God, unto a perfect man, unto the measure of the stature of the fulness of Christ:” ~ Eph 4:13
“all diligence” in 2Pet 1:4 certainly is not by plural contrary “works” that oppose themselves “so that ye cannot do the things that ye would.” Gal 5:17, like being married to his grace but still whoring with law, either make the tree good and its fruit good: “My Father worketh hitherto, and I work.” (not wonder-ful works that get rebuked by swordy Jesus Lk 13:27) 
“Wherefore, beloved, seeing that ye look for such things, be diligent that ye may be found of him in peace, without spot, and blameless.” ~ 2Pe 3:14 AKJV
Without spot, like pure and undefiled religion Jam 1, blameless, no law to guilt-trip anyone. 
This grace diligence is the work performed to find grace (at the throne of grace, where only mercy is notably obtainable, not both mercy and sacrifice), and find grace "in time" (not too late), to "help"(not hinder). Thereby find rest (peace): there remaineth a rest to the people of God: Hebrews 3 and Hebrews 4. Also "find" the son that got "lost"(dead), which is notably "found"("alive") in Luke 15. Perhaps also "find" no fault (law) in J→C, as Pilate did "find" no fault in him: Luke 23 & John 19. And if concupiscence a problem (as CrossWalk says it is with half the men in churches), perhaps "find" a law as the source of said hind-rance, as Paul did in Romans 7; Where Paul also notes "we are delivered from the law" (as if in answer to "deliver us from evil": Mt 6:13). [1]
“giving all diligence” is by making our election sure through “study to shew thyself approved unto God” not reproved by the schoolmaster that fails every student for the hell of it. 
“Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith. But after that faith is come, we are no longer under a schoolmaster.” ~ Gal 3:24-25 AKJV
The only one that can approve us is grace:
“To wit, that God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them; and hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation.” ~ 2Co 5:19 AKJV
