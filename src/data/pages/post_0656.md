## Content

CHRISTMAS? How much of it is a package of lies, to unlearn?
Here's what my research found, about things I had to unlearn:
(not shared for accusation nor condemnation, but awareness)
- Lie: it's nicest time of year. Police records & media archives reveal it's worst time of year: more thefts, shoplifting, suicides, domestic disputes, financial woes, alcoholism, drunk drivers, stressful mental disorders, travel accidents, deaths; And word worst is not a New Testament word, only 
occurs 1 time in OT.
- Lie: it's the birth time of Jesus. According to biblical data Jesus birth was at Sukkot (Rosh HaShanah) by using clues such as Zacharias' course of service, birth of John the Baptist, Herod's death. Also Galatians 4:22-24 says it's all "allegory" in both "covenants"('testaments'), so not even literal, but allegorical.
- Lie: on earth peace is about Jesus. Luke 2:11-14 clarifies it's about Christ; And Luke 12:51 Jesus was NotPeace; And Romans 5:1 clarifies we have peace through Jesus→Christ, in Christ.
- Lie: three wise men visited babe at Bethlehem. According to biblical narrative shepherds watching flocks by night visited babe at Bethlehem. Magi (probably Jews returning from Babylon), enough to stir up all Jerusalem & Herod in another royal city visited child(toddler) at his house in Nazareth 2 yrs after birth. 
- Lie: Christmas is a Christian based holiday. Seems it's rooted in, melded with, a week long festival of Misrule called Saturnalia. 
- Lie: kill a tree & decorate it with 5 pointed star is Godly thing. According to Jeremiah 10 it's a vain Babylonian custom of fools. Seems 5 pointed star symbolizes 5 books of Moses = law, which JC allegorically called five in one house divided.
- Lie: Shepherds were watching flocks by night in mountainous fields in Israel at Christmas. According to historical records it's too cold, and flocks are in sheepfolds in valley areas in winter.
- Lie: there's a Santa Clause, elves, flying reindeer.  Etc.
So for myself, I concluded the spirit of Christmas was a spirit of error, and decided not to do Christmas; Yet I find no fault with people who still do the Christmas tradition.
