## Content

Of Bible characters, apostles, PETER is interesting, in that he got called "Satan", but prayed for & fore-told WHEN he got "converted" ('epistrepho', unlike Mt 18:3's 'strepho' child converted), THEN he'd strengthen brethren (rather than weaken with dissimulation). Seems conversion of Peter allegorically as if conversion of Satan.
Here's a few nifty things I finally notice about Peter, with the help of the Spirit of truth:
- early in 
Acts 1-5, even though Peter had been previously told to forgive 70 x7 = 490 times, he didn't forgive Ananias & Saphirra even once, but invoked the unpardonable sin (Mt 12:32 & Mk 3:29) to KILL them. The result was not good, for Acts 5:11 notes "great fear" (not love void of fear) then came upon all the church.
- even in Acts 11, where first called "Christians" at Antioch, seems Peter wasn't yet converted (and even Paul was still Saul, until Acts 13); For in Galatians 2:11-14 Paul notes Peter was to blame for Barnabas getting carried away, due to his dissimulation, aka hypocrisy of eat with Gentiles, then separate to Jews, for fear.
- Acts 12 gets ALLEGORY interesting, for then it notes Peter gets jailed & bound, for 1day called Easter, then loosed, but goes underground as a fugitive of Roman Empire, later surfaces in Italy, perhaps as 1st Pope, perhaps then using the name Cephas.... 
Jumping to Peter's time allegory in 2Peter 3:8, where he allegory says be not ignorant 1day=1000yrs & 1000yrs=1day, it seems he may be allegory referring to himself as Satan in Revelation 20:2-7, where it says Satan got bound 1000yrs (aka1day), then loosed, and shall go out to deceive the nations....
Yet in 2Peter 3:15,16 it seems Peter may also be revealing he finally got converted (as fore-told) by reading all Paul's epistles.
Interesting stuff to study, ponder, discuss
