What can we now learn about peace, in our love→joy→peace Walk in the Spirit?
1st mention of peace in OT is in Genesis 15:15, where peace = ‘shalowm’ in Hebrew, which is kinda like Jewish greet/salute ‘Shalom’ (‘shalowm’ shortened to Shalom). Problem with this peace is Gen 15:15 also refers to burial, like RIP does; thereby a grave subject, and something to remember if greeted or saluted with Shalom. It’s allegorically as if greeted/saluted with their Law ↓Peace↓←|→ instead of Grace→Mercy→↑Peace↑.
Islamic greet/salute of ‘Peace be upon you’ is similar, as it’s like saying Allah’s Law be upon you; So, it’s like false Peace of their law, rather than graceful and merciful peace.
Hindu greet/salute ‘Namaste’ (I bow to the divine in you) seems Nice; Yet along with a Buddhist cult-ural greet/salute comes their con-cept of karma, which is not merciful.
Even so-called Christ-ians (still more like Jesus-ians) will either offer you or sign off discussion with ‘peace’; Not (yet) knowing the kind of temporal peace they offer can also be taken away, like forgiven in Matthew 18:23-35’s forgiven + unforgiven + tormented (conditional love not yet amazing grace).

- Luke 12:51 notes Jesus did _not come to give peace on earth, but division_.
- Yes, peace and Peace occur 36 times in Mt, Mk, Lk, Jn; But it’s tricky stuff.

Apostles & followers of Jesus were of “_one accord_” in Acts, but such Law Peace accord didn’t work out well for Ananias & Sapphira: killed for lying to Holy Ghost (Holy Law); Which in Matthew 12:32 & Mark 3:29 is clarified as unpardonable (unforgiveable) sin. Seems Peter, previously told to forgive 70 x 7 = 490 times, didn’t forgive them once; But invoked Ghostly Law to bring great (law) fear to donor$ & convert$. So perhaps the ‘accord’ synonym for peace needs more clarifying. So let’s clarify, clarify, clarify, until there is no more confusion, but peace; And peace that lasts instead of temporal peace.
What about the God of peace? Seems even a “God of peace” NT mentioned 5 times has to be clarified, until it’s your “whole spirit and soul and body” made “blameless” unto the coming of “our” Lord “Jesus→Christ”. For save the world is not in 2SonsJesus mode, but “through him”: John 3:17…”through his name”: John 20:31… “through Jesus→Christ”: Romans x6; Yet also twice in Romans it clarifies peace with God and joy in God is “through our Lord Jesus Christ”, so then uses “our” Lord JC, not just “the” Lord JC. Same for “victory” in 1Corinthians 15:57; it’s notably “through our Lord Jesus Christ”.
So it’s noticeable, that although Apostle Paul generally begins his epistles with a holy kiss greeting of “Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ”; He also generally ends his epistles with “the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ”. Seems he even ↑upgrades↑ his holy kiss greeting to include mercy and our Lord JC in 1Timothy 1:2 KJV: “Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord”. Seems then the “peace” aspect is not just 2/3, but 3/3, thereby not just a temporal peace that can be taken away, like in Rev 1:4→Rev 6:4, but a lasting peace.
So we should not only look at the difference between Law Peace and Grace Peace, but also the difference between Grace & peace=2/3 ←&→ Grace, mercy, peace=3/3; For in Revelation 1:4 there’s Grace & peace offered (from whom?), but this peace is later taken in Rev 6:4.
