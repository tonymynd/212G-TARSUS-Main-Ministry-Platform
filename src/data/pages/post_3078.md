## Content

CHARITY [agape 2]: 1Corinthians 13 KJV.
and
LOVE [agape 1]: Matthew 24:12 KJV.
QUESTION:
Is there an 'agape' contrast portrayed in KJV to notice?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God, our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord. 
The KJV (7th English Bible) uses BOTH "love" and "charity" for the Greek 'agape' in New Testament, Yet allegory as if for contrast. 
For example, 
First mention of 'agape', by Jesus, in Matthew 24:12 KJV says:
"And because iniquity shall abound, 
the love [agape] of many shall wax cold". 
note: seems to allegory portray going backward of these 3:
Cold ←> LukeWarm → Hot
vs
For 'agape' in 1Corinthians 13 KJV, it is called "charity":
13:5 (charity: agape) "thinketh no evil"...
13:8 Charity [agape] never faileth, but
whether (there be) prophecies, they shall fail,
whether (there be) tongues, they shall cease,
whether (there be) knowledge [gnosis], it shall vanish away. 
So, in KJV, 
there seems to be a "love"[agape] & "charity"[agape] contrast.
Love not only can, but "shall" wax cold for many: Mt 24:12.
Charity never faileth (never waxes cold for any): 1Cor 13:8.
But most modern Bibles, by Y2Ks, have changed "charity" to "love" for 'agape', which then seems to contrast itself in most Bibles:
Love [agape] shall wax cold: Mt 24:12.
Love [agape] never fails: 1Cor 13:8.
KJV first uses "charity" for 'agape' in 1Cor 8:1 KJV:
"Now as touching things offered unto idols, we know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge [gnosis] puffeth up, but charity [agape] edifieth".
KJV then notes in 1Cor 13:4 that "charity" is "not puffed up".
KJV notes in 1Cor 13:8 knowledge [gnosis] shall vanish away.
KJV, in Colossians 3:12-14, notes "charity" [agape] can be new man put on above 7 things (as if 8th above 7), even above forgiving one another, as then called the bond of perfectness [teleiotes].
In 1Cor 13:13 KJV it says:
And now abideth faith, hope, charity [agape], these three; but the greatest of these is charity [agape].
note:
seems "charity" allegory "greatest" of great, greater, greatest.
As if in contrast, for "love", it speaks only of great & greater:
John 15:13
"Greater love [agape] hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends".
What are your thoughts & insights about 
KJV's "love" [agape] & "charity" [agape] use?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ (be) with you all. Amen.
