## Description

Is too much marketing data making everything look the same? 🎤 Ahead of J2’s Marketing Day at UKREiiF, we sat down with Rory Sutherland to get his take on what happens when every business uses the same data, tracks the same metrics, and optimises in the same direction. Safe? Maybe. Smart?…

## Transcript
One of the problems you have in any large business is people love data not necessarily for its actual value, but for its defensive capabilities, that if you've made a data-driven decision, it can always be presented as rational and you'll never be blamed for making it.

However, there's a problem in business in that it's much much easier to get fired for being irrational than it is for being unimaginative. And the problem with data is all big data comes in the same place, the past. All big data, by the way, is also highly up unrepresentative because there are a lot of very important emotional features about any kind of property which don't fit into a spreadsheet because you can't really quantify them.

Consequently, all companies, which for often defensive purposes, become obsessively dated driven, become unoriginal, they're fixated on the past, they're incapable of innovation, but also they're very frequently optimised for the wrong things because it doesn't matter at first. If you optimise for a new metric, you know, Year 1 it's probably quite useful. Year 10, you've been doing it optimising for exactly the same thing for 10 years. Most of your competitors are optimising for exactly the same metric. Suddenly what you've got is a kind of corporate isomorphism where all businesses resemble each other and their products resemble each other, their approach to pricing resembles each other. And at that point you've got the worst kind of market, which is a market which is commodified and poorly differentiated.
