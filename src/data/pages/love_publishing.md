# # 17. What about LOVE? (Part 1/2)

(Continuation study from “Set your affection on things above.” Colossians 3:3 AKJV)

Love is the 1st aspect of fruit of the Spirit and the 1st step in our Walk before we learn to run the race of grace of 1 Cor 9:24 all the way to charity the perfect:

[This] I say then, Walk in the Spirit, and ye shall not fulfil the lust of the flesh. - Gal 5:16 KJV

This Walk is not crawling like children being tossed ←to and fro→ Eph 4:14, Is a Big Walk, as a Big Talk for a grown man (not a child). Since the heir, as long as he is a child, differs nothing from a servant (still under tutors and governors) Gal 4:1;2

John Lennon in his song “Love is real, Real is love” ya, but seems that many have their own meaning of love, and when they get in a relationship, this multi-face love tends to wax cold, but:

“God dwelleth in us, and his love is perfected in us.” 1 Jo 4:12.

So, love can be perfected from an unperfected state, which announces a transition from imperfect to “perfect love casteth out fear: because fear hath torment. He that feareth is not made perfect in love”, and the source of fear is law, the great terror-ist, Deu 34-12, something that “God is love” would not have ever.

This fear, linked to law as a source of terror, reveals that living under law leads to fear, rather than experiencing freedom and release from fear that comes from Walking in God's perfect love.

Yet “perfect love” still has potential to be confused with failing love (gets cOLD), but AKJV translators made a cleaver and crucial distinction when translating Greek word ‘agape’ as both “love” & “charity”, let’s compare for AWARENESS:

 “the love of many shall wax cold” (it fails) Matthew 24:12  
“Charity never faileth” (never waxes cOLD) 1 Corinthians 13:8

Many bible versions do not make this distinction, they translated all instances of ‘agape’ as ‘love’ falling in “contrary” things which Christ blot out Col 2:14, for is like saying love fails and love never fails, con-fusion:

“For God is not [the author] of confusion, but of peace, as in all churches of the saints.” - 1Co 14:33 KJV

Let’s continue learning more about love to better locate its position as a base aspect of the fruit. There are many instances where the word ‘love’ is used, but under the hood, the Greek words used differ widely in their meanings:

10 kinds of Love:

1. agapao: Mt 5:43 (still has enemies, but Christ abolished enmity Eph 2:15)

Ye have heard that it hath been said, Thou shalt love thy neighbour, and hate thine enemy.

2. phileo: Mt 6:5 (still has leaven of pharisees Luk 12:1)

And when thou prayest, thou shalt not be as the hypocrites [are]: for they love to pray standing in the synagogues and in the corners of the streets, that they may be seen of men. Verily I say unto you, They have their reward.

3. agape1: Mt 24:12 LawLove=WaxCold (it fails)

And because iniquity shall abound, the love of many shall wax cold.

4. thelo: Mk 12:38 (still based in appearances, but they can be deceiving)

And he said unto them in his doctrine, Beware of the scribes, which love to go in long clothing,

5. aspasmos: Mk 12:38 (LoveOfGreetings)

 and [love] salutations in the marketplaces,

6. philadelphia: Rom 12:10 (still one←→another duality)

[Be] kindly affectioned one to another with brotherly love; in honour preferring one another;

7. agape2: 1Cor 8:1 GraceLove=NeverFails

Now as touching things offered unto idols, we know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge puffeth up, but charity edifieth.

8. philargyria: 1Tim6:10 LoveOfMoney

For the love of money is the root of all evil: which while some coveted after, they have erred from the faith, and pierced themselves through with many sorrows.

9. philadelphos: 1Pet 3:8 LoveOfBrethren

Finally, [be ye] all of one mind, having compassion one of another, love as brethren, [be] pitiful, [be] courteous:

10. agape3: teleios agape: 1Jn 4:8 PerfectLove(God)

He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love.

# 17. What about LOVE? (Part 2/2)

17. What about LOVE? (Part 2/2)

(Continuation study from “Set your affection on things above.” Colossians 3:3 AKJV) Original study: https://t.ly/CHU-I

To All: Grace, Mercy and Peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.

I want to thank all that have endured with me in this study about the foundational aspect of the Fruit of the Spirit in Gal 5:22-23 AKJV: "love", As if the Spirit of Grace, through perfect love (charity), edifies us from the base level of imperfect love to higher levels of spiritual maturity:

Knowledge puffeth up, but charity edifieth. ~ 1Co 8:1 AKJV

Let's remember some the key points from previous study re "love" part 1/2:

- Comparing love 'agape' vs charity 'agape' is as comparing allegories contrasting law and grace for our learning Gal 4:22-24

- Love can be perfected from an imperfect state to a perfect love that casts out fear. (1 John 4:18)

- Love is the first aspect of the fruit of the Spirit mentioned in Gal 5:22-23.

- The KJV Bible translators used both "love" and "charity" for 'agape' to distinguish between two types of love, one that can wax cold and one that never fails. Mat 24:12, 1 Cor13:8

- There are 10 different types of "love" in the New Testament, each with diverse meanings, e.g. 1Jo 4:8, Mat 5:43, 6:5, 24:12, Mar 12:38, Rom 12:10, 1Ti 6:10, 1Pe 3:8, etc.

- Love can be perfected, transforming us from an imperfect mode to one that casts out fear. 1 John 4:18.

- When we hear "Jesus Loves You" or "God Loves You", yes God is love, but we are still called to discern which Jesus, which God, and which love of twain to a-void being deceived, Mat 24:4 or fall into in con-fusion, of what God is not the author. 1 Cor 14:33

Let's continue with our Walk through love.

Now, is not a small "walk before me" or else, but a follow me: led by the Spirit...Gal 5:18

As we continue our exploration of love, let's expand a little more on how the AKJV Bible translators, learned men (neither popish nor protestant, who sought to make God's holy truth more widely known) who employed both, "love" and "charity" to distinguish between two types of love found in the New Testament e.g. love 'agape' waxes cOLD Mat 24:12 ←vs→ charity 'agape' never fails 1 Co 13:8.

This choice was not made arbitrarily but to preserve the allegory that runs throughout the Scripture by contrasting law and grace ( see AKJV Bible Translators' Notes to Readers).

As we Walk in the Spirit, we discover that love is merely the first step in our journey toward perfection. Our understanding of love must grow in grace, from a natural, fleshly love that can fail to a spiritual, perfect love that casts out fear and hatred. This transition is not immediate but a gradual process of growth and learning, as we move from being children under tutors and governors to mature adults who no longer differ from servants Gal 4:1-2.

Ultimately, our study of love should lead us to recognize that true, perfect love is not rooted in the law but in the grace, mercy and peace from God our father.

Unperfect love rooted in law still has judgment (jeopardy every our):

"Therefore thou art inexcusable, O man, whosoever thou art that judgest:..." Rom 2:1, Rom 5:20

Perfect love is perfected by "God of all grace" but notice how "ye" still suffers in 1 Pet 5:10 but is "you" who gets perfected, we'll make another study about ye/you mystery.

Once love is perfected, then no more fighting, warring, striving, etc.; but we get to inner-stand* how perfect joints grace and mercy which begets peace, because "that which is perfect is come, then that which is in part shall be done away" 1 Cor 13:10

* inner-stand is higher than under-stand, which still has an undertaker.

Love was nice, but let's evaluate some issues with love before we step up to "joy"

Love gets commanded Joh 15:17, also portrayed as part of the two great commandments where all the law and the prophets got hanged Mat 22:36-40 but "law and prophets" have an expiry date "until John" Mat 11:13 since that time the kingdom of God(perfect love) is preached since the KofG is "within you" because "...[it is] a good thing that the heart be established with grace..." Heb 13:9.

Commanded love tends to wax cOLD due to iniquity(lawlessness and/or lawfulness both still contain law) Mat 24:12 being the source of poison that kills any trail of love to the point of death 1 Cor 15:56.

This is why Apostle Paul questions this commandment of "love thy neighbour" in Romans 7:

"Wherefore the law [is] holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good. Was then that which is good made death unto me? God forbid. But sin, that it might appear sin, working death in me by that which is good; that sin by the commandment might become exceeding sinful." ~ Rom 7:12;13

In conclusion, law-love, unperfected, source of "fear hath torment" then we find that commanded or imperfect love rooted in the law ultimately fails, as it can wax cold over time. However, perfect love that is perfected by God's grace never fails. Let's rehearse:
