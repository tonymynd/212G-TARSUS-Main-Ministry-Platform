## Content

ROMANS -- having 7 Amen's is allegory like a 7 course meal. An interesting thing served, to chew on: Romans 12:2A says be "transformed", as if from a caterpillar to a butterfly, by reNEWing of your mind, as if from Law to Grace; Then 12:2B gives the reason: to then be able to PROVE there's not just 2 aspects to the will of God, but 3: "good, and acceptable, and perfect".  Hmmm,.. 
Seems if only 1/3 or even 2/3 of 3, still counts as if 
"nothing" yet.
Seems why, in Matthew 5:48, JC didn't say be good, nor even acceptable, but "Be ye therefore PERFECT, as your Father which is in heaven is PERFECT ('teleios', of 7 NT kinds of perfect). 
Seems why Apostle Paul, in 1Cor 13 KJV, twice mentions being "nothing" [yet] without charity, of these 3: faith, hope, charity; Allegory as if nothing yet till greatest of great, greater, greatest.
Seems why Apostle Paul told the Colossians to also read the epistle from Laodicea, which is 1Timothy; Which in 1Tim 2:1-3 firstly lists things "good and acceptable" (2/3 of 3 in Rom 12:2B), then 1Tim 2:4 gets to the 3rd of 3: God our Saviour WILL have all men saved AND come unto the knowledge ('epignosis') of the truth; Perhaps as if to allegory say the 'gnosis' knowledge of gnostic believers is only 2/3 = 0.666...to infinity of "nothing" yet.
So the shew goes on.
