**SelfControl Book: Chapter 5:**

**Longsuffering: 4th Fruit**

Love→Joy→Peace→leads us to & through **Longsuffering**→unto Gentleness….

Yet, prior to Gentleness, let’s see what can we now learn about Longsuffering?

Seems _longsuffering_ is Greek rendered as both a

-        **noun:** ‘**makrothymia’:** patience, endurance, constancy, steadfastness, perseverance, forbearance,longsuffering, slowness in avenging wrongs: longsuffering x12, patience x2 = 14.

-        **verb:** ‘**makrothymeo’:** to be of a long spirit, not to lose heart, to persevere patiently and bravely in enduring misfortunes and troubles,  to be patient in bearing the offenses and injuries of others: be patient x3, have patience x2, have long patience x1, bear long x1, suffer long x1, be longsuffering x1, patiently endure x1 = 10.

Either way, noun or verb, it has a lot to do with patience & endurance. The verb is also rendered as_endured_. Perhaps it’s like saying, as Paul says in Hebrews 6:15 about what God sware to Abraham:_Surely_ _blessing I will bless thee, and multiplying I will multiply thee._ _So,_ _AFTER he had patiently endured__, he obtained the promise_. So Paul also says in Hebrews 10:36, _ye have need of patience, that, AFTER ye have done the will of God, ye might receive the promise_. Perhaps he’s firstly allegory referring to the Seed: Christ: the end of the law; Which allows us to thereby also lay hold on eternal life [higher calling], after fighting the good fight of faith [lower calling]).

So to get to what comes AFTER, we firstly l-o-n-g-suffer.
