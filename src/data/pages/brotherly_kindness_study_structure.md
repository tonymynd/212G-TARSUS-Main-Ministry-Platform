Here’s a suggested refinement and title that maintains your PEP (Positive–Evaluate–Positive) flow, keeping clarity, strength, and scriptural integrity:

### **Title: “Philadelphia: The Greater Fold before Charity”**

---

### **INTRO**  
Grace, mercy, and peace to you. We follow the divine sequence from 2 Peter 1:5–11, reaching _brotherly kindness_ (_philadelphia_)—the sixth grace-fold before charity. Last time, we examined _godliness_ (eusebeia), meaning “well-worship” or reverence toward God. Now we unfold _philadelphia_, love of the brethren—unfeigned and incorruptible.

### **POINT**  
Philadelphia is the living proof of the godly man. As written, “declaring the end from the beginning” (Isaiah 46:10), brotherly kindness points toward charity. This is not sentiment, but a pattern of grace: true fellowship not in knowledge, but in the Spirit of Christ.

---

#### **Why After Godliness, Before Charity**  
Godliness makes a man aligned with God; _philadelphia_ makes him aligned with those in Christ. Without it, charity has no proper stage. Just as Christ is the only begotten of the Father (godliness), He is also the firstborn among many brethren (brotherly kindness), and finally, the Head of the Body (charity, the bond of perfectness).

#### **What Opposes Philadelphia**  
The opposite is division, partiality, puffing up in knowledge. Jews had sects (Pharisees, Sadducees, etc.), and the Corinthians exalted gnosis—knowledge eaten without love, causing offence (1 Cor 8:1-12). The "conscience" of law (good and evil) is not _epignosis_, but duality—a science of oppositions (1 Tim 6:20). Philadelphia is unity in the Spirit.

#### **How It's Relevant Today**  
Today, while some destroy by law (e.g., Israel over Gaza), the church is called to build by grace. God shows no partiality (James 3:17): peaceable, gentle, full of mercy. Arabs, Jews, Gentiles—all can be added to the body of Christ. Our role is not to take sides, but to edify _all_ brethren through _philadelphia_—the greater fold of grace.

---

### **Summary**  
Philadelphia is not the end—it is the grace before the greatest. Godliness brought us into union with the Father; brotherly kindness joins us to the Body; charity is the love of Christ flowing through all.

### **Repass the POINT**  
Let _philadelphia_ be seen not just in doctrine but in our daily walk. Bear one another's burdens, speak truth in love, be kindly affectioned with brotherly love (Romans 12:10). This is grace lived.

#### **Closing Words**  
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ is with you all. Amen. Let us press on toward the happy end, not by law, but by the manifold grace of God, unto charity—the bond of perfectness.
