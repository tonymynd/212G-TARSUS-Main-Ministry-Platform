## Content

When it's "both good and evil", it's not only law, but ends bad.
Perhaps the following examples will help people realize such:
Eg: Matthew 12:30-37 (twain things that all end bad)
- with and against... ends against
- 
gather and scatter... ends scatter
- forgiven and unforgiven... ends unforgiven
- tree fruit: good and corrupt... ends corrupt
- man: good man and evil man... ends evil man
- justified and condemned... ends condemned
Eg: John 5:29 (good and evil resurrections)
resurrection of life and resurrection of damnation... ends bad
Eg: 1John 4:6
spirit of truth and spirit of error... ends error  
Seems if both, correct order is lie/truth = ends truth.
Eg: John 1:17 contrasts both law/grace as if lie/truth.
