Of Two Faiths: This/That Faiths: That Faith is Come  

The Bible is [allegoric](Allegory.md). It compares [two faiths](TwainShew2.md#Faiths) as if blind faith vs seeing faith; allegorically trust in Moses' law vs God's evidential grace. Of the two faiths: this/that faiths compared, Paul states "[that faith is come](ShewBread3.md#thatfaith)" (Galatians 3:25), as if "[that Jesus Christ is come](GodShew3.md#Second)" (1John 4:2): As a "better" thing of good/better things provided for them/us ([Hebrews 11](Hebrews12.md):40), and like the law/grace of Moses/JC is also compared as lie/truth (John 1:17). Such an allegorical comparative teaching (Eg: John 5:45) is biblically called a [shew](shew.md): allegory comparison of [contrary things](ContraryThings.md).

Two Faiths to [Compare](ShewBread8.md#Dare): "[from faith to faith](GraceWalk.md)"  
This Faith: trust in Moses' law: John 5:45  
"That Faith": trust in God's grace: Gal's 3  

  
Allegorically "that faith" [of this/that] reckoned to "Abraham" [of Abram/Abraham] is also "[the faith](EpistleDedicatory.md) of the Son of God" by which we all are saved and live (in Christ: "the end of the law": in him there is "no sin", therefore [no sting of sin: death](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md)). "That Faith" is come is also biblically called "The Faith". King James is called Defender of The Faith in the [Epistle Dedicatory](EpistleDedicatory.md) of [Translators of the Bible](BibleTranslators.md).  
  

"[That Faith" is Come](ShewBread3.md#thatfaith) = "The Faith": of the Son of God  
Defender of The Faith = Defender of That Faith is Come  

  
Mike Williams, of [Gospelrevolution](http://www.gospelrevolution.com/), notes in audio tapes of grace conferences: if it's not your sin which makes you a sinner (rather the sin of one man), why think it's your faith which makes you saved (rather it's the faith of one man, the faith of the Son of God)...summary of Mike's take on 'one man vs one man' in Romans.  
  

[The Law](RevelatorySermons23.md) is [not of faith](BiblicalDots.md): Galatians 3:12.  
Without faith you can't please God: Hebrews 11:6.  
So begin your faith>hope>[charity](GMP/Notes/GodShew/Charity.md) with [Grace](GraceUntoYou.md);  
Unless you want your hope to become hopeless.  

Romans 1:17 speaks of the righteousness of God being [revealed](CurrentEvents.md), and revealed "from faith to faith", from this faith to that faith (to see better in greater light which rules the day); As "we all" are [changed](biblicalemblems.md) from [glory to glory](TwainShew3.md#Glorys) (bondage to liberty), by "[that Spirit](ShewBread5.md#IAMTHAT)" who's "now" [the Lord](Usward.md) (2Corinthians 3). Such "from faith to faith" is as taking a [grace walk](GraceWalk.md) ([through](through.md) the valley of the "[shadow](Law.md)" of death)  [from mtn to mtn](TwainShew4.md#Mountains)  ([from Sinai to Sion](Sevens2.md)): ([Hebrews 12](Hebrews13.md)); But fearing no [evil](BiblicalDots.md), for a "shadow" is only a phantom menace, an illusion witch can become a delusion; And when we all from such turn away from it, then it vanishes (like the end of the [Merlin movie](Insights.md#Movies)). For we are Pauline told: what's ready to vanish: Heb 8:13 shall vanish: 1Cor13:8.  
  

[Hebrews](Hebrews.md) is like a [mini bible](GodShew2.md#Hebrews) itself, firstly compares twain [ages](TwainShew.md#Ages): time past/last days, then also exhorts to "go on" (from twain "principles") ... [us-ward](Usward.md)... to [perfection](GraceUntoYou.md):  
as a [Good > Better > Best](GodShew.md#GBB) and [Great > Greater > Greatest](GodShew.md#Greatest) chat.  

  
In Hebrews 11, which many 'word of faith' preachers oft use to spout their oxymoron mixture of faiths ([unaware](ShewBread7.md#Bliss) of the comparison and conclusion therein), we not only have a theme: comparison of faiths then/now, noting blind faith heroes trusting in [law](Law.md) endead in a hall of shame (all died and rec'd not the promise) for playing the blame game (law is a ministration of condemnation), but also a conclusion stating: God having provided some "better" thing for "[us](ShewBread2.md#justus)"; And the theme verse states what faith is "now" (of then/now):  
  

Now faith (that faith, the faith) is  
 "[the substance](Graphically.md)" of things hoped for,  
 "the [evidence of things not seen](http://community.livejournal.com/stargate100/141742)".

  
The faith of the Son of God is knowing [what's evident from the evidence](CurrentEvents.md), and taking heed (Mt 24:4,5), even more earnest heed (Heb 2:1), so as not to stumble in [greater light](TwainShew4.md#Lights) than darkness, not be deceived by [many](ShewBread.md#contraryto). The goal is to get understanding, thereby never be duped, tempted, victimized, bewitched, ensnared; nor look back (as Lot's wife), let alone turn back (as John in Revelation) to [law](Law.md) which is "behind" [you](YeYou.md) when taking a grace walk from [Mt Sinai to Mt Sion](Sevens2.md); not even if such a "great voice" trumpets "great" things, like "great" mystery, or "great" tribulation, for such is also a "great" terror (Deuteronomy 34:12).

                            Grace is eternal, immortal.

The Faith is not only better of good/better, but best of good/better/best.  

The Faith, aside from being better of two faiths: good/better faiths (2 things), as New Testament a "better testament" of [two testaments](TwainShew6.md#Testaments) having a "[better hope](TwainShew3.md#Hopes)" of [seven NT hopes](Sevens5.md). "The Faith" is eventually best of good better best (3 things), and greatest of great greater greatest: [best](Best.md) for all, greatest of all (not some).  
  
                            The (better) faith of two faiths compared  
                            gets you unto hopeful, of hopeless/hopeful;  
                            And hopeful gets you to [charity never fails](Charity7.md).  
  
When comparing two things, like [law law](TwainShew4.md#Laws) in Romans 8, such is as comparing good and better for some, one good for them, one better for us of them/us; But when comparing three things, one is notably greatest. Charity never fails, because it's "charity out of a pure heart", which is "the end of the commandment": love one another, "as I have love [you](YeYou.md)". For as in 1Pet 5:10, and as by "[the God](GodShew3.md#Only) of [all grace](GodShew2.md#Allness)" (no law at all), [ye becomes you](YeYou.md): afterward "strengthened" > "stablished" > "settled" in "the faith", "[that faith](ShewBread3.md#thatfaith)", "the faith of the Son of God", which results in being risen and [seated](Revelations7.md#21) (Col 3:1-3), at [rest](OneLiners.md#dannymiles) (peace) at the right hand of God, as what's right with God: His grace.
