FAITH
7th aspect of The Fruit, and 11 aspects to go till "charity" The Greatest.
To ALL: Grace, Mercy and Peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Love→Joy→Peace→Longsuffering→Gentleness→Goodness→Faith = 7th fruit of Spirit. (attribution, study guide and spiritual aspects map in the comments)
Exercising our senses while we step up our Walk in the Spirit, now studying the concept of faith in Gal 5:22-23, being the 7th asif reaching perfection, that is, by the God of all grace, to "make you perfect, stablish, strengthen, settle you."
Notice "perfect" here is preceded by stablish, strengthen, settle. Seems to allegory tell us "perfectly joined together", set ready and fully restored to innerstand how and why we can now be stablish, strengthen, settle.
---
Faith is a HUGE topic. The word “faith” occurs 247 times, yet only twice in OT. So it’s primarily a NT topic, now our 7th fruit of the Spirit topic to study, ponder, learn about.
NT “faith” in Greek is always ‘pisits’, except when called “little faith”, which may be allegory for law faith, or childish belief. The word “faith” (‘pistis’: feminine noun) is described as a conviction of the truth of anything: belief; Also English rendered as “assurance” x1, “belief” x1, “fidelity” x1, as if continually assuring us of our salvation through sanctification of the Spirit unto belief of the truth, which involves let go of lies we may have firstly erroneously believed to be true, such as a childish Boogie Man.
So beyond “belief of the truth” is “know the truth”, as an upgrade from believe to know.
But first, let’s Review:
As we’ve been Led of the Spirit, through Fruit of the Spirit, beginning with Love being the “commandment” (rule, ordinance, law) to Love one another; We’ve now seen there was a notable goodness1/goodness2 difference from 5th(midst) & to 6th fruit,
Perhaps(?) a 5/4 ratio change (like ‘chrestotes/agathosune’ 5/4 ratio of NT “goodness”):
FROM these 5: Love, Joy, Peace, Longsuffering, Gentleness(goodness1)=midst
TO-WARD these 4: Goodness, Faith, Meekness Temperance,
Perhaps(?) as if a transition per se, maybe allegory as if the Spirit has been leading us forward: From under the law →To→ under grace has better things, as if upgrading. For Galatians 5:18 KJV had said “if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law”; And Romans 6:14/15 had said ye/we are not under the law, but under grace. Seems, our Walk in the Spirit maybe as if From needed governance / To less needed governance, b/c we’re being fed spiritual food we can digest & apply, by what we’ve been learning.
So, as we’ve seen in the previous chapter, there was an interesting NT 5/4 ratio of goodness1/goodness2, which may(?) even allegory suggest there’s a 5/4 ratio of 9 fruit. For 5th=midst gentleness fruit still notably had both “goodness and severity”; And not as contrasting goodness or severity, but as if commanding Love’s goodness1 being conditional on u be good, else severity = cut off. So 5th gentleness fruit aspect was still kinda like law = both good and evil, by still having both goodness and severity.
So it seems first 5 fruit aspects may(?) have been allegory portraying still under the law, till past midst. Then 6th as goodness2 was as goodness only; Perhaps allegory as if going on From under law / To under grace as if go on to better things God provided for us, of them/us; Or as if going From left hand(side) / To right hand(side) of plural “hands” of a PluralGod on high. Something to ponder as we now go on to our discussion about faith.
Re faith:
So now, when look at, study “faith” fruit, it may become as if Paul’s allegoric faith upgrade: “from faith / to faith” in Romans 1:16,17; Where Paul first notes his gospel (of Grace & peace) is now “the gospel of Christ” (as if gone through Jesus→Christ), notably a gospel in which he’s “not ashamed”.
Why?
First he 1:16 says it’s the power (dunamis) of God unto salvation, to the (singular) Jew first, and also to the Greek; Then also 1:17 says: For “therein (the gospel of Christ) is the righteousness of God revealed from faith to faith”; Perhaps as if from “great faith” under law to greater faith under grace, as if growing in faith, by grace, from justified “by faith” as circumcision were, to justified “through faith” as uncircumcision are, as noted in Romans 3:29,30, about how one God is both the God of the Jews by faith & the God of the Gentiles through faith.
Clever wording.
But what does it mean?
Seems Paul suggests by faith is by promise, so not of the law, nor by works of the law; And through faith is as going or growing from your faith to the faith of the Son of God. So in our Introduction Chapter 1 we had 2 lists of upgrades, first 9 fruit of the Spirit in Gal 5:22,23, then also 7 things to “add to your faith” in 2Pet 1:5-10; Since in 1Peter 1 it said, re “your faith”: it’s only for the salvation of your soul, of soul/spirit, perhaps as if to suggest there’s also a spirit salvation to go on unto, beyond salvation of your soul. For even when in spirit mode, of soul/spirit, there’s still this to do: “comparing spiritual things (plura) with spiritual”. Why? Because law is both natural & spiritual, yet also both good & evil, so law may also be natural distress & spiritual wickedness afterward.
Re: their faith:
Seems, on the topic of faith, most faith preachers use Hebrews 11, wherein “by faith” is mentioned 17 times, and “through faith” also mentioned 5 times; Yet most faith preachers, who say you can’t please their rewarder God without their faith (from Hebrews 11:6), never seem to mention Paul’s conclusions in Hebrews 11:13,39 are:
11:13 these all died (1st of 2 lists of OT law faith heroes), “not having received the promises”, and
11:39 these all (2nd list, also all died), having obtained a good report “through faith”, (but yet stil) “received not the promise”:
Seems as if Paul’s revealing oops1 & oops2, both by faith & through faith, if still law faith. So Heb 11:40 finally concludes God provided some better thing for us (of them/us), as if something better than all dying and neither receiving the promises, nor the promise.
For Hebrews 10:36 previously talked of “receive the promise” after do the will of God, which seems to be I will have mercy, and not sacrifice, as if to allegory say have faith in grace, not in law. For Gal 3:11 said what’s evident from evidence is that no man justified “by the law”, and Heb 10:8 says sacrifice and offering, for sin, is offered “by the law”.
So re faith, or rather firstly re what’s “not of faith”,
let’s connect these dots in two different verses by Paul:
whatsoever is NOT OF FAITH is sin: Romans 14:23 KJV,
and the law is NOT OF FAITH: Galatians 3:12 KJV.
Seems why, in John 5:45 KJV, JC pointed out to law believers that he (as grace & truth) would not accuse them, but one they trusted (aka had faith in), Moses (law giver), does accuse them to the Father. So it’s like revealing law = accuser of brethren, and can’t be trusted. Also seems, by his twice stoop to write on ground, in John 8, where they brought a woman caught in adultery, such awareness of law = accuser, convicted their conscience. Interesting to notice “conscience” begins in John 8:9, and can be “evil conscience” till last mention of “conscience” is only a “good conscience” toward God in 1Peter 3:21.
So people in general, whose faith still in law, are more like am I (?), than like I am (!).
Paul’s allegory example of such: “when I am (grace) weak, then am I (law) strong”.
It’s interesting to notice sometimes a verse may say “am I” instead of “I am”.
