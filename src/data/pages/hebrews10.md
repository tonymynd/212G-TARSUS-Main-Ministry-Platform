Hebrews 9:26 For then must he often have suffered since the foundation of the world: but now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.  
  
Notably we're talking a [once only](TwainShew6.md#Servants) eternal redemption instead of yearly redemption by high priests who themselves died and stayed dead; And also notably about the end of the world, [the old world](ShewBread7.md#Worlds) of law where sin was imputed to all whether or not they sinned and then such was 'covered up' with a bloody sacrifice by law, which was an accusation-all ministration of condemnation and a ministration of death, which came upon all: Romans 5:14.  
  
Notably we're also talking ["hath he" (a question)](SAQs.md) instead of he hath (a statement). We're also talking "appeared" to (ass-u-me, [appearances can be deceiving](Easter2.md)) instead of did (fact). Let us reconsider our [Christ doctrine](GodShew5.md#Peace) and see there are [two finisheds](TwainShew2.md#Finisheds): [before the cross vs at the cross](RevelatorySermons31.md), and that the latter end may be [better](GodShew.md#GBB) or [worse](Allegory3.md) accordingly for [them](ShewBread2.md#inthem) or [us](ShewBread2.md#justus). Them, even them on the right of them vs them, tend to draw back to [sacrifice](ShewBread5.md#willx2), perdition thereof, like a [dog](RevelatorySermons26.md) returned to his vomit, making their [latter end](LatterEnd.md) ["worse"](Allegory3.md). Converted Peter speaks to such in [2Pet 2:20-22](http://www.apostolic-churches.net/bible/av/2pe002) (noting latter end is worse when those escaped get entangled again with law<=law: Rev 13). JC also spoke to such in Mt 12:45 (noting if the house and the cup aren't [totally cleansed](Reconciliation.md) to the point the [conscience is purged](Hebrews10.md) of sin, the demonic spirit then returns with [seven spirits](Revelations9.md) "MORE WICKED") and also in Mt 23:15 (noting the proselyted one is made "MORE THE CHILD of hell" than the previous child of hell: hypocrites, fools who sit in [Moses' seat](Allegory4.md)).  Paul also exhorts: [be no more children](GodShew.md#nomorechildren) tossed to and fro, rather man kind which is [better blessed](TwainShew.md#Blesseds). For neither of two (dead/living) [sacrifices](TwainShew6.md#Sacrifices), nor two [laws](TwainShew4.md#Laws), is able to purge the conscience of sin. Hence we are exhorted to [leave the (good/better) "principles"](Hebrews7.md) to go on unto [perfection](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md) ([best](GodShew.md#GBB)).  
  
Hebrews 9:27 And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment:  
  
Notably we can rule out reincarnation here, for men once die, then comes the judgment. Yet also notable is the use of "as", denoting allegory in the manner of "as in Adam all die", an as if.  
  
Hebrews 9:28 So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many; and unto them that look for him shall he appear the second time without sin unto salvation.  
  
Notably we have a few twists and a zinger to such a great mystery being revealed. Perhaps the Christ offered to bear the sins of many was the Christ of [Christ Jesus](GodShew5.md), not of [Jesus Christ](ShewBread.md#contraryto) (in that Christ, of [that Jesus Christ is come](GodShew3.md#Second), whom [that God](ShewBread5.md#IAMTHAT) was in [reconciling the world](Reconciliation.md), "there is no sin" even imputed to even bear. Selah). To wit, that God was in Christ (of Jesus>Christ) reconciling the world unto himself, NOT IMPUTING SIN (2Cor 5:19). The last half of the last verse is a question (shall he?), not a statement (he shall!). And it's a question many "saints in Christ Jesus" should ask them-selves; POINT being sin not "purged" gives place and power to return of [law worketh wrath](Law.md) (law being both source of sin and strength of sin's death sting) when [a 2nd beast gives place & power to a 1st beast](Revelations8.md#26), by such better of two laws (greater damnation thereof) and such better of sacrifices gives place to the first sort of such twain laws: the vengeful "unjust judge" which notably takes vengeance on those asking to be avenged (lawed). Selah.
