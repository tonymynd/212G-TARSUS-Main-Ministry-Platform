## Content

Interesting Bible things about 'agape' ...
Grace, Mercy, Peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
1John 4:8 &16 twice says God is love [agape]. Yet 1John oddly has no mention of grace, nor of mercy, nor of peace; But does mention a day of judgment [krisis: damnation, accusation, condemnation] in 1John 4:17, and in 4:18 mentions it takes perfect [teleioo] love [agape] to cast out all fear hath torment. 
For 
the first mention of love [agape], Jesus said this:
Matthew 24:12
And because iniquity shall abound, the love [agape] of many shall wax cold.
Hmmm...
Seems to say love [agape] for many can & shall go backward from LukeWarm to cold of 3: Cold ←> LukeWarm → Hot.
For perfect [teleioo] love [agape], Jesus noted perfected [teleioo] mode for him was the 3rd of 3 things in Luke 13:32:
And he said unto them, Go ye, and tell that fox, Behold, 
I cast out devils, and 
I do cures to day and to morrow, and 
the third day I shall be perfected [teleioo].
Hmmm...
Seems to suggest he was not yet perfected [teleioo] love when still casting out devils and doing cures (before the cross); Perhaps as if to allegory say perfected mode (aka teleioo love) would no longer perceive devils to cast out nor ills to cure. Wow to that.
Paul did note in Romans 8:38,39 that he got fully persuaded that the love [agape] of God in Christ Jesus is inseparable. Yet...
For 'agape', KJV Translators used both "love" 86x & "charity" 28x. Unlike modern bibles, which say "love" [agape] never fails, KJV for 1Cor 13:8 says "Charity" [agape] never faileth;
Hmmm...
Perhaps KJV portrays charity [agape 2] is more than love [agape 1]? Seems in 1Cor 12:31 it's called a "more excellent way" to be shown, which is then called "charity" (not still love) described in 1Cor 13.
Seems (?) to portray a love [agape] & charity [agape] difference:
Perhaps love [agape] would still think evil, to punish or forgive.
But charity [agape] "thinketh no evil" in 1Cor 13:5, which is like neither to punish nor to forgive (as if by think no law flaw).
So...
Colossians 3:12-15 KJV portrays "charity" can be new man put on even above forgiving one another, as if then the 8th above 7, allegory like in Ephesians 4:4-6 Father is the 8th above 7.
What are your insights about 'agape' ?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
