## Content

Is GOOD [kalos] vs GOOD [agathos] 
biblical law vs grace contrast? 
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
In 
Mt 19, Mk 10, Lk 18 when Jesus was called "Good [agathos] Master", he replied why call me good [agathos]? then clarified there's none good [agathos] but one: God. 
In John 10:11,14 Jesus said I am the good [kalos] shepherd.
In Romans 7:21 KJV Paul said I find then a law, that when I would do good [kalos], evil [kakos] is present with me. 
In Romans 8:28 all things work together for good [agathos] to them who love God.
Seems when look at "good" in NT Greek mode, 
the good [kakos] vs good [agathos] contrast suggests an allegoric law vs grace contrast.
 In Hebrews 5:14 being of full age, beyond milky way of babes, has senses exercised to discern "both good [kalos] and evil [kakos]".  This may allegory suggest avoid both good & evil aspects of law, for as Rom 7:21 noted, when do good [kalos] evil [kakos] is present.
Seems law is both good [kalos] and evil [kakos], lastly nasty.
But grace is only good [agathos]; Not kalos nor kakos.
What are your insights on good [kalos] vs good [agathos]?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
