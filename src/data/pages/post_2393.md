## Content

Scriptural ALLEGORIES: seem to say the same thing in many different allegoric ways. Here's a few examples:
- Mt 5:17b: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfill.
-- Allegory: Grace is not come to law, but to grace & truth
- Mt 9:13 learn what I will have mercy, and not sacrifice means.
-- 
Allegory: learn what I will have grace, and not law means. For grace will have mercy, multiplies peace; But law will have sacrifice, multiplies sorrow.
- Mt 22:32; Mk 12:27; Lk 20:38 He is not God of the dead, but God of the living.
-- Allegory: He is not Law (sacrifice, death), but Grace (mercy, peace)
- John 3:17 God sent not his Son into the world to condemn the world, but that the world through him might be saved.
-- Allegory: God sent not Grace & Truth (came by JC) into the world to law the world, but that the world "through"  Jesus→Christ=the end of the law might be graced & truthed unto His eternal life having no condemnation nor death.
- John 5:14b (to the impotent man made whole, then later found back in the law temple): Behold, thou art made whole, sin no more, lest a worse thing come upon thee.
-- Allegory: Behold, thou art made whole by grace, so law no more, lest the law, being both good + evil, be lastly evil=reverse of live. When graced, get out & stay out of law.
- 1Cor 14:33 God is not the author of confusion, but of peace.
-- Allegory: God is not author of any surely die lie (law), but of grace that giveth life only.
Which (contrasting) things are an allegory; About Law is not Grace, no matter how it get's Bible storied & allegorized.
