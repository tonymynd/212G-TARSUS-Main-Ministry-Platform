##  The "mystery of godliness"
 The "mystery of godliness" is part of the broader allegoric mystery presented in the Bible. To resolve this mystery, we must follow a trail of connected dots:

## The Bible as Allegory and Mystery:
- The sources consistently state that the Bible is an allegory and a mystery. This means it contains hidden meanings and requires seeking and finding to be understood.
## Connecting Biblical Dots:
The method to solve this allegoric mystery is by connecting biblical dots. This involves associating different scriptures to reveal a larger truth. For example, connecting Matthew 6:13 ("deliver us from EVIL") with Romans 7:6 ("now we are delivered from the LAW") reveals that evil can be equated with the law. Similarly, connecting Romans 14:23 ("whatsoever is NOT OF FAITH is sin") with Galatians 3:12 ("And the law is NOT OF FAITH") leads to the conclusion that law equals sin.
    
## The Central Theme of Law vs. Grace:
A crucial connection to make is the distinction between law and grace. The sources argue that the Bible presents a transition from law to grace. Being "fallen" is described as moving from grace to law, while being "changed" is moving from law to grace.
    
## Understanding as the Goal:
The ultimate goal of solving the allegoric mystery is to gain **understanding**. This understanding is linked to grace; Solomon is quoted as saying that with all thy getting, get understanding, and that understanding is the crown of grace glory. Clarity of light equals understanding.
    
## Reconciliation to God through Christ:
The process of moving from law to grace and gaining understanding leads to reconciliation with God. This reconciliation is specifically stated to be **through Christ** (Jesus → Christ), who is presented as the end of the law and the savior of the world. Reconciliation is not to Jesus but to God, and this is done in Christ.
    
## Conclusion
While the sources do not provide a direct, comprehensive definition of the "mystery of godliness" in 1 Timothy 3:16, they offer a framework for understanding such biblical mysteries. By connecting the dots, we can infer that resolving the mystery of godliness, like other mysteries in the Bible, involves understanding the **transition from law to grace**, achieved through **reconciliation to God through Christ**, and attained by **seeking and finding understanding** within the allegorical structure of the scriptures. This understanding is deeply connected to the concept of grace.
