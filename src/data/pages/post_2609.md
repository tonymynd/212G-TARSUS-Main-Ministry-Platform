## Content

Who/What said 
"I am Alpha and Omega" ?
Analyzing Revelation 1:10-16 
Grace, Mercy, Peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Analyzing Revelation 1:10-16 questions Who/What said "I am Alpha and Omega", based on clues therein. Let's investigate:
Rev 1:10 notes it was a "great [megas: loud] voice, as of a trumpet". Hebrews 12:18-20 noted sound of a trumpet was at Mt Sinai, where the voice of words was law, which the people could not endure.
Rev 1:10 & 12 notes John heard it from "behind", which caused him to be "turned" to what was behind. 2Peter 2:22 notes it happened to them, symbolically as a dog "turned" back to his own vomit.
Rev 1:12-16 portrays a character like unto Son of man (noted in Daniel 7:13) dressed as an Old Testament law priest. But the gospel of "Christ", through Jesus → Christ, in Romans forward, notably has no more mention of OT Son of man, only Son of God for Jesus.
Rev 1:16 notes out of his mouth went a sharp "twoedged sword". Such is not the word [logos] of God; For Hebrews 4:12 clarified the word [logos] of God is "sharper than any twoedged sword". OT Psalms 149:6,7 noted a "twoedged sword" executes vengeance upon the heathen and punishments upon the people.
Perhaps, based on such clues, it may allegorically be law trying to be both first and last, by saying I am Alpha and Omega. Hebrews 10:9 noted He taketh away the first, that he may establish the second (allegory as if it takes delete law to establish pure grace).
Alpha and Omega are both in the Greek Alphabet, but Alpha is not Omega; Allegory as if law (first) is not grace (last).
What are your insights about this character in Rev 1:10-16?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
