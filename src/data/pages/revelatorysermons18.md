### [God](GodShew2.md#Allness) hath NOT appointed us to wrath: 1Thessalonians 5:9

[Allegory](Allegory.md): Grace (understanding) hasn't appointed us to law (ignorance)  
  

**Of Law vs Grace, the "[Law](Law.md) worketh [Wrath](lawphoto.md)": Romans 4:15**  
**There's no wrath in [merciful](ShewBread5.md#willx2) God: "the God of all grace".  
There is no dis-comfort at all in "the God of all comfort".  
**  
**Law (wrath) vs Grace (salvation) [Allegory](Allegory.md):**  
**God hath NOT appointed us to wrath (law),**  
**but to obtain salvation (grace): 1Thess 5:9**  

**Wrath of God? Law worketh wrath. God worketh Grace -> Mercy -> Peace**  

**Wrath of God? [Wrath](lawphoto.md)(punishment, destruction, death) from [merciful](ShewBread5.md#willx2) God: perfect love? It just [doesn't compute](ShewBread9.md#Question). Rather such "wrath of God" is an oxymoron, for wrath and mercy are [contrary things](ContraryThings.md) which only mix to make an oxymoron. So wrath "of God" is not of "[that God](ShewBread5.md#IAMTHAT)" (of [this/that Gods](ShewBread7.md#Worlds)); Not of [the only wise Go](GodShew3.md#Only)[d](GodShew3.md#Only) (of foolish/wise [Gods](ShewBread6.md#Gods)); Not of [the only true God](TwainShew6.md#Spirits) (of false/true [Gods](TwainShew3.md#Gods)); Not of "the living God" (of dead/living [Gods](HGvsHS.md)); Not of [the God of all grace](GodShew2.md#Allness) (is [no law](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md) at all); Not of that God is light(only), no darkness at all. [To wit](Understanding.md), [that God](GraceUntoYou.md) was in [Christ](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md) [reconciling the world](Reconciliation.md).  
  
**

**God is constructive, not destructive. Law is destructive, not constructive.**  

**People often [blame](RevelatorySermons15.md) God our Father for such destructive things as hurricanes, tornadoes, earthquakes, and floods which often leave innocent children harmed or dead. They call such destructive things an 'act of God'. Vain words. For JC said God said: "I am not come to destroy(law), but to fulfil(grace)". Such things are an [allegory](Allegory.md) (Galatians 4:24), written aforetime for our [learning](OneLiners.md#BibleTranslators) (Romans 15:4).  
  
**

**Wrath of God? God hath not appointed us to wrath!**  

**When a [biblical study](Bibles.md) of "wrath" is done, it's associated with "[them](ShewBread2.md#inthem)"(Adam: Gen 5), also called "children of disobedience", who speak "vain words", which brings wrath upon all, both the just(us) and the unjust(them), and in diverse places. It is 'also' written(told): "God hath not appointed us to wrath(law), but to obtain salvation(grace)". So we may conclude, especially from a comparison of 1Thess 5:3 & 5:9, wrath is appointed to "them" who say Peace & safety in Law, not to "[us](ShewBread2.md#justus)", of them/us. Hence it reasons the Lord is longsuffering to "[us-ward](Usward.md)", so that [none perish](NoLawNoDeadEnd.md).  
  
**

**Law worketh Wrath. True God worketh Grace Mercy Peace.**  

**[Law](Law.md) worketh [wrath](lawphoto.md) (Romans 4:15). Hence allegoric biblical mention of the "wrath of God" may safely be allegorized as the 'wrath of Law'; For [you](YeYou.md) won't ever get any wrath at all out of a "merciful" God such as "the God of all grace" [is](OneLiners.md#Shakespeare).  
  
**

**Wrath of God is only plausible when Law worketh Wrath is given place.**  

**Adding "law worketh wrath" to "my grace is sufficient for thee"(no law at all req'd) only makes the oxymoronic mixture of contrary things: His grace("is") + law("added"), which is as blessed + cursed = accursed(Gal 1:9), and as life + death = a dead end (for all). There is no respect of persons with God. A little leaven leaveneth the whole. So until all get it: [understanding](Topics.md), the [vain words](Law.md) of [some](RevelatorySermons10.md) brings wrath on all: both the just and the unjust; So it becomes evident it's a [global matter](Understanding.md): [life or death](OneLiners.md#Ghandi).  
  
**

**Wrath of God vs God hath not appointed us to wrath**  

**God hath not appointed [us](Usward.md) to wrath, but to [obtain](Hebrews5.md) [salvation](Usward.md#Salvation) by [Jesus Christ](GodShew5.md). In "which things are an allegory", and a keyword in such a verse is "obtain"; For when coming boldly (on behalf of all, not some only) to a throne of grace ([Heb 4](Hebrews5.md)), we "obtain" mercy (for all). For if "there is no respect of persons with God", mercy for some only is not even plausible. Yet if "a little leaven leaveneth the whole lump", vain words of some can and will bring wrath upon all, till "we all are one" [get it](Topics.md): [understanding](OneLiners.md#Plato).
