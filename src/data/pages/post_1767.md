## Content

Is there a Jesus "upbraided" & God "upbraideth not" difference?
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
Here's the reason for the seemingly odd mystery question. 
Matthew 11:20 notes Jesus did pre-cross "upbraid" cities.
Mark 16:14 notes Jesus still post-cross "upbraided" eleven.
James 1:5 reveals there's a God who "upbraideth not".
Matthew 11:20 (before the cross):
Then began he to upbraid the cities wherein most of his mighty works were done, because they repented not:
Mark 16:14 (after the cross):
Afterward he appeared unto the eleven as they sat at meat, and upbraided them with their unbelief and hardness of heart, because they believed not them which had seen him after he was risen.
James 1:5 (a God who does not ever upbraid):
If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him.
Kindly share your opinion or insight on this difference.
The grace (only) of our Lord Jesus→Christ [be/is] with you all. Amen.
