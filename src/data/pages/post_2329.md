## Content

God who cannot lie promised only ETERNAL LIFE; 
NOT OT "everlasting life" nor even NT "life everlasting"

Eternal Life? I learned it's not like "life eternal" nor "everlasting life" nor "life everlasting", but a higher calling than all these. 
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ. 
I got impatient, ran off ahead of Spirit of truth is "Comforter" & "guide" = Follow Him; Thereby had a minefield stumble, felt both internal & external UnComfort for months, until I Humbly let Spirit of truth be both my 
Comforter & guide again. Then...
Spirit not only graciously comforted, but further helped me Bible see what I'd not yet noticed: of 4 "life" phrases, only "eternal life" occurs past 1Timothy, as only 1 of 4 which God, that "cannot lie", promised before the world began: Titus 1:2...
Athough Jesus had mentioned all 4 of these "life" phrases; As NT biblical story forward→progresses, Spirit helped me see 3 of such NEVER still occur beyond a certain point. Eg:
-  "life eternal" (Son of man's reverse of "eternal life") NEVER occurs past John 17:2,3 after contrasted with "eternal life".
- "everlasting life" (OT term, also used in NT) NEVER occurs past Romans 6:22,23 after contrasted with "eternal life" is clarified as "gift" of God "through Jesus→Christ" our Lord.  
- "life everlasting" (NT reverse of OT "everlasting life") NEVER occurs past 1Timothy 1:16; Because 1Tim 6:12b notes to "lay hold on eternal life" is like a higher calling "also called" unto. 
- "eternal life" is the only 1 of 4 "life" phrases that still occurs beyond 1Tim 1:16. Seems it's because Titus 1:2 clarifies the God, that "cannot lie", promised (only) "eternal life" of such 4.
Seems it thereby makes such previous mentions of "life eternal" & "everlasting life" & "life everlasting" as if still something other than & not yet God's "eternal life" purity.
Seems why last "eternal life" mention, in Jude 1:21b, notes "looking" for the "mercy" (not the sacrifice) of "our Lord Jesus Christ" unto "eternal life" (not life eternal nor everlasting life nor life everlasting); And prior to enter Revelation, has no mention of "mercy" nor mention of "eternal life". Why?
Seems Hebrews 10:36 had noted a need of "patience", and  (only) "after" will of God "done", then "receive the promise". Will of God: "I will have mercy, and not sacrifice", seems allegory for God will have grace, and not law; As why God had no pleasure in any sacrifice "for sin" is "by the law". 
As such, we may have to re-think a lot of our assumptions.
The "grace" of our Lord "Jesus Christ" [be/is] with you all. Amen.
