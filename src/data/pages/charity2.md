Amen... which is how Paul ends his epistles, [including Revelation](GMP/Notes/GodShew/Revelation.md#Author), with [his unique Pauline salutation](Paul.md), and notably such (all grace and no law at all) is by his own hand.   
  
Charity vaunteth not itself. Yet how often do we hear Evangelical and Pentecostal brethren being evangelastic and oxymoronic about the truth, oft bragging and boasting how they have rightly divided the word of truth, when the higher exhortation is "unity" and [sevenfold oneness](Sevens5.md) ending with "one God" as the seventh of seven beginning with one body, not with "division" into center/right sides and being right only to make others wrong, or center (behind).  
  
Many Christian brethren also brag about their spiritual warfare, when the higher exhortation is "peace", not war. War, what is it good for? Absolutely nothing. Christ is our peace, not war. Peace is the absence of all such childish wars via the absence of all such childish law law. For childish warfare does not end strife, but rather feeds it, by another law of such law < law giving place to sin and death.  
  

Fighting the good fight of faith is as fighting the good fight of grace,  
since "the law is not of faith" and "whatsoever is not of faith is sin".  

  

Such a good (God) fight, of faith (grace), is not as beating the air.  
The good fight of faith lays hold on [eternal life](ShewBread2.md#inthem) and doesn't let go.  
  
Laying hold on "eternal" life is not I fought the law and the law won.  
Laying hold on eternal life is flush law as dung to have eternal grace.  
  
Lay hold on eternal life is not holding onto "it is finished" in John 19.  
For law imputed sin, when "it is finished", brings forth death, not life.
