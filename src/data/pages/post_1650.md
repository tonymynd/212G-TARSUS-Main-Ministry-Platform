## Content

There is no respect of persons with God: Romans 2:11. What does it mean? Does it have two Law & Grace applications?
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
Seems in Rom 2 Paul notes Jew first, Gentile also, who both receive bad things for doing evil & good things for doing good. Then says: For there is no respect of persons with God.
Seems it's assumed God punishes doing evil & rewards doing good; Yet Paul's intent was not such, but rather that there's no Jew & Gentile difference.  
Seems he later notes how such applies to both Law & Grace:
- By the Law, as written, it's none righteous, no, not one; So by the law it's sinners all, both Jew & Gentile are guilty by the law. Hence by the law God concluded them all in unbelief.
- By Grace, righteousness {freely} came upon all men by Jesus Christ. So then it's all righteous, yes, every one; Even those not yet renewed in mind from Law to Grace, nor yet aware of it. Hence by grace God will have mercy upon all.
Yet, seems there's more to the mystery. For if we still think of such as law vs grace, and of being right on the grace side to make others wrong on the law side, seems such would still be double minded, divisional, and  still partiality on right side.
Seems why 1Cor 13:10 notes when that which is perfect is come, then that which is in part shall be done away. Seems part done away would include both their part vs your part. 
So perfect is not being right to make others wrong, but higher Oneness that's above wrong/right duality, where there is no f-law thought of any, and no thought of evil (reverse of live).
For in 1Peter 5:10 the "God of all grace" who makes you perfect after, isn't law/grace duality, but all grace & no law. 
The "grace" of our Lord "Jesus Christ" [be/is] with you all. Amen.
