Some Christmas Things To Investigate & Ponder About... 
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Not to humbug, judge, or condemn any about Christmas things, but to ask and be more aware about some Christmas things, that upon investigation, may not be true.
Was 
Jesus really born on December 25, or perhaps 9 months later (about September 22) based on biblical clues mentioned about Zacharias priestly course, birth of John the Baptist, Herod's death (also recorded by Roman historian Josephus)? 
Does the date matter?
Were there only three wise men [magos] from the east, or as Matthew 2:3 noted enough of such magos to "trouble" king Herod (who lived in another royal city) and "all Jerusalem" with him?
Why are 3 gifts, not 3 magos, noted?
Did the wise men visit a "babe" in a manger at Bethlehem (as some pageants still portray) or rather visit the "young child" (toddler) at his "house" in Nazareth at a later date than the birth?
What is the significance of their 3 gifts?
Do shepherds abide flocks by night in fields in winter time in Israel, or would flocks be in valley sheepfold during that cold season? 
Would this answer reveal about the birth time of Jesus?
Does Jeremaih 10 imply the Christmas "tree" cutting & decorating tradition was a brutish & vain Babylonian custom of fools? 
Is this tradition harmful or just a custiom?
Was saint Nicholas real, or perhaps a made up story?
Is Christmas the nicest time of year for all, or perhaps, based on police and newspaper archive evidence, the worst time of year for many re: shopping  thefts, suicides, domestic disputes, alcoholism, depression, accidents, deaths, etc?
Is Santa Claus a lie told to children, a conspiracy theory to debunk?
Is it ok to lie to children?
Again, not to humbug, judge, or condemn any about Christmas, but to suggest we investigate and ponder about Christmas things.
Three Best Wishes: Grace, Mercy, Peace to all.
What are your insights about such things?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
