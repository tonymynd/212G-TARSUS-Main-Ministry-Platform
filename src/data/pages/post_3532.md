Re: Grace "goodness" = agathosyne (not chrestotes)

GOODNESS
6th of 9 fruit aspects, asif beyond the midst and the 12th in our study before "charity" the greatest.
(attribution, study guide and spiritual aspects map in the comments)
Is interesting to see the constant pattern of moving away from contrary things, as listening to the the higher exhortation of leaving the plural principles Heb 6:1. To stop playing the middle man who longsuffers being in the midst of opposites like goodness & severity as we learned in the previous study of gentleness.
To ALL: Grace, Mercy and Peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
Love→Joy→Peace→Longsuffering→Gentleness→Goodness = 6th of 9 Fruit aspects.
NOW, of then/now, here’s a shocking, but in-teresting thing we can learn about THAT→goodness, which is now notably DIFFERENT from all THIS previous←goodness.
How much DIFFERENT?
It’s as DIFFERENT as law goodness was ←&→ grace goodness is.
So, here’s the details.
there’s 51 mentions of “goodness” in 48 Bible verses: 42 in OT, 9 in NT.
there’s a notable chanGe of goodness x9 in NT, from Law←to→Grace.
NT goodness=chrestotes x5 ←&→ goodness=agathosyne x4 in Greek mode.
It’s similar to how 5th fruit was chrestotes ←but→ 6th fruit is now agathosyne.
So, gentleness=goodness=midst of 9 was still both goodness + severity = law.
It’s as DIFFERENT as Psalms 23’s surely goodness and mercy of LORD (LAW) shall follow me ←&→ I’m now following the Spirit to BEYOND the midst of 9. (notice the changed from being followed to being led Gal 5:18)
It’s why, in the previous chapter, there was something to notice about 5th = midst.
Law has goodness, but Law is “both good and evil”; So Law goodness can also become “evil” = reverse of “live”. Law goodness also has blame that ends in “shame”, ultimate shame: “these all died” and received neither the promises nor the promise: Noted about two lists of law (faith) heroes in Hebrews 11. So then, it’s not a hall of fame, as supposed, but more like a hall of shame, to put away. It’s why the conclusion of Hebrews 11 says God provided some better thing for us, than them, such as not all lying and dying and receiving neither the promises nor the promise. Even when Law goodness is spiritual, it also has spiritual “wickedness” in high places. So the goodness of Law can also be shame and wickedness, since the gentleness of law can also be something other than gentle. 
So the 6th = Goodness only; Notably DIFFERENT from Gentleness = 5th (midst), which was also English rendered as goodness, but then still both goodness and severity = ouch. 
What can we learn from this? First, let’s do a quick review of things we’ve learned so far.
We’re doing a Big “Walk in the Spirit” of “Walk”, “walk” in Galatians 5. It’s like Big Heaven of “heaven”, “Heaven” in Genesis 1, heaven created, Heaven made. So like being made, beyond created, of PluralGod created & made things. Selah.
We’re using KJV, uses both Ghost & Spirit for ‘pneuma’, b/c “God is a Spirit”.
We’re not Ghost driven, but “led of the Spirit”, to then be “not under the law”.
We use KJV, uses both love & charity for ‘charis’, b/c “charity never faileth”.
We’re not Walking to Love, but from Love→unto→Temperance = SelfControl. 
We’re using Greek mode, also, to see things we may not see in English mode.
We’re progressive learning things about each aspect of the “Fruit” of the Spirit.
It’s kinda like many nifty aspects of the “Truth”, when known, makes you free.
When we got to Gentlesness = 5th = midst of 9, we should have gotten OMG shocked; For this Gentleness can also be something other gentle, because this conditional goodness can also be something other than goodness, both goodness and severity = ouch = suffer.
Now let’s notice Paul didn’t say goodness or severity, about the chrestotes goodness; but goodness “and” severity of this OT & NT law God to behold.
So then, as we move along, from 5th fruit to 6th fruit, both English rendered as goodness, there’s a notable difference of no more law severity, only goodness of grace, about 6th.
The midst of 9 was not as if law/grace, but still as if law is both good & severe, the goodness of law being conditional on you being good, else severity quite severe: 
cut off

Romans 2:4 Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness (chrestotes) and forbearance and longsuffering; not knowing that the goodness (chrestotes) of God leadeth thee to repentance?
Romans 11:22 Behold therefore the goodness (chrestotes) and severity of God: on them which fell, severity; but toward thee, goodness (chrestotes), if thou continue in [his] goodness (chrestotes): otherwise thou also shalt be cut off.
Romans 15:14 And I myself also am persuaded of you, my brethren, that ye also are full of goodness (agathosyne), filled with all knowledge (gnosis), able also to admonish (warn) one another.

goodness, faith, meekness, temperance = 4 (4 allegory symbolizes fulness of God).
