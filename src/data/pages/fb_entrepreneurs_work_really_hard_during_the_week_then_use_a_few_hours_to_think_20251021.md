Eric Schmidt shares a simple but powerful habit he learned from his coach, Bill Campbell. Work as hard as you can during the week—12 to 14 hour days if needed—but on weekends, carve out a few hours to think. No phone, no notifications, just focused reflection.

He recommends reviewing your past week: what went well, what you forgot, and what needs follow-up. Then, plan your next week with intention. Studies show people who do weekly reviews are 30% more productive and make 40% fewer execution errors.

It may sound obvious, but writing things down forces clarity. Schmidt credits this habit for keeping him on track as a CEO and solving problems before they grow. Leadership starts with self-awareness—and that takes time alone with your thoughts.
