## Content

Jesus is the Christ -or- Jesus becomes the Christ?? I now perceive it more accurate to say Jesus becomes the Christ of thru J→C, after prompted by the Spirit to re-read & notice many Jesus & Christ differences in the narrative I'd not previously noticed. 
Here's just a few examples:
- Jesus was "made under the law" (Gal 4:4) to make "change of the law" (Heb 7;12), from OT Law to NT BetterLaw (Mt 5:43,44... Jn 13:34) as "mediator of the 
new covenant" in a new & better testament speaks better things; BUT Christ is "the end of the law", which takes us beyond NT BetterLaw to GraceToU & peace.
-  when still teaching law & change of law, Jesus twice said he did not come to give nor send peace on earth: Mt 10:34 & Lk 12:51. So Lk 2:11-14's heralding of "on earth peace" is about "Christ" (not Jesus) & Romans says how we have peace with God is THROUGH Jesus→Christ = end of law (not just mend of law).
-  while in Jesus mode and called "Good Master" he replied why call me good: as if to say when still in law mode, then it's both good and evil, not [yet] good only like Grace God is good only.
- as 2SonsJesus (not yet 1SonChrist) he's called "Master, master" in Lk 8:24, which is TWO many masters in Mt 6:24; So Mt 23:8,10 says "one" is your "Master": "Christ" (of thru Jesus→Christ).
- so when Peter said Jesus (as Son of man) = the Christ, then he got called Satan in Mt 16:13-23, and foretold he'd later get 'epistrepho' (more than 'strepho') "converted" in Lk 22:32.
