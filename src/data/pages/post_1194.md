## Content

SHOCKER: God did NOT say let us create man; But, of create/make (bara/asah) in Genesis 1:1-25, said let us "make"(asah) man in Genesis 1:26a. WOW. Didn't see this until Spirit got me to re-read Gen 1 to notice & ponder it.
Then in Gen 1:26b God gave two before/after scenarios to play out, allegory as if to thereby compare create/make (via image/likeness):
- Before: "in our image" ... (eg: created in Eph 2:10)
- After: "after our likeness" ... (eg: made in 1Pet 5:10)
Then 
in Gen 1:27 God "created" man in "image" mode, and called such male & female Adam "them" (of them/us). Seems doing this was to set the stage for the before part of a before/after shew(KJV plural of show); 
Allegory as if the man in the mirror, who reverses things, and as if an endless  mirror (image vs likeness) battle that can only be won by "from such turn away", and let this reverse image in the mirror "vanish away" 
(kinda like in 1Cor 13:8... 2Tim 3:1-5 ... Heb 13:8 KJV). 
So what's the point? Why did God NOT say let us create man, but rather "let us make man", and then do this: created man in reverse image mode?
Seems created is not [yet] made, and image is not [yet] likeness; So even an express "image" (Hebrews 1:3) is not [yet] the true "likeness" of the only true God. 
Seems the point is to learn neither OT Law nor NT Better Law are yet pure Grace, like "the God of all grace" = ALL GRACE. 
So then, 
- Ephesians 2:10 KJV notes even "we" (of they/we) first got "created" (of created/made) in "Christ Jesus" (of CJ/JC), as if the "before" (of before/after, aka the image of image/likeness) to "walk" (of walk/run) in "them" (of them/us)...
- 1Cor 12:26 previously noted in this many member "body" it's still ALL "suffer" if any member therein suffers...
- 2Timothy 3:12 notes ALL who will live godly in CJ (of CJ/JC) shall "suffer" persecution (noted to Tim, when still a CJ)...
That kinda explains why apostle Paul always clarifies he's now a JC (was a CJ before, in Acts & in Gal 2:4), and when now a JC writing to CJs, then always turns CJ around to JC in epistles, including Hebrews. For in CJ, there may be no condemnation to "them" who walk after the Spirit, instead of after the flesh (as noted in Rom 8:1); But such are still "them", and as such it's still ALL "suffer". For although NT Better Law, of Rom 8:2's Law1/Law2, may make (them) free of OT Law;  Such Law2 is still Law, and not [yet] also free of NT Better Law mediated by CJ, and thereby not [yet] Grace of JC.
Hmmm, I also didn't previously notice, in greek mode, there's two kinds of liberty(free): 
- Liberty1: 'eleutheria' freedom (eg: Rom 8:2)
- Liberty2: 'apolyo' freedom (eg: Heb 13:23 re Timothy)
So then,
1Peter 5:10 KJV notes we may also get MADE (of created/made) even more perfect than before, which comes "after" we first "suffer" a while (not for ever) in CJ mode; And then, when made (of created/made), it's as if the end of "suffer", for then it's by "the God of all grace" = ALL GRACE. Interesting to notice no more mention of CJ beyond 1Peter 5.
WOW. For me, and after much suffer, it was then like meet your Maker (of Creator/Maker), to end my "suffer" mode.
Hope that FYI rant made some sense. 
For me, 
it was a pretty big revelation, also a long study of CJ/JC contrasts, to finally upgrade from CJ mode to JC mode, and then go "through our Lord Jesus Christ", from his Jesus mode (change of the law) to his Christ mode (end of the law), so "all things" could be new, by old things passed away; Yet then also "through Christ God-ward" go higher in awareness, as if from 'gnosis' to 'epignosis' knowledge(s) of our Lord JC.
