## Content

WISDOM that is "from above" is "FIRST PURE". First Pure What?
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord.
James 3:15-17's contrast of 2 wisdoms has interesting things to notice. Let's investigate what:
Wisdom1: this wisdom "descendeth not from above", but is "earthly, sensual, dEVILish": James 3:15. 
So, 
Because it's "not from above", James 3:16 says: 
WHERE envying[zelos] & strife is,
THERE is confusion[akatastasia] & every evil work.
But
Wisdom2: "that is from above" is "first pure"; 
So, 
when it's "first pure" & descendeth "from above", 
"then" it's seven things:
- peaceable,
- gentle, and
- easy to be entreated, 
- full of mercy (midst of 7) and
- (full of) good fruits,
- without partiality, and
- without hypocrisy (aka it has no law leaven)
It's interesting to search & notice, in greek, 
when wisdom is "from above" & "first pure" is mentioned; 
Then no more bible mention of envying[zelos] nor of confusion[akatastasia]. 
So Then both 'zelos' & 'akatastasia' things cease to be mentioned.
So my post question is: if the wisdom that is "from above" (not earthly, sensual, devilish) is "first pure", then "pure" what? 
Seems what James is allegory portraying is what Paul said: 
"GRACE, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and Jesus Christ our Lord". 
So then it seems first "pure" would be:
- pure Grace (void of laws, as if then void of flaw finding),
- pure mercy (void of sacrifices, as if void of blood shed),
- pure peace (void of confusion, as if then void of strife).
Seems to be pure "truth" (void of lies), from the "God that cannot lie"; Which Titus 1:2 calls "eternal life", which that God "promised" before the world began. 
Seems to be pure "life" (void of death) as being 
pure "sweet" (void of bitter).  
Seems why the end of the biblical story (allegory, mystery) has only mention of "grace" that is "with you all":
The grace (only) of our Lord Jesus→Christ [be/is] with you all. Amen.
