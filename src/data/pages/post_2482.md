PEACE PLEASE

Why do these PEACE phrases not occur until the Epistles of Paul? Is it because then no more mention of Son of man, only Son of God?
Grace to you, and PEACE from God our Father & Lord Jesus Christ: Rom 1:7; 1Cor 1:3; 2Cor 1:2; Gal 1:3; Eph 1:2; Phl 1:2; Col 1:2; 1Th 1:1; 2Th 1:2; Phm 1:3
Grace, Mercy, and PEACE: 1Tim 1:2; 2Tim 1:2; Tit 1:4
God 
of PEACE: Rom 15:33; Rom 16:20; Phl 4:9; 1Th 5:23; Heb 13:20
gospel of PEACE: Rom 10:15; Eph 6:15
PEACE of God: Phl 4:6,7; Col 3:15
PEACE with God: Rom 5:1
Discussion:
SEEMS Jesus, while still both Son of God and also Son of man, said he did not come to send nor to give peace on earth, but rather a sword and division (Mt 10:34; Lk 12:510), 
SO 
John 7:43; 9:16; 10:19 records "division" (not peace) b/c of him.
YET 
even when Jesus of Nazareth risen (Mk 16:6), not still in tomb, he still post cross "upbraided" & "damned" (Mk 16:14,16)
BUT 
after not only risen from dead to make appearances, but also received into heaven & sat on the right hand of God (Mk 16:19),
THEN 
returned to say "PEACE be unto you": Lk 24:36; Jn 20:19, 21, 26.
YET, as post noted, several PEACE phrases do not occur until the Epistles of Paul, which then have no more mention of Son of man, only Son of God (as if then not twain, but one); 
THEN called gospel of "Christ" & gospel of "peace", as then being "through our Lord Jesus→Christ", then Christ = end of the law (Rom 10:4) , so we then have PEACE with God (Rom 5:1), and God gifted eternal life (Rom 6:23), and God given victory (1Cor 15:57). 
What are your insights about such PEACE phrases do not yet occur until Epistles of Paul?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
