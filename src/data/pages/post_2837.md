## Content

What does Bible say about LAW?
Grace, Mercy, Peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
Our Father ....... deliver us from EVIL: Luke 11:4,
Now, 
we are delivered from the LAW: Romans 7:6.
Whatsoever is NOT OF FAITH is sin: Romans 14:25,
And the law is NOT OF FAITH: Galatians 3:12.
first bind the STRONG MAN to spoil his house: Matthew 12:29,
and the STRENGTH of sin is the law: 1Corinthians 15:56.
The law was given by Moses, but 
grace and truth came by Jesus Christ: John 1:17,
sent by the only true God to kNOW: John 17:3.
the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ: Gal 3:24,
Christ is the end of the law for righteousness: Romans 10:4.
law is tricky: it will both forgive and un-forgive, then torment.
(such is noted by Jesus in Matthew 18:23-35)
law entered that the offence might abound: Romans 5:20a,
But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound: 5:20b.
law was the ministration of death, written and engraven in stones,
and the ministration of condemnation, to be "done away": x2, such that by reason of grace glory that excelleth, law would then have no glory at all, as if not even a was. 2Cor 3: 5-11.
WHERE no law, THERE no transgression: Romans 4:15,
(the woman, being deceived, was in the transgression: 1Tim 2:14)
WHEN no law, THEN sin is not imputed: Romans 4:8; 5:13.
(blessed the man[aner] to whom the Lord will not impute sin)
For sin shall not have dominion over you: 
for ye are not under the law, but under grace: Romans 6:14.
The law worketh wrath: Romans 4:15,
Wrath is what to put away: Ephesians 4:31,
God has not appointed us to wrath, but salvation: 1Thess 5:9.
Christ abolished the enmity: the law of commandments: Eph 2:15.
note:
seems Genesis 2:17 allegory fits the "law" of "commandments",
So if Gen 2:17 abolished, then no need to add more law: such as 10 in stone, 613 in ink, which were added because of transgressions until the seed (aka Christ is end of the law) should come: Gal 3:19.
ye are become dead to the law by the body of Christ: Rom 7:4.
(note: dead to the law is then alive unto grace of God: Rom 6)
law demands: walk before me (as driven), and be perfect: Gen 17:1,
but law can NEVER make comers thereunto perfect: Hebrews 10:1.
law is holy, and the commandment holy, just, good: Rom 7:12,
but
law is both holy and unholy: bad ending,
law is both just and unjust (judge): bad ending,
law is both good[kalos] and evil[kakos]: bad ending.
law is both natural and spiritual afterward:
law is natural,
but then also called natural disaster.
law is (also) spiritual: Romans 7:14,
but then called spiritual wickedness in high places" Eph 6:12.
Seems law both saves and destroys afterward: Jude 1:5,
So, 
Jesus said endure unto the end to be saved (only): Mt 24:13,
which Paul had noted is saved (only) by grace (only): Eph 2:6,8,
and the end written to endure unto: Revelation 22:21 KJV is notably (only) Paul's pure grace salutation, and written by his "own hand":
(noted in Rom 16:24... Phil 4:23... 2Thess 3:17,18... Rev 22:21 KJV)
The grace (only) of our Lord Jesus → Christ be/is with you all. Amen.
