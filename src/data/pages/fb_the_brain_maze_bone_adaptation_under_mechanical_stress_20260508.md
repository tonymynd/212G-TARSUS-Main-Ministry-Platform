## Description

How exercise increases bone strength - How does your body secretly rebuild and weaken itself over time depending on how you move or don’t?

## Transcript

Bone adapts to exercise through
a process called remodeling.
When mechanical stress from
movement and load is applied,
bone cells respond. Osteocytes
sense strain and signal
osteoplasts to build new bone
tissue. While osteoclasts break
down older material. This
coordinated activity increases
bone density and improves
structural strength. Weight
bearing and resistance
exercises are especially
effective because they create
repeated stress along specific
regions. Over time the internal
architecture becomes more
organised. With thicker
trebecular and stronger
cortical layers. Without
regular stress this balance
shifts. Leading to gradual bone
loss and reduced strength in
inactive individuals over long
periods of time.
