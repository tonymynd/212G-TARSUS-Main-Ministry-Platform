## Content

Why does King James Version of Bible use both YE & YOU?
YE & YOU both occur in AKJV (not NKJV), often in same verse. 
So 
YE is not old English to replace with YOU, 
but 
YE & YOU are for allegoric mystery contrast. 
Examples:
Grace to YOU, and peace from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ. 
But seek YE first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto YOU. Mt 6:33
Jesus had said YE do err (Mt 22:29; Mk 12:27), 
So, 
the kingdom of God is within YOU (Lk 17:21) 
And Peace be unto YOU (Lk 24:36)
And YE shall know the truth, and the truth shall make YOU free. John 8:32
A new commandment I give unto YOU, That YE love one another; as I have loved YOU, that YE also love one another. John 13:34
YE are my friends, if YE do whatsoever I command YOU. Jn 15:14
I have yet many things to say unto YOU, but YE cannot bear them now. Jn 16:12
And God is able to make all grace abound toward YOU; that YE, always having all sufficiency in all things, may abound to every good work: 2Cor 9:8
But the God of all grace, who hath called us unto his eternal glory by Christ Jesus, after that YE have suffered a while, make YOU perfect, stablish, strengthen, settle YOU. 1Pet 5:10
Note: seems modern bibles that changed ye to you, by Y2Ks, also removed "Christ" from the end to endure unto in Revelation 22:21. 
Hmmm,
What are your insights about such?
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with YOU all. Amen.
