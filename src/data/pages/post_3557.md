## Content

Let's investigate this:
FYI: KJV (7th English bible) uses both "love" & "charity" for Greek word 'agape', allegory as if love = agape1 & charity = agape2.
Grace, Mercy, Peace from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.
KJV 
uses both "love" & "charity" in English for Greek word 'agape'. It first uses "charity" for 'agape' in 1Cor 8:1, to contrast knowledge [gnosis] "puffeth up" [physioo: make proud], but "charity" edifieth [okodomeo: builds up by edify]. 
Then after 1Cor 12:31 notes a "more excellent way" is to be shewn, it then uses "charity" (not love) for 'agape' in 1Cor 13:1-13 KJV to describe such more excellent way (as if more than love is excellent).
For "charity" it 1Cor 13:5 notes charity "thinketh no evil",
perhaps as if why it 1Cor 13:8 says charity (not love) never fails. 
In 1Cor 13:8 it says "charity" never faileth, but 
whether prophecies, they shall fail,
whether tongues, they shall cease,
whether knowledge [gnosis: that puffs up in 1Cor 8:1], it shall "vanish away" (as if a lower law knowledge is done away, to be upgraded with higher knowledge [epignosis: as noted what God will have all saved also come unto in 1Tim 2:4]
Then, for "charity never faileth", Colossians 3:12-15 KJV reveals "charity" can even be new man put on as 8th above 7 things, even above [epi] forgiving one another (because 1Cor 13:5 said charity "thnketh no evil", as if neither to punish nor even to forgive).
note:
this 8th above 7 aspect (in Col 3:12-15) was first noted for Father in Ephesians 4:4-6 KJV, where Father of all is above all 7 things called one; as Father of all being above all & through all & in you all.
Then, charity is also portrayed as being 8th above 7 things to add to your faith in 2Peter 1:5-11.
Clarity: such is not posted to bash nor suggest love is not excellent, but to reveal in KJV "charity" is described as a "more excllent way". Seems why is in Mt 24:12 Jesus said this about "love" [agape]:
And because iniquity shall abound, "the love of many shall wax cOLD" (as if draw←back to cOLD ←> LukeWarm → Hot) of 3.
What are your insights about why KJV uses both love & charity for agape; And uses both "Ghost" & "Spirit" for 'pneuma' in Greek?
