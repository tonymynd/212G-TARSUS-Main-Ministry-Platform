## Content

The "book of life" mentioned 7 times in Revelation?
Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ.
A "book of life" in Rev 3:5, 15:8; 17:8, 20:12; 20:15; 21:27; 22:19 is called "another book" & "Lamb's book". Yet in greek, "Lamb" in Revelation is 'arnion', never 'amnos' Lamb of God which taketh away the sin of the world; And a "book of the living" any can be blotted out of is from Psalms 69:28. So it's OT, and 
a jewish law concept to be flushed as if dung.
The idea of having a book people can be threatened to be blotted out of or flattered to be included in is not gospel. For God will have "mercy" upon all, and the gift of God is "eternal life"; Yet Revelation has no mention of mercy nor eternal life.
The character in Rev 3:5 (many assume is Jesus Christ) was described in Rev 1:10-13 as "one like Son of man" (not Son of God) dressed as OT high priest, with a "great voice" as of a "trumpet" & a sharp "twoedged sword" coming out his mouth. In Psalms 149:6,7 a "twoedged sword" executes vengeance & punishments (not grace & peace). Ezekiel was called Son of man. In John 3:14 Son of man calls himself a "serpent". In Hebrews 4:12 the word (logos) of God is "sharper than any twoedged sword". So...
Revelation (uncovering) reveals John got "turned" to what's "behind" him, which 2Peter 2:22 says happened, like a dog "turned" to his own vomit; Allegory as turned back to OT law. 
It makes every reference to a book of life as being OT law.
Thank God Revelation ends well, with Paul's salutation:
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you all. Amen.
